    The 𝔽₂-cover obstruction calculus and the #

    paper-faithful (140) reducer

    Central 𝔽₂-covers enter the (140) analysis through one scalar invariant: whether a continuous hom f : Γ → C₀ lifts through the cover. This file builds that calculus and the (140) reducer consuming it:

    • iotaB — the coboundary indicator ι_Γ on 2-cochains (0 iff the cochain is a continuous coboundary); additive on cocycles when #H²(Γ,𝔽₂) = 2 (iotaB_add — the only place the source's H²-size enters, threaded as hH2).

    • pullCoc — pullback of a finite-group 2-cochain along f × f, with Z2/B2 membership lemmas (pullCoc_mem_Z2, pullCoc_coboundary_mem_B2).

    • centralCover_lift_iff — for a central double cover equipped with a section of defect δ (s c · s d = z^{δ(c,d)} · s(cd) — centralCoverOfCocycle_exists_section supplies it for the phase covers): f lifts iff f^*δ ∈ B²(Γ,𝔽₂). This is the (141)-side liftability criterion and the s_Γ(ζ)-to-n_{Γ,0}(ζ) conversion.

    • phaseSign/sum_phaseSign — the signed liftability count (141): Σ_ρ (±1) = 2·n_{Γ,0}(ζ) − e_Γ(C).

    • phase140_of_phaseObstruction — the paper-faithful (140) reducer: unlike phase140_of_nonsingular (which routes through the Lin/κ_ρ/ε_ρ/N(κ,ε)-interpolation of lemma_8_5), this reducer consumes the per-ρ phase-obstruction identity

      2·|D_T| · #(central red_T image)(ρ) = |V| · (|V| + G0 · Σ_ζ phaseSign(ζ, ρ))

      — exactly the shape the paper's proof of Prop 8.9 produces from (126)+(135) before summing over ρ (pp. 42–43), with the phase family and μ-slot as in the c1s-repaired engine (μ_total = |V|·μ₀). The master-count file (GQ2/VLiftCount.lean) supplies hMobst.
