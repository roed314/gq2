    P-13d: tameness of the split-case simple factors #

    For a nontrivial simple 𝔽₂[C]-module V at a marking whose tame/wild inertia (τ, x₀, x₁) acts trivially and which generates C, the C-action factors through the cyclic ⟨σ̄⟩, so it is abelian. Two consequences, which prop_5_15 (P-13f) feeds to lemma_5_13_split:

    • sigma2_smul_trivial (hU) — the 2-primary part σ₂ = σ^{ω₂} acts trivially. Because the action is abelian, σ₂ is central, so its fixed space V^{σ₂} is a C-submodule; ⟨σ₂⟩ is a 2-group, so (p-group fixed points, char 2) V^{σ₂} ≠ 0; simplicity forces V^{σ₂} = V.
    • fixedPoints_sigma_eq_zero (hVS) — V^S = 0 when σ acts nontrivially: V^σ is a C-submodule (σ central), so ⊥ or ⊤; nontriviality kills ⊤.

    The central-fixed-point mechanism is central_pow2_smul_trivial, the analogue of lemma_5_12 with centrality in place of normality. No finite-field / image-group construction is needed.
