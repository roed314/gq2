    B13-3 — the residue field of the unit ball #

    This file proves the B13-3 component of dyadicUnitFiltration. For a finite extension k/ℚ₂ it builds the residue field O/𝔪 of the valuation ring O = {‖x‖ ≤ 1} and records its cardinality:

    • Osub k : Subring ↥k — the unit ball, and its CompactSpace;
    • maxIdeal k : Ideal ↥(Osub k) — the maximal ideal 𝔪 = {‖x‖ < 1} (intrinsic, π-free — so this file is independent of the B13-2 uniformizer lane);
    • ResidueField k := ↥(Osub k) ⧸ maxIdeal k — finite (𝔪 open in the compact O), an integral domain (norm multiplicativity), hence a field, of characteristic 2;
    • residue_card — #(O/𝔪) = 2^f and #(O/𝔪)ˣ = 2^f − 1 with f ≥ 1.

    The graded isomorphisms and the two Nat.card counts against these (B13-4), and the capstone (B13-5), append to this file. Imports GQ2.UnitFiltrationTop (B13-1) + Mathlib.

          B13-4 — the graded isomorphisms and the two Nat.card counts #

          Two group homomorphisms cutting the unit filtration into residue-field data: U⁰ = normUnits → (O/𝔪)ˣ (u ↦ ū) with kernel U¹ and surjective, and for i ≥ 1 U^{(i)} → Multiplicative (O/𝔪) (u ↦ (u−1)/πⁱ mod 𝔪) with kernel U^{(i+1)} and surjective. The isomorphism theorem then gives #(U⁰/U¹) = #(O/𝔪)ˣ = 2^f − 1 and #(U^i/U^{i+1}) = #(O/𝔪) = 2^f. Everything is parameterized by a uniformizer π : ↥k (B13-2's exists_uniformizer).
