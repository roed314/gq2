    The common tame–pro-2 boundary ∂_bd = T_A ×_{ℤ₂} Π and the boundary package (Stage C) #

    This file assembles the compatible tame and maximal pro-2 quotient maps of a profinite group Γ into a single boundary condition, realising manuscript §sec:boundary (reference/q2_manuscript.tex, l.1135–1161) and plan reference/plan_4turns.md Turn 4 §6–7.

    Main contents:

    • BoundaryPackage Γ — the uniform data of a tame quotient tameMap : Γ ↠ Ttame, a pro-2 quotient pro2Map : Γ ↠ PiGrp, and a common unramified character nu : Γ ⟶ ℤ₂ through which both factor (tameMap ≫ nu_t = nu = pro2Map ≫ nu_2).
    • Boundary : ProfiniteGrp — the fibre product T_A ×_{ℤ₂} Π = pullback nu_t nu_2 (eq:boundaryobject). ProfiniteGrp has all limits, hence pullbacks.
    • BoundaryPackage.toBoundary : Γ ⟶ Boundary — the boundary map b_Γ of eq:boundarymap, the pullback.lift of the compatible square.
    • boundaryPackage_toBoundary_surjective — profinite Goursat surjectivity of b_Γ. Manuscript: "Surjectivity follows from profinite Goursat. Any common quotient of T_A and the pro-2 group Π is a pro-2 quotient of T_A, hence a quotient of its maximal pro-2 quotient ℤ₂."

    The Goursat argument (manuscript) #

    Write S = range(b_Γ), a closed subgroup of Boundary = T_A ×_{ℤ₂} Π projecting onto both factors (tameMap, pro2Map surjective). Surjectivity reduces, by elementary group manipulation against the two surjections and the shared character, to the single Goursat core (tameMap_ker_pro2Map_ge):

    every t₀ ∈ ker(ν_t) is tameMap ε for some ε ∈ ker(pro2Map).

    i.e. ker(ν_t) ⊆ tameMap (ker pro2Map). This is the assertion that the common quotient T_A / tameMap(ker pro2Map) — being a quotient of the pro-2 group Π — is pro-2, hence (since ℤ₂ = MaximalPro2Quotient T_A via ν_t, Ttame_maximal_pro2) factors through ν_t, forcing ker ν_t ⊆ tameMap(ker pro2Map). The reverse inclusion is the compatibility tameMap ≫ ν_t = pro2Map ≫ ν_2 evaluated on ker pro2Map.

          Profinite infrastructure for the Goursat core #

          The single non-elementary ingredient of the Goursat core (tameMap_ker_pro2Map_ge) is a profinite-topology fact absent from mathlib in usable form: the common quotient T_A / tameMap(ker pro2Map) is pro-2, which via the maximality of ν_t (nu_t_ker_le) forces ker ν_t ⊆ tameMap(ker pro2Map). We supply it in two reusable pieces:

          • isPGroup_two_of_isProP2 — a finite discrete continuous quotient of a pro-2 group is a 2-group (the kernel is open normal, so it is one of the defining quotients).
          • boundaryQuot_isPGroup_two — for a boundary package, every open normal N ⊴ T_A containing M = tameMap(ker pro2Map) has T_A ⧸ N a 2-group: the quotient map T_A ↠ T_A ⧸ N, precomposed with the surjection tameMap, kills ker pro2Map, hence descends along the quotient map pro2Map to a continuous surjection Π ↠ T_A ⧸ N; apply the previous lemma to the pro-2 group Π (isProP2_PiGrp).

          The bridge from these per-N statements to t₀ ∈ M is mathlib's ProfiniteGrp.closedSubgroup_eq_sInf_open (a closed subgroup of a profinite group is the intersection of the open subgroups containing it), reduced to the normal core.

            The Goursat core #

            source
            theorem Q2Presentation.tameMap_ker_pro2Map_ge {Γ : ProfiniteGrp.{0}} (B : BoundaryPackage Γ) {t₀ : ↑Ttame.toProfinite.toTop} (ht₀ : (ProfiniteGrp.Hom.hom nu_t) t₀ = 1) :
            ∃ (ε : ↑Γ.toProfinite.toTop), (ProfiniteGrp.Hom.hom B.tameMap) ε = t₀ ∧ (ProfiniteGrp.Hom.hom B.pro2Map) ε = 1

            The Goursat core (manuscript §sec:boundary): for a boundary package on Γ, the tame image of ker(pro2Map) contains the kernel of the unramified character ν_t, i.e. ker ν_t ⊆ tameMap (ker pro2Map). This is the single load-bearing content of "Surjectivity follows from profinite Goursat"; everything else (exists_pro2_of_ker_nu_2, exists_preimage, toBoundary_surjective) is the elementary group-theoretic reduction to it, proved below.

            MATHEMATICAL PROOF. Let M := tameMap (ker pro2Map), a closed normal subgroup of T_A (continuous image of the compact ker pro2Map), with M ⊆ ker ν_t (compatibility: k ∈ ker pro2Map ⟹ ν_t (tameMap k) = ν_2 (pro2Map k) = ν_2 1 = 1). The common quotient T_A / M is pro-2: every open normal V ⊇ M gives a finite quotient T_A / V that is the image of the surjection q_V ∘ tameMap : Γ ↠ T_A / V, whose kernel contains ker pro2Map (as tameMap (ker pro2Map) = M ⊆ V); hence T_A / V is a finite continuous quotient of Γ / ker pro2Map ≅ Π, a 2-group by isProP2_PiGrp. Since ν_t : T_A ↠ ℤ₂ realises the maximal pro-2 quotient (Ttame_maximal_pro2; nu_t_ker_le is the kernel half), the pro-2 quotient T_A / M factors through ν_t, forcing ker ν_t ⊆ M. Surjectivity of tameMap on ker ν_t onto its image in M then yields the witness ε.

            FORMALISATION. The two non-elementary ingredients are supplied above: (1) mathlib's ProfiniteGrp.closedSubgroup_eq_sInf_open (a closed subgroup of a profinite group is the intersection of the open subgroups containing it) reduces t₀ ∈ M to "t₀ ∈ N for every open subgroup N ⊇ M", and passing to the normal core N₀ (Subgroup.normal_le_normalCore, Subgroup.normalCore_isClosed, Subgroup.isOpen_of_isClosed_of_finiteIndex) makes N₀ an open normal subgroup; (2) boundaryQuot_isPGroup_two shows T_A ⧸ N₀ is a 2-group, exhibiting N₀ as a Pro2Index Ttame, whereupon the existing maximality core nu_t_ker_le gives t₀ ∈ N₀ ⊆ N. M is closed (tameKerImage_isClosed) and normal (tameKerImage_normal).
