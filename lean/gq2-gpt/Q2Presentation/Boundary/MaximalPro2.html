    The maximal pro-2 quotient of a profinite group (Stage C, work order: MaximalPro2) #

    This file is a general, Q₂-independent module of profinite group theory. For an arbitrary profinite group P it constructs the maximal pro-2 quotient MaximalPro2Quotient P, the canonical surjection maxPro2Proj P, and proves the universal property characterising it. In the manuscript (reference/q2_manuscript.tex) this is the construction underlying Proposition 3.10 (prop:pro2, the maximal pro-2 quotient Π of the candidate Γ_A) and the marked local Demushkin comparison (the maximal pro-2 quotient of G_{Q₂} is ≅ ℤ₂ on the unramified line); the boundary isomorphisms consume maxPro2_universal as their load-bearing tool.

    Mathlib status #

    Mathlib v4.28.0 has no pro-p / maximal pro-p notion (a search for IsProP, MaximalPro, "pro-p" finds only unrelated CategoryTheory.ObjectProperty.Ind files). We therefore build it here, mirroring three proven mathlib/​project templates:

    • the cofiltered-limit idioms of Candidate/GammaA.lean and Boundary/Ttame.lean;
    • ProfiniteGrp.diagram/proj/cone/isLimitCone and ProfiniteGrp.limit from Mathlib.Topology.Algebra.Category.ProfiniteGrp.{Basic,Limits};
    • ProfiniteGrp.ProfiniteCompletion.lift/lift_unique from …/ProfiniteGrp/Completion.lean (the universal property of a limit of finite quotients), which the universal property here mirrors essentially verbatim.

    Construction #

    The index is the full subcategory Pro2Index P of OpenNormalSubgroup P on those open normal U whose quotient P ⧸ U is a finite 2-group (Pro2Prop). It is cofiltered (instIsCofilteredPro2Index): closed under ⊓ because P ⧸ (U ⊓ V) embeds in P ⧸ U × P ⧸ V, a product of 2-groups. The maximal pro-2 quotient is the inverse limit, in ProfiniteGrp, of the restricted finite-quotient diagram (pro2Diagram = (Pro2Prop P).ι ⋙ ProfiniteGrp.diagram P):

    • MaximalPro2Quotient P : ProfiniteGrp := ProfiniteGrp.limit (pro2Diagram P);
    • maxPro2Proj P : P ⟶ MaximalPro2Quotient P, the canonical projection (mirrors ProfiniteGrp.toLimit), surjective (maxPro2Proj_surjective);
    • maxPro2_isProP2 : IsProP2 (MaximalPro2Quotient P) — every finite continuous quotient is a 2-group;
    • maxPro2_universal — for any IsProP2 Q and f : P ⟶ Q there is a unique g : MaximalPro2Quotient P ⟶ Q with f = maxPro2Proj P ≫ g.

    source
    def Q2Presentation.IsProP2 (P : ProfiniteGrp.{u}) :
    Prop

    A profinite group P is pro-2 if every finite continuous quotient is a 2-group. Finite continuous quotients are exactly the quotients P ⧸ U by open normal subgroups U, so this says P ⧸ U is a 2-group for every U : OpenNormalSubgroup P.

                            theorem Q2Presentation.maxPro2_isProP2 (P : ProfiniteGrp.{u}) :
                            IsProP2 (MaximalPro2Quotient P)

                            The maximal pro-2 quotient is pro-2: every finite continuous quotient of MaximalPro2Quotient P is a 2-group. Given an open normal W, the domination lemma provides a coordinate projection φ = coordProj U with ker φ ≤ W; then M ⧸ ker φ ≅ range φ ≤ P ⧸ U is a 2-group, and M ⧸ W is a quotient of it.

                            The universal property #

                            For a pro-2 target Q and f : P ⟶ Q, every open normal V ⊴ Q pulls back to a pro-2 index comapIndex f hQ V = f⁻¹V of P (its quotient embeds in the 2-group Q ⧸ V). The induced finite quotient maps P ⧸ f⁻¹V ⟶ Q ⧸ V form a cone over diagram Q with apex MaximalPro2Quotient P; lifting it through Q.isLimitCone yields the universal g. This mirrors ProfiniteGrp.ProfiniteCompletion.lift/lift_unique.
