    The Nielsen transform, finite part (Stage C, work order C4) #

    This file is the finite-quotient-category form of the manuscript's Nielsen transform to the pro-2 boundary (reference/q2_manuscript.tex, Proposition 3.11 prop:abstractDemushkin, lines ~998–1025). It is pure finite group theory: independent of Stage B (GammaA) and of all profinite machinery.

    The substitution (manuscript Prop 3.11) #

    The standard rank-three dyadic Demushkin group D₀ has the single defining relation (manuscript l.208, l.489, l.575) a² s⁴ [s,y] = 1 (here [s,y] = commutatorW s y). The Nielsen change of generators (manuscript eq. nielsenrelative, l.1005) σ = s, x₁ = y, x₀ = s⁻² a⁻¹ carries the closed normal closure of that relator to that of the pro-2 boundary relator (manuscript eq. relativeDemrelation, l.1010) x₀^{σ²} x₀ [x₁,σ] ( = rconj x₀ (σ²) * x₀ * commutatorW x₁ σ, the EXACT relation form of Boundary/PiFinite.lean's admissible_twoGroup_iff_piMarked ).

    Why nielsen_rel_equiv is not a single group call #

    The manuscript proof (l.1016–1025) is explicit that the two relator words are cyclic conjugates, not equal: writing P = x₀^{σ²} x₀ and Q = [x₁,σ], the inverse of the standard relator is Q P, while the boundary relator is P Q, and QP, PQ have the same closed normal closure only because they are cyclic conjugates. Concretely (c := s⁻⁴ a⁻²) one has the pure group identity rconj x₀ (σ²) * x₀ * commutatorW x₁ σ = c · (a² s⁴ [s,y])⁻¹ · c⁻¹ , so the boundary relator is a conjugate of the inverse of the Demushkin relator. Hence R = 1 ↔ W = 1 holds (the content of nielsen_rel_equiv) but a bare group cannot close the Iff — group proves equalities, and the two "… = 1" sides are genuinely different elements. We discharge each direction by rewriting along the conjugation identity (itself closed by group), mirroring the manuscript's cyclic-conjugacy argument. The plan's / Prop 3.11's substitution is correct as written; no correction was needed.

    Category equivalence #

    D0MarkedFinite ≌ PiMarkedFinite is the induced equivalence of the two finite 2-group quotient categories. Objects correspond via the (mutually inverse) markings; morphisms are the marking-preserving homomorphisms of the skeletal carriers, and nielsen_rel_equiv is what makes the defining relation transfer. Both round trips are the identity on markings up to a group-provable rewrite, so the unit and counit are built from identity homomorphisms.

    The profinite isomorphism D₀ ≅ Π is deferred: it needs the inverse-limit machinery (not yet built). See the note at the end of the file.

    The Fin n group-instance threading mirrors Candidate/AdmissibleIndex.lean and Candidate/AdmissibleCat.lean: the bundled groupFin is made an instance and letI := …groupFin is used whenever a fresh Group (Fin n) is required, so the global Fin.instMul is never synthesized.

            The relation equivalence (manuscript Proposition 3.11) #

            source
            theorem Q2Presentation.nielsen_rel_equiv {G : Type u_1} [Group G] (a s y : G) :
            a ^ 2 * s ^ 4 * commutatorW s y = 1 ↔ rconj ((s ^ 2)⁻¹ * a⁻¹) (s ^ 2) * ((s ^ 2)⁻¹ * a⁻¹) * commutatorW y s = 1

            Proposition 3.11 (pure group form). Under the Nielsen substitution σ = s, x₀ = s⁻² a⁻¹, x₁ = y, the standard Demushkin relator a² s⁴ [s,y] vanishes iff the pro-2 boundary relator x₀^{σ²} x₀ [x₁,σ] (the EXACT relation form of PiFinite.admissible_twoGroup_iff_piMarked) vanishes.

            This is not an equality of the two relator words: as in the manuscript (l.1022–1024) they are cyclic conjugates. The proof rewrites each direction along the conjugation identity W = c · R⁻¹ · c⁻¹ with c = s⁻⁴ a⁻², which group verifies.

            source
            theorem Q2Presentation.nielsen_rel_equiv' {G : Type u_1} [Group G] (sigma x0 x1 : G) :
            rconj x0 (sigma ^ 2) * x0 * commutatorW x1 sigma = 1 ↔ (x0⁻¹ * (sigma ^ 2)⁻¹) ^ 2 * sigma ^ 4 * commutatorW sigma x1 = 1

            The inverse-direction relation equivalence (used to transfer the relation along Pi_to_D0): the pro-2 boundary relator at (σ, x₀, x₁) vanishes iff the standard Demushkin relator at (x₀⁻¹ σ⁻², σ, x₁) vanishes. Derived from nielsen_rel_equiv by rewriting σ⁻²(x₀⁻¹σ⁻²)⁻¹ = x₀.

            Generation transfer #

            The two markings generate the same subgroup because each generator of one is a word in the generators of the other.

            source
            theorem Q2Presentation.closure_nielsen_to {G : Type u_1} [Group G] (a s y : G) (h : Subgroup.closure {a, s, y} = ⊤) :
            Subgroup.closure {s, (s ^ 2)⁻¹ * a⁻¹, y} = ⊤

            If {a, s, y} generates, so does the Nielsen image {s, s⁻²a⁻¹, y}. (Worked at the proof-term level: a occurs inside the target generating set, so rw on the goal is unavailable; we rewrite the membership proof instead.)

            source
            theorem Q2Presentation.closure_nielsen_from {G : Type u_1} [Group G] (sigma x0 x1 : G) (h : Subgroup.closure {sigma, x0, x1} = ⊤) :
            Subgroup.closure {x0⁻¹ * (sigma ^ 2)⁻¹, sigma, x1} = ⊤

            If {σ, x₀, x₁} generates, so does the inverse Nielsen image {x₀⁻¹σ⁻², σ, x₁}.
