    Π is pro-2, and the supporting profinite-iso infrastructure (Stage C) #

    This file isolates the Γ_A-independent part of Boundary/CandidatePro2.lean: the fact that the profinite pro-2 group Π = PiGrp is pro-2 (isProP2_PiGrp), together with the two reusable profinite tools it rests on:

    • profiniteGrpIsoOfBijective — a bijective continuous homomorphism of profinite groups is an isomorphism;
    • isProP2_of_iso — IsProP2 transports along a continuous isomorphism.

    These were previously stated inside CandidatePro2.lean; they are factored out here so that the boundary package (Boundary/BoundaryPackage.lean, which is Γ-generic and must not depend on the candidate Γ_A) can consume isProP2_PiGrp without importing the Γ_A machinery. CandidatePro2.lean now imports this file.

    The proof of isProP2_PiGrp is unchanged: Π is the inverse limit of the finite 2-groups Fin A.n, so its explicit limit model is pro-2 (isProP2_piModel, mirroring maxPro2_isProP2), and this transports to PiGrp along the bridge piCME.

    A bijective morphism of profinite groups is an isomorphism: package it as a ContinuousMulEquiv (the inverse is continuous because the source is compact and the target Hausdorff) and apply ContinuousMulEquiv.toProfiniteGrpIso.

      The pro-2 property transports along a continuous isomorphism of profinite groups: pushing an open normal subgroup U ⊴ P forward along e gives an open normal V ⊴ Q with P ⧸ U ≃* Q ⧸ V, and Q ⧸ V is a 2-group.
