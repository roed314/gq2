    The profinite group ℤ₂ as an inverse limit (Stage C, work order C1) #

    ProfiniteZ2 is the profinite group of 2-adic integers, realised as the inverse limit, in the category ProfiniteGrp, of the finite cyclic groups ZMod (2 ^ n) along the reduction maps ZMod (2 ^ m) → ZMod (2 ^ n) (n ≤ m).

    Because ProfiniteGrp is a category of multiplicative groups we carry the layers as Multiplicative (ZMod (2 ^ n)) (C2Pow n); additively this is ℤ / 2ⁿℤ, so the 2-adic integers 0, 1, -2 appear here as z2Zero, z2One, z2NegTwo (with z2Zero = 1, the multiplicative identity).

    This is a self-contained boundary building block, independent of the Q₂ word and of Stages A/B. It supplies:

    • ProfiniteZ2 : ProfiniteGrp — the inverse limit;
    • C2Pow n and z2Projection n : ProfiniteZ2 ⟶ ProfiniteGrp.of (C2Pow n);
    • ProfiniteZ2_ext — two points agree once all projections agree;
    • the marked elements z2Zero, z2One, z2NegTwo.
