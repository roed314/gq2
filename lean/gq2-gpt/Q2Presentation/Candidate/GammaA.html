    The candidate profinite group Γ_A as an inverse limit (Stage B, work order B3) #

    This file realises the manuscript's candidate group Γ_A = F / N_A (manuscript §"The candidate as a profinite group", eq. candidateinverse) as the cofiltered inverse limit, in ProfiniteGrp, of all admissible finite marked quotients. The index category is AdmissibleIndex (work order B1/B2), which is cofiltered (admissibleIndex_isCofiltered), and the diagram sends each admissible index A to its finite carrier Fin A.n viewed as a discrete profinite group.

    Main contents:

    • admissibleDiagram : AdmissibleIndex ⥤ ProfiniteGrp — the inverse system.
    • GammaA : ProfiniteGrp — its categorical limit Γ_A.
    • gammaGen : Gen → GammaA — the four universal generators σ, τ, x₀, x₁, realised as the compatible family with coordinate A.q a at every A.
    • gammaGen_eval_projection — projecting gammaGen a to the coordinate A returns the marked generator A.q a.
    • GammaA_tameRel, GammaA_wildRel — Γ_A satisfies the two relators, in the inverse-limit (coordinatewise) sense: projecting gammaGen to any admissible quotient and evaluating the relator there gives 1 (true because that quotient is admissible). See the doc-comment on GammaA_tameRel for why the relator is stated coordinatewise rather than as a bare GExpr.eval … gammaGen = 1 (the Stage-A GExpr.eval requires a finite group; Γ_A is profinite/infinite).
    • gammaGen_topologically_generate — the four generators topologically generate Γ_A (dense generation by finite-coordinate approximation, plan §4).

    Construction idiom (ProfiniteGrp) #

    admissibleDiagram.obj A is ProfiniteGrp.ofFiniteGrp (FiniteGrp.of (Fin A.n)) (the discrete-topology profinite group on the finite carrier Fin A.n; bare finite groups carry no topology, so ProfiniteGrp.of (Fin A.n) would not elaborate — cf. Boundary/Z2Limit.lean). Morphisms are ProfiniteGrp.ofFiniteGrpHom (FiniteGrp.ofHom f.toMonoidHom), whose underlying function is definitionally f.toMonoidHom (admissibleDiagram_map_apply).

    The categorical limit Limits.limit admissibleDiagram is opaque, so we bridge it to mathlib's explicit model ProfiniteGrp.limit admissibleDiagram (an honest subtype of compatible families, with .val and ProfiniteGrp.limit_ext) via the canonical gammaIso : GammaA ≅ ProfiniteGrp.limit admissibleDiagram (Limits.limit.isoLimitCone against ProfiniteGrp.limitConeIsLimit).

    Instance gotcha (inherited from B1/B2) #

    Fin n carries the global Fin.instMul, not the transported group structure A.groupFin. We never synthesize a standalone Group (Fin n); A.groupFin is the registered instance and all maps go through the bundled AdmissibleHom.

              The two relators hold in Γ_A #

              source
              theorem Q2Presentation.GammaA_tameRel (A : AdmissibleIndex) :
              (Q2Word.tameRelator.eval fun (a : Gen) => (ProfiniteGrp.Hom.hom (CategoryTheory.Limits.limit.π admissibleDiagram A)) (gammaGen a)) = 1

              The tame relator holds in Γ_A (manuscript eq. tame), in the inverse-limit sense: projecting the universal generators to any admissible finite quotient A and evaluating the tame relator there gives 1.

              We state it coordinatewise rather than as a bare GExpr.eval Q2Word.tameRelator gammaGen = 1, because the Stage-A evaluator GExpr.eval is defined only for finite groups (its powOmega2 case is the finite ω₂-power), whereas Γ_A is profinite, hence infinite. Since two points of Γ_A are equal iff all their coordinates agree (GammaA_ext), this coordinatewise statement is precisely "the universal generators of Γ_A satisfy the tame relation". The proof rewrites each projected generator to A.q a (gammaGen_eval_projection) and invokes admissibility of A (A.admissible.2.1 : tameRel A.q).

                  The subgroup generated by the universal generators is dense in the explicit limit model. Mirrors ProfiniteGrp.denseRange_toLimit: a nonempty open set fixes finitely many coordinates; cofiltered domination collapses them to one coordinate B; and the generators surject onto Fin B.n there.

                  The four universal generators topologically generate Γ_A (plan §4): the closure of the subgroup they generate is everything. Equivalently, that subgroup is dense. Density is transported from the explicit limit model (dense_gammaGenFamily) along the homeomorphism gammaHomeo, using that gammaIso.hom carries the generators to their explicit families.
