    Admissible markings (Stage A) #

    A marking q : Marking G of a finite group is admissible when it generates G, satisfies the two displayed relators (the tame relator τ^σ τ⁻² and the wild relator h₀ u₁⁻¹ x₁^σ c₀, evaluated through the word language GExpr.eval, never by hand), and sends the two wild generators x₀, x₁ into the 2-core O₂(G).

    This is the formal kernel of the Q₂ presentation (plan §1; manuscript §2, Proposition 2.3). The two structural theorems are:

    • admissible_map_surjective — admissibility transports along a surjection;
    • (in Subdirect.lean) admissible_subdirect — admissibility is closed under finite subdirect products (manuscript Lemma 2.1).

    The relator preservation is uniform: it is GExpr.eval_map applied to the two relator words, so the wild word h₀ is never unfolded by hand.

            theorem Q2Presentation.admissible_map_surjective {G : Type u_1} {H : Type u_2} [Group G] [Finite G] [Group H] [Finite H] (φ : G →* H) (hφ : Function.Surjective ⇑φ) {q : Marking G} (hq : Admissible q) :
            Admissible fun (a : Gen) => φ (q a)

            Admissibility transports along a surjection. If q is admissible and φ : G ↠ H is a surjective homomorphism of finite groups, then the pushed marking φ ∘ q is admissible. Generation comes from surjectivity, the two relators from GExpr.eval_map, and the 2-core conditions from hom_image_twoCore_le_twoCore.
