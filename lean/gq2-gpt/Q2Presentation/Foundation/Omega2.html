    The finite ω₂-power machinery (Stage A) #

    ω₂ ∈ Ẑ = ∏_p ℤ_p is the idempotent whose ℤ₂-component is 1 and whose ℤ_ℓ-component is 0 for every prime ℓ ≠ 2 (the 2-primary projection). Its residue modulo n is the CRT idempotent: writing n = 2^a · m with m odd (a = v₂ n), it is ≡ 1 (mod 2^a) and ≡ 0 (mod m).

    To raise a finite-order group element g to the ω₂-power one reduces ω₂ modulo the order of g (or any multiple of it) and takes the ordinary power. The manuscript's finite-exponent independence (lem:finiteexponent) says any two integer representatives of ω₂ modulo the exponent of the finite target give the same element; here this is finite_omega2_pow_independent_of_rep.

    Main definitions:

    • omega2Mod n : ZMod n — the idempotent reduced mod n.
    • powOmega2 g — g to the ω₂-power, depending only on orderOf g.

    Main lemmas:

    • omega2Mod_eq_one_on_two_part, omega2Mod_eq_zero_on_odd_part
    • finite_omega2_pow_idempotent
    • finite_omega2_pow_independent_of_rep

    def Q2Presentation.omega2Mod (n : ℕ) :
    ZMod n

    The 2-primary idempotent ω₂ ∈ Ẑ reduced modulo n: the odd part raised to φ of the 2-part. It is ≡ 1 modulo the 2-part ordProj[2] n = 2 ^ (v₂ n) and ≡ 0 modulo the odd part ordCompl[2] n.

      noncomputable def Q2Presentation.powOmega2 {G : Type u_1} [Group G] [Finite G] (g : G) :
      G

      g raised to the ω₂-power in a finite group: reduce ω₂ modulo orderOf g and take the ordinary power. This depends only on g (through its order), not on any fixed verification modulus.
