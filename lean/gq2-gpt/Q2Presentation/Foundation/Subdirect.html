    Finite subdirect closure of admissible markings (Stage A) — manuscript Lemma 2.1 #

    Given a finite family of admissible markings q i : Marking (G i), the diagonal marking a ↦ (i ↦ q i a) into the product ∀ i, G i, corestricted to the subgroup it generates (subdirectSubgroup q), is again admissible. This is the formal content of manuscript Lemma 2.1 (and the engine behind the cofinality argument of Lemma 2.2).

    The proof decomposes uniformly, exactly as in the plan (§5):

    • relations — via GExpr.eval_map (corestrict along the subgroup inclusion) and GExpr.eval_pi (work coordinatewise); the wild word is never unfolded;
    • generation — by construction, the corestricted generators generate the closure;
    • wild pro-2 condition — the diagonal images of x₀, x₁ lie in the normal 2-subgroup ∏ᵢ O₂(Gᵢ), whose pullback to the subdirect subgroup is a normal 2-subgroup, hence inside O₂ of the subdirect subgroup by maximality (normal_pSubgroup_le_pCore).
