    F-ram F-G1 — the ONE citable axiom of the wave (class (c): citable-as-is plus ONE named referee step).

    Citation (primary). R. L. Griess, Jr., Automorphisms of extra special groups and nonvanishing degree 2 cohomology, Pacific J. Math. 48 (1973) 403–422, Theorem 1, SPLIT half — verbatim, both types ε = ±: for E extraspecial of order 2^{2n+1} the sequence 1 → Inn(E) → Aut(E) → O^ε(2n,2) → 1 is "nonsplit when n ≥ 3, split when n = 1, 2" (quoted in full at audit/CITABILITY_AUDIT_20260708.md §F l.462–471). D. L. Winter, The automorphism group of an extraspecial p-group, Rocky Mountain J. Math. 2 (1972) 159–168, supplies the identification Out(E) ≅ O^ε(2n,2) of the quotient. So for dim V = 2n ≤ 4 a homomorphic section σ : O(q) → Aut(E_f) exists; composing with any form-preserving act : G → O(q) lifts the WHOLE acting group — the split half covers EVERY acting subgroup at small heads (audit §F finding 3(iii) grades exactly this clause EXACT-CITABLE, with the instruction "check dimV ≤ 4 instances first").

    The ONE named step (the coordinate dictionary). lem:extraspecialconnecting (reference/q2_manuscript.tex l.2102–2131) states — AS AN EQUIVALENCE, verbatim "Equivalently, c⋅(v,z) = (cv, z + m_c(v)) defines an action by automorphisms of E_f" — that centre-fixing lifts of the action through Aut(E_f) ARE the families m_c : V → 𝔽₂ satisfying eq:mquadratic and eq:mcoherent, i.e. exactly the conclusion below. For biadditive f with diagonal q nonsingular, E_f = V × 𝔽₂ is extraspecial with E_f/Z ≅ V, squaring map q, commutator = polar, so Aut(E_f) → O(q) IS the Griess–Winter sequence. This normalized-coordinates reading is textbook-standard (lifting a group action to a central extension) and the manuscript states it as an equivalence; a referee accepts it as one step (design §2.1: match grade "EXACT after one named step"; restriction of the split section along act is composition, not a step).

    Guards (load-bearing). hd : finrank ≤ 4 is exactly n ≤ 2: Griess Thm 1's NONSPLIT half makes the ambient extension genuinely nonsplit for every n ≥ 3, and Griess Thm 0 manufactures nonlifting 2-local subgroup configurations there — Griess: "our methods do not indicate how big these nonzero cohomology groups are"; NO positive subgroup-restriction criterion exists in the literature (audit §F findings 1–2, 4). Dropping or weakening hd is refutable in principle. hns is load-bearing: E_f is extraspecial (centre exactly 𝔽₂) only for nondegenerate polar. At odd finrank the hypotheses are vacuous (no nonsingular form exists — Quadratic.nonsingular_even_finrank); at V = 0 the instance is outright provable (m := 0). Biadditivity of f is STRONGER than Griess needs (any normalized factor set with square map q works), so the axiom is WEAKER than the citation — the safe direction.

    Refutation instrument (design §6.2): per-instance falsifiable — at a concrete (V, q, f, G, act) with finrank ≤ 4 the conclusion is a finite search over m : G → V → 𝔽₂ (|G|·2^{dim V} bits); a failed exhaustive search at a nonsingular instance refutes the axiom AND the citation. Statement verbatim design §2.3.
