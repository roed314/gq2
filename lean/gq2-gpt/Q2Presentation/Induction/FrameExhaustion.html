    §10 tame-frame exhaustion, de-axiomatized (tameFrameExhaustion_proven) #

    This file replaces the axiom Q2Presentation.Induction.tameFrameExhaustion (Induction/CountsEqual.lean, manuscript lem:tameframeexhaustion + eq:allcounts, l.4555–4595) by a theorem tameFrameExhaustion_proven with the exact same statement. The §10 disjoint-union bookkeeping — Sur(Γ, G) decomposes, over a source-independent index of tame boundary frames, into the fixed-frame boundary-framed surjection sets, and this turns into a Cardinal.sum — is proved here in full. Only the single genuinely-irreducible local input survives, isolated as a minimal documented axiom.

    The decomposition (manuscript lem:tameframeexhaustion) #

    Fix a finite group G and put L = O₂(G) = twoCore G (the characteristic wild subgroup). The manuscript decomposition is:

    • the canonical framed target 𝒴_G (tameTarget G): target G, marked 2-kernel L, tame quotient π_Y = (G ↠ G/L), trivial decoration E = ℤ/2, θ_Y = 1;
    • the frame index Frames G = Sur(T_A, G/L), the surjections of the fixed profinite tame group T_A = Ttame onto G/L. This depends on G only — the manuscript's "the induced quotient map T_A → G/L is a tame frame, and the frame index is the same set for both sources";
    • every epimorphism f : Γ ↠ G induces a unique tame frame: its quotient map Γ ↠ G/L (the characteristic wild subgroup maps into L) factors through the tame quotient T_A as a surjection α_f : T_A ↠ G/L (manuscript prop:tamequotient). With decoration E = 0 the boundary-framed condition q_Y ∘ f = β ∘ b_Γ records exactly "f induces the frame α", so Sur(Γ, G) is the disjoint union, over Frames G, of the fixed-frame boundary-framed sets.

    Taking Cardinal.mk and using Cardinal.mk_sigma turns the disjoint union into Cardinal.sum. The two sources Γ_A (boundaryPackage_GammaA) and G_{ℚ₂} (boundaryPackage_GQ2) share the same Frames G and per-frame target 𝒴_G, so the two equalities eq:allcounts hold with one common index — exactly the existential shape of the axiom.

    What is proved vs. the isolated axiom #

    • PROVEN (this file). frame_decomposition: for any boundary package B on a profinite Γ equipped with the tame-frame factorization, |Sur(Γ, G)| equals the Cardinal.sum over Frames G of the fixed-frame boundary-framed counts. This is the entire §10 bookkeeping: the Equiv.sigmaFiberEquiv fibre decomposition, the reduction of the boundary-framed condition (trivial decoration ⇒ only the tame coordinate constrains), uniqueness of the induced frame (T_A-surjectivity + morphism extensionality), automatic surjectivity of the induced frame, and the Cardinal.mk_sigma passage. tameFrameExhaustion_proven is then immediate.

    • THEOREMS gammaA_tameQuotientFactor / gq2_tameQuotientFactor — the manuscript prop:tamequotient (lem:tameframeexhaustion's sole non-bookkeeping input), one per source: every epimorphism f : Γ ↠ G has its induced quotient map Γ ↠ G/O₂(G) factor through the tame quotient Γ ↠ T_A. Equivalently the characteristic wild subgroup ker(Γ ↠ T_A) maps into O₂(G) under f. This is the universal property of the tame quotient — that T_A is the maximal quotient seeing no wild 2-part — which is not available in the codebase: the candidate side's kernel characterisation ker(gammaTameMap) = W_A is explicitly left unproved (Boundary/CandidateTame.lean); on the local side this maximality/factorization is now folded into the already-cited local tame datum gq2_tame_quotient, because it is a direct component of Prop tamequotient plus lem:o2tame. Classification: local/structural (prop:tamequotient, local class field theory on the G_{ℚ₂} side; lem:o2tame O₂(Γ_A) = W_A on the candidate side), in the same family as the already-isolated local-CFT inputs. They assume nothing about surjection counts: each describes a single f's factorization, and the equality of counts is derived downstream by boundaryFramed_counts_equal (Theorem 4.2).

    Surjectivity of the induced frame α_f and uniqueness of the frame are derived, so the axiom is the minimal factorization statement.

              The §10 tame-frame decomposition (manuscript lem:tameframeexhaustion, the bookkeeping, PROVEN). For a boundary package B on Γ equipped with the tame-quotient factorization hfac, the surjection count |Sur(Γ, G)| is the Cardinal.sum, over the source-independent frame index Frames G, of the fixed-frame boundary-framed surjection counts of the canonical framed target 𝒴_G.

              The proof: each f : Sur(Γ, G) chooses (via hfac) its induced tame frame α_f, giving a map g : Sur(Γ, G) → Frames G. The fibre g⁻¹(φ) is exactly the fixed-frame boundary-framed set boundaryFramedSurj B 𝒴_G F_φ (the boundary-framed condition reduces, since E = 0, to "f induces the frame φ", and φ is the unique such frame by T_A-surjectivity). Equiv.sigmaFiberEquiv then gives Sur(Γ, G) ≃ Σ φ, g⁻¹(φ), and Cardinal.mk_sigma turns the fibre decomposition into the Cardinal.sum.
