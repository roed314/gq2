    The Frattini quotient of a finite 2-group is elementary abelian #

    The Burnside-basis core needed by the §7 concrete tower (lem:collapse uses Φ(K) = K²[K,K], manuscript l.3610): for a finite 2-group G,

    • every maximal subgroup is normal (IsPGroup.coatom_normal) with quotient of exponent 2 — so squares and commutators lie in every maximal subgroup, hence in frattini G (IsPGroup.sq_mem_frattini, IsPGroup.commutatorElement_mem_frattini);
    • hence G ⧸ frattini G is elementary abelian: commutative (IsPGroup.frattiniQuotientCommGroup) of exponent 2 (IsPGroup.frattiniQuotient_sq_eq_one), so its additive form is a ZMod 2-module (IsPGroup.frattiniQuotientModule).

    Everything here is proved — no axioms. Mathlib (this toolchain) has frattini as the subgroup-lattice radical but no Burnside basis theorem, so we derive what the tower needs directly.
