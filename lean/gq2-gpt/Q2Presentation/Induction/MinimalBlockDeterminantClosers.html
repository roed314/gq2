    Determinant-side Section 7 minimal-block closers #

    This sidecar refines the first determinant-pushout residual in the final Section 7 route without touching the existing minimal-block sidecars.

    The current final route already uses MinimalBlockGroupTowerData for the subgroup tower provenance. The remaining determinant family starts from minimalBlock_preheadCentralPushoutSelfDualityData, which only records the self-duality map phi. The manuscript's central pushout, however, carries a square map and a polar map:

    • q_lambda(m) = lambda(mtilde^2);
    • b_lambda(m,m') is the commutator pairing;
    • the self-duality phi_lambda identifies these with the diagonal and symmetrized polar pairing.

    The packet below records that square/polar interface and then projects it to the older phi-only packet. Routing the capstone through minimalBlock_of_nonScalar_proven_determinant_refined therefore replaces the old minimalBlock_preheadCentralPushoutSelfDualityData axiom in the final closure by the more concrete minimalBlock_preheadCentralPushoutSquarePolarData packet.

    The concrete square/polar data of the scalar central pushouts on the pre-head minimal-block tower.

    Manuscript anchor: Section 7, prop:simpleheaddet, where each nonzero invariant lambda : R^vee forms K_lambda = K / ker lambda, with square map q_lambda and polar form b_lambda. The fields square_diagonal and polar_symm record that the self-duality family phi_lambda : M -> M^vee is the source of those maps.

    • phi : Module.Dual (ZMod 2) D.R → D.M →ₗ[ZMod 2] Module.Dual (ZMod 2) D.M

      The self-duality family induced by the scalar central pushouts.

    • square : Module.Dual (ZMod 2) D.R → Quadratic.QuadF2 D.M

      The square map of each scalar central pushout, as a quadratic form on M.

    • square_diagonal (lam : Module.Dual (ZMod 2) D.R) (m : D.M) : (self.square lam).form m = ((self.phi lam) m) m

      The square map is the diagonal of the self-duality pairing.

    • polar_symm (lam : Module.Dual (ZMod 2) D.R) (m m' : D.M) : ((self.square lam).polar m) m' = ((self.phi lam) m) m' + ((self.phi lam) m') m

      The polar map is the symmetrized self-duality pairing.
