    Kernel-side Section 7 semantic proof packets #

    This sidecar closes/decomposes the first half of the remaining Section 7 semantic packets:

    • minimalBlock_kernelChoiceSemanticData;
    • minimalBlock_frattiniKernelSemanticData;
    • minimalBlock_moduleExtractionSemanticData;
    • minimalBlock_simpleHeadSemanticData.

    The non-minimal part of the kernel choice is a theorem: P itself is a Y-normal candidate with P S = P, and the K <= L_Y field follows from K <= P <= L_Y. The downstream interface asks for a least such K, so that leastness is exposed as a narrower finite-group choice packet rather than hidden inside the old semantic axiom.

    The remaining packets are reduced to narrower manuscript-shaped packets:

    • Frattini collapse: normality and nontriviality of the literal Phi(K) = K^2 [K,K] inside Y;
    • module extraction: the raw R,M,T,C objects, the induced actions, and a K/R ~= M bridge;
    • simple head: nontrivial head, dual-invariant vanishing, and raw invariant determinant character.

    The aggregate theorem at the end reassembles MinimalBlockGroupTowerData without using the four semantic packet axioms listed above.
