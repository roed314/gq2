    Realization-side Section 7 minimal-block closers #

    This sidecar leaves the routed files and the root import untouched. It records a group-tower provenance interface, plus two alternative Section 7 reassembly routes over the existing APIs:

    • MinimalBlockGroupTowerData is the manuscript-facing provenance packet for the subgroup tower R = Phi(K) <= K <= L_Y, the elementary layer M = K/R, the submodule T, the lower quotient C, and the induced actions. It feeds the raw module/action/head/dual-invariant/raw-character packets and also supplies the K/R ~= M bridge needed by CentralCoverTargetSubgroupConcreteData.

    • minimalBlock_of_nonScalar_proven_concrete_noHeadResidual keeps the concrete residual split of MinimalBlockConcreteClosers, but derives the head nontriviality field from determinant nonzeroness. Its closure drops the current minimalBlock_headNontrivialData residual and replaces it by the manuscript-facing determinant nonzero packet.

    • minimalBlock_of_nonScalar_proven_realization_refined also uses the existing pre-head invariant-character packet from MinimalBlockPacketClosers2, so its closure drops both minimalBlock_headNontrivialData and the raw-character residual from the concrete route.

    The point is not to reroute the main proof here. It is to give follow-up workers strictly smaller closure targets that can be compared by #print axioms.

    structure Q2Presentation.Induction.MinimalBlockGroupTowerData (p : FramedPair) (chief : NonScalarChiefFactor p) :
    Type 1

    Manuscript-facing provenance for the minimal-block subgroup tower.

    The current downstream interfaces are intentionally module-level. This structure keeps the missing finite-group provenance in one place: a minimal normal subgroup K, its Frattini subgroup R = Phi(K), the raw module/action data extracted from R, M = K/R, T, and C, the simple-head conclusions of lem:simplehead, and the K/R ~= M bridge consumed by the target-tower concrete packet.

    • Ksub : Subgroup p.fst.Y

      The realized minimal normal subgroup K <= P.

    • Ksub_normal : self.Ksub.Normal

      K is normal in Y.

    • Ksub_le_upper : self.Ksub ≤ chief.upper

      K lies in the selected chief-factor upper group.

    • Ksub_le_LY : self.Ksub ≤ p.fst.LY

      K lies in the marked wild kernel.

    • KS_eq_upper : self.Ksub ⊔ chief.lower = chief.upper

      The minimality condition is imposed among Y-normal subgroups with K S = P.

    • minimal_K (K' : Subgroup p.fst.Y) : K'.Normal → K' ≤ chief.upper → K' ⊔ chief.lower = chief.upper → self.Ksub ≤ K'

      Minimality of K subject to K S = P.

    • Rsub : Subgroup p.fst.Y

      The realized Frattini subgroup R = Phi(K).

    • Rsub_normal : self.Rsub.Normal

      R is normal in Y.

    • Rsub_le_Ksub : self.Rsub ≤ self.Ksub

      Manuscript tower condition R <= K.

    • Rsub_card_gt_one : 1 < Nat.card ↥self.Rsub

      R is nontrivial, so the middle quotient is a genuine strict step.

    • Rsub_eq_frattini : self.Rsub = Subgroup.map self.Ksub.subtype (frattini ↥self.Ksub)

      Manuscript R = Phi(K), realized inside Y.

    • rawModuleData : MinimalBlockRawModuleData p chief

      The additive F_2 raw module objects R, M, T, and C.

    • rawActionData : MinimalBlockRawActionData self.rawModuleData

      The conjugation actions of C on R and M, with T stable.

    • headNontrivialData : MinimalBlockHeadNontrivialData (MinimalBlockRawTowerData.ofModuleAction self.rawModuleData self.rawActionData)

      Nontriviality of the simple head M/T.

    • dualInvariantData : MinimalBlockDualInvariantVanishesData (MinimalBlockRawTowerData.ofModuleAction self.rawModuleData self.rawActionData)

      The dual-invariant vanishing (M^vee)^C = 0.

    • rawInvariantCharacterData : MinimalBlockRawInvariantCharacterData (MinimalBlockRawTowerData.ofModuleAction self.rawModuleData self.rawActionData)

      A raw invariant determinant character on R.

    • KmodR_equiv_rawM : ↥(Subgroup.map (QuotientGroup.mk' self.Rsub) self.Ksub) ≃* Multiplicative self.rawModuleData.M

      The quotient K/R realizes the abstract elementary module M.
