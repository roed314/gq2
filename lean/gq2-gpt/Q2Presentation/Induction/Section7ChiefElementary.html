    The chief factor is elementary abelian, and R ≤ K ∩ S #

    lem:collapse steps (i)–(ii) (manuscript l.3674: "Since K/(K∩S) ≅ V is elementary abelian, R ≤ K∩S"):

    • chief_sq_comm_mem_lower — squares and commutators of P lie in S: the Y-normal subgroup S ⊔ ⟨sq, comm⟩ is S or P by chief-ness, and = P would put every element of the nontrivial finite 2-group P/S inside its Frattini subgroup (Burnside forward) — impossible;
    • kernelFrattini_le_lower — hence K/(K∩S) is elementary abelian, so by the Burnside converse R = Φ(K) ≤ K ∩ S ≤ S.

    Everything here is proved — no axioms.
