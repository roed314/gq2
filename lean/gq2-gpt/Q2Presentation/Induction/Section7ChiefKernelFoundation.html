    Section 7 corrected foundation: first non-scalar chief factor + ⊆-minimal kernel #

    Manuscript-verification pass (2026-07-01) found the §7 kernel interface makes three statements STRONGER than the manuscript proves (PROGRESS.md "SOUNDNESS FINDINGS"):

    1. the manuscript chooses S ◁ P with all chief factors below S scalar and V = P/S the first non-scalar factor (l.3616–3623) — NonScalarChiefFactor records no such condition, and lem:collapse genuinely needs it (the [S,Ñ] = 1 coprime step);
    2. the manuscript chooses K ◁ Y, K ≤ P, minimal subject to KS = P — the interface field minimal_K asserts least (Ksub ≤ K' for every candidate K'), which is false in general (incomparable minimal complements);
    3. the interface forces 1 < |Φ(K)|, but the manuscript explicitly allows R = Φ(K) = 1 (thm:closedrecursion: "when R ≠ 1"; the inductive step l.4542: "If R = 1, then B = Y and the induction closes at this [elementary] stage").

    This file is the corrected, PROVEN foundation:

    • FirstNonScalarChiefFactor — a non-scalar chief factor all of whose strictly-below chief factors are scalar; firstNonScalarChiefFactor_exists (choose a non-scalar chief factor of least upper cardinality) is proved, from ¬ ScalarTerminal p alone.
    • KernelMinimal — the manuscript's kernel: K ◁ Y, K ≤ P, KS = P, and ⊆-minimality inside K (exactly the form used by lem:simplehead and lem:collapse); kernelMinimal_exists is proved by finite descent.
    • frattini_case_split — the R = 1 / R ≠ 1 dichotomy of thm:closedrecursion, stated on the literal Φ(K) ≤ Y.

    No axioms are introduced here; everything is proved.
