    Fixed lifts and the chiefness contradiction #

    The fixed-vector theorem produces a non-identity class in P/S fixed by Y. Here we convert such a class into the manuscript's chief-factor contradiction: the fixed classes lift to a Y-normal subgroup between S and P; since the class is nontrivial this subgroup is not S, hence chiefness forces it to be P, making the whole chief factor trivial.
