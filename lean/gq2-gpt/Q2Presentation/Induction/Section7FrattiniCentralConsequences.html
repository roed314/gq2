    Consequences of Frattini centrality in the minimal kernel #

    Once R = Phi(K) centralizes K, the Burnside-basis facts for the finite 2-group K become pointwise algebra on the ambient target:

    • squares from K lie in Phi(K) and hence centralize K;
    • commutators from K lie in Phi(K) and hence centralize K; The commutator-square and fourth-power identities need a separate explicit class-two algebra calculation; this file keeps only the cheap packet inputs that are immediately available from the proved centrality theorem.

    These are the local algebraic inputs for the remaining K^4 = 1 and Phi(K)-exponent-two part of lem:collapse.
