    Route40 target K/R bridge sidecar #

    Route39 lowered the target branch to two literal same-tower packets:

    • TargetKmodRRoute39BridgeProofs.centralCover_targetRoute39LowerQuotientIdentificationData;
    • TargetKmodRRoute39BridgeProofs.centralCover_targetRoute39LiteralRepresentativeActionData.

    This sidecar keeps the Route39 lower quotient identification, but lowers the fixed-literal representative action packet one more step. Instead of asking that Lean's theorem-level Classical.choose representative of each class in Y/K realizes the action, Route40 asks for some selected section of the literal quotient whose representatives realize the C-action on M = K/R.

    The passage from that selected section to Route39's fixed literal representatives is theorem-level: two representatives of the same Y/K class differ by an element of K, and inner conjugation by K on K/R is already closed by centralCover_targetKmodRInnerConjugationData_from_KmodR_bridge.

    Manuscript anchors:

    • §7, eq:targettower (pp. 40--42): Y -> B = Y/R -> C = Y/K and M = K/R;
    • §8, exact-image lower maps and central-cover transforms (pp. 42--49): choose lower-target representatives, while same-coset independence makes the choice immaterial.
