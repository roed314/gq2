    N8 — the Gauss-sum radical fibration G(Q) = |U| · G(Q̄) (R4b unit ZeroEdgeGaussDescent) #

    Unit N8 of audit/BLOCKR_R4B_DESIGN.md (§4-N8, §6.1; dependency: the green value layer only). This file is fully abstract quadratic-form algebra over ZMod 2 carriers — no R4b/zero-edge carrier is imported or mentioned — so it lands independently of the Induction wave (N1–N7). Contents:

    • gaussSum_of_radical_fibration — the design's headline: if a QuadF2 M annihilates a submodule U (form vanishes on U and U lies in the polar radical — the green Annihilates of Induction/MinimalBlock.lean), then the Gauss sum fibres over the U-cosets: G(Q) = |U| · G(Q̄), with Q̄ = h.descend the green descended form on M ⧸ U. No nonsingularity is needed for the raw fibration.
    • gaussSum_of_radical_fibration_pow — the §2.1 spelling G(Q_W) = 2^d · G(Q̄) (d = dim U): this is the factor that fuses the B¹(V)-part of the multiplicity μ = |B¹(V)|·|Z¹(T′)| (eq:mumultiplicity, manuscript l.4065) into the engine's |W|-term so that the pointwise identity rearranges to the keep's muT·(2^d + G(Q̄)·Σ_χ …) shape (design §2.1).
    • gaussSum_of_radical_fibration_value (+ _of_rad_le, _of_nontrivial) — the applied value form of §4-N8: with the descended form nonsingular (in the application: edgeHeadForm_nonsingular-transport, done by N7) and the descended dimension positive (in the application: from edgeHead_nontrivial), gaussSum_eq_sign_two_pow gives G(Q) = 2^{dim U} · (baseGaussSign (gaussType Q̄) · 2^{d̄/2}).

    theorem Q2Presentation.Induction.gaussSum_of_radical_fibration {M : Type u_1} [AddCommGroup M] [Module (ZMod 2) M] [Fintype M] {U : Submodule (ZMod 2) M} [Fintype (M ⧸ U)] {q : Quadratic.QuadF2 M} (h : Annihilates q U) :
    Quadratic.Dickson.gaussSum q = ↑(Nat.card ↥U) * Quadratic.Dickson.gaussSum h.descend

    The Gauss-sum radical fibration (design §4-N8): a QuadF2 M that annihilates a submodule U inside its polar radical (Annihilates q U — the two clauses q|_U = 0, b_q(U, M) = 0) has Gauss sum

      G(Q) = |U| · G(Q̄),

    Q̄ = h.descend the green descended form on M ⧸ U: the quadratic map is constant on U-cosets (form_add_mem), so the sign-character sum fibres over the quotient with |U|-fold multiplicity. No nonsingularity hypothesis.

    theorem Q2Presentation.Induction.gaussSum_of_radical_fibration_value {M : Type u_1} [AddCommGroup M] [Module (ZMod 2) M] [Fintype M] {U : Submodule (ZMod 2) M} [Fintype (M ⧸ U)] {q : Quadratic.QuadF2 M} (h : Annihilates q U) (hns : h.descend.Nonsingular) (hpos : 0 < Module.finrank (ZMod 2) (M ⧸ U)) :
    Quadratic.Dickson.gaussSum q = 2 ^ Module.finrank (ZMod 2) ↥U * (Quadratic.baseGaussSign (Quadratic.gaussType h.descend) * 2 ^ (Module.finrank (ZMod 2) (M ⧸ U) / 2))

    The applied value form of §4-N8 — with the descended form nonsingular and of positive dimension, gaussSum_eq_sign_two_pow evaluates the descended factor:

      G(Q) = 2^{dim U} · (baseGaussSign (gaussType Q̄) · 2^{d̄/2}),   d̄ = dim (M ⧸ U).

    gaussType (h.descend) is the Gauss type of the descended (base-problem) form and is LEFT FREE here (F1 + audit-§A discipline; see the file header): its constancy/value belong to the §5 sign floors, never to this unit. In the R4b application (N7) the nonsingularity hypothesis is discharged by edgeHeadForm_nonsingular-transport and hpos from edgeHead_nontrivial.
