    Candidate deformation duality — the Fox–Heisenberg chain map and its boundaries #

    This file formalises the candidate side of cor:adjointboundary (reference/q2_manuscript.tex, §5, l.1687–2025): the four numerical/duality endpoints attached to the finite word complex C_{ρ,A}^• of Lifting/WordComplex.lean. These are the candidate-source values that Stage F compares numerically with the local Galois cohomology — there is no map between the two complexes, only an equality of dimensions, multiplicities and perfect pairings.

    1. The Fox–Heisenberg chain map D_{ρ,A} (prop:chainmap, eq:Dmap) #

    Under the identifications Hom((A^∨)^k, F₂) ≅ A^k, the chain map D_{ρ,A} : C_{ρ,A}^• → Hom(C_{ρ,A^∨}^•, F₂)[-2] has the explicit endpoints

      D⁰(a) = (a, a)                          (eq:D0)
  ⟨D¹(x), y⟩ = 𝓑_{ρ,A}(x, y)              (eq:D1)
  D²(u_t, u_w) = u_t + u_w.                (eq:D2)

    Its degree-one component D¹ is the traced Fox–Heisenberg central form 𝓑_{ρ,A} = β_t + β_w (prop:tracedstokes). For the trivial module (prop:defduality, l.1936) this traced form is exactly the scalar cup–Bockstein table cupFormQ of Lifting/HeisenbergCup.lean.

    2. Candidate deformation duality (prop:defduality) #

    The chain map is a quasi-isomorphism, so the induced cup pairings H^i_{A,ρ}(A) × H^{2-i}_{A,ρ}(A^∨) → F₂ are perfect. For the trivial module the degree-one pairing on H¹ is cupFormQ, whose nondegeneracy (cupFormQ_nondegenerate) we package as a self-duality isomorphism H¹ ≃ₗ (H¹)^∨ and the resulting dimension equality.

    3. The lift torsor (lem:affinelifting, l.4015) #

    Z¹_{Γ,ρ} acts freely and transitively on each nonempty fibre of the map from lifts to their T-quotient, so a nonempty lift set is a torsor under Z¹; its cardinality is Nat.card Z¹ (lift_torsor_card).

    4. The connecting homomorphism ∂_{Γ,ρ} (l.2005) and cor:adjointboundary #

    ∂_{Γ,ρ} : H¹_{Γ,ρ}(V) → H²_{Γ,ρ}(T) is the connecting homomorphism of the coefficient short exact sequence 0 → T → M → V → 0, realised on the candidate side as the snake map of the degreewise-exact sequence of word complexes (lem:exactcoefffunctor, eq:coneshortexact). cor:adjointboundary (eq:adjointboundary) is its adjointness ⟨∂c, χ⟩ = ⟨c, ρ*γ_χ⟩ against the perfect cup pairing.

                    §4. The connecting homomorphism ∂_{Γ,ρ} (l.2005) and cor:adjointboundary #

                    The candidate-side connecting homomorphism ∂_{Γ,ρ} : H¹_{Γ,ρ}(V) → H²_{Γ,ρ}(T) of the coefficient short exact sequence 0 → T → M → V → 0 is the snake map of the degreewise-short-exact sequence of word complexes (lem:exactcoefffunctor, eq:coneshortexact). We construct it abstractly from the degree-(1, 2) truncation: degree-two cohomology is the cokernel H² = coker d¹, degree-one cocycles are Z¹ = ker d¹, and the connecting map is Z¹(V) → coker d¹_T = H²(T). This is the candidate ∂_{Γ,ρ} at cocycle level (it already descends to H¹; the manuscript uses it on Z¹ directly, l.4015).

                              The adjoint-boundary relation (cor:adjointboundary, eq:adjointboundary). The connecting map ∂ : H¹_{Γ,ρ}(V) → H²_{Γ,ρ}(T) is adjoint to the dual-boundary map ρ*∘∂_C^∨ : H²(T)^∨ → H¹(V^∨) against the evaluation pairing on H²(T) and the perfect cup pairing cup = ⟨·,·⟩_Γ on H¹(V) × H¹(V^∨):

                                ⟨∂c, χ⟩ = ⟨c, ρ*γ_χ⟩_Γ        for all c ∈ H¹(V), χ ∈ H²(T)^∨.

                              On the candidate side this holds by the naturality of the Fox–Heisenberg chain map D_{ρ,A} (prop:chainmap, eq:chainid1/eq:chainid2, i.e. the traced finite-word Stokes identity prop:tracedstokes); on the local side by the compatibility of connecting homomorphisms with cup product and the invariant map (prop:localduality). The two sources thus share the same connecting map.
