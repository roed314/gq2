    The Fox–Heisenberg cup calculation (manuscript §5, l.1488–2025) #

    This file formalises the class-2 second-derivative layer of the candidate linear-lifting theory: the mixed Heisenberg/extraspecial coefficient group, the exact class-two collector identity, the bilinear commutator (cup) pairing it carries, the ω₂-twisted mixed-Hessian pairing operator 1 + U + U⁻¹ (U = S^{ω₂}), and the scalar cup/Bockstein Hilbert form on H¹.

    It sits on top of Lifting/WordComplex.lean (the word complex d⁰, d¹, the cohomology H¹, H²) and Foundation/Omega2.lean (powOmega2 = (·)^{ω₂}).

    The mixed Heisenberg coefficient group H(A) (manuscript l.1490–1510) #

    For an elementary F₂[C]-module A with dual A^∨ = Hom_{F₂}(A, F₂), set H(A) = A × A^∨ × F₂ with (a,λ,z)(a',λ',z') = (a+a', λ+λ', z+z'+λ(a')). This is a genuine (non-abelian) Heisenberg 2-group; its multiplication and inversion are the finite Fox recursion formulas (eq:heisrules1, eq:heisrules2).

    The exact class-two identity (lem:class2square, l.1301, eq:class2collector) #

    In any class-≤2 group with squares/commutators central, commutators of order ≤2, and g⁴=1, the collector 𝔥_φ(X,D) = φ(X)·X · φ(D)·D · D²·[φ(D),D] collapses to φ(X)·X·D⁻¹·φ(D); for φ=1 it is X² (the h₀ ↦ x₀² replacement of lem:h0shadow, central coordinate λ(c)).

    The cup / Hilbert form (manuscript l.1125–1133, l.1944) #

    For the trivial module A=F₂, d¹(a,b,c,d)=(b,b), so H¹ is 3-dimensional on (σ,x₀,x₁), and the traced Fox–Heisenberg form is ad'+da'+cc', i.e. the scalar cup/Bockstein table ac'+ca'+bb' (eq:HilbertTable) with quadratic initial form b²+ac+ca.

              Part 4 — the ω₂-twisted mixed-Hessian pairing operator 1 + U + U⁻¹ #

              (lem:mixedhessian eq:pairingoperator, lem:simplenormalforms, l.1862–1888.) With U = S^{ω₂} the 2-primary part of the σ-action, the degree-one cup pairing on a nontrivial simple module is induced by 1 + U + U⁻¹, which the manuscript shows is invertible because U has 2-power order, so its minimal polynomial divides a power of X+1, coprime to X²+X+1. We capture this as: a unipotent U (i.e. U−1 nilpotent) makes 1 + U + U⁻¹ a unit, and U = S^{ω₂} is unipotent because it has 2-power order.
