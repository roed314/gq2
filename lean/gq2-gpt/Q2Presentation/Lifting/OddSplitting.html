    Odd-averaging equivariant splitting (generic) #

    Pure group/module theory backing the §7 shear-splitting residual: for a finite group H acting linearly on ZMod 2-modules M and V through ρM : H →* (M ≃ₗ M), ρV : H →* (V ≃ₗ V), with an equivariant surjection π : M →ₗ V whose kernel is fixed pointwise, an H-equivariant linear section of π exists as soon as there is an odd normal subgroup A ≤ H that is either everything (Maschke averaging) or has no nonzero fixed vectors on V (averaging over A, then norm-trick rigidity kills the H-defects).

    Also: in a finite group generated by s, t with the tame relation s⁻¹ t s = t², the cyclic subgroup ⟨t⟩ is normal (manuscript Lemma 3.1 mechanism, extracted standalone).
