    Stage E foundation — the candidate linear-lifting word complex (manuscript §5) #

    This file formalises the candidate side of the linear lifting theory of reference/q2_manuscript.tex §5 ("Linear lifting theory and duality", l.1215–1490): the finite word complex

      C_{ρ,A}^•:   0 ⟶ A  --d⁰-->  A⁴  --d¹-->  A²  ⟶ 0           (eq:defcomplex)

    attached to a finite group C, a boundary-compatible epimorphism ρ : Γ ↠ C (Γ = G_A, the candidate group), and a finite elementary F₂[C]-module A (an F₂-vector space, here [AddCommGroup A] [Module (ZMod 2) A]).

    The data (l.1251) #

    S, T, X₀, X₁ : A →ₗ[ZMod 2] A are the actions on A of the four lower values ρ(σ), ρ(τ), ρ(x₀), ρ(x₁) of the marked generators σ, τ, x₀, x₁ (Q2Presentation.Gen). They are the images of the four generators under ρ followed by the C-action; in §FromRepresentation below they are produced from a genuine ρ : C →* Module.End (ZMod 2) A (this monoid hom is the datum Representation (ZMod 2) C A) and four group elements s t x₀ x₁ : C.

    d⁰ (eq:d0explicit, l.1254) #

    Simultaneous infinitesimal conjugation with the lift convention (a,g) in the semidirect product gives

      d⁰(v) = ((S-1)v, (T-1)v, (X₀-1)v, (X₁-1)v).

    Here 1 is LinearMap.id and, since A is an F₂-module, S - 1 = S + 1.

    d¹ — the evaluated Fox Jacobian (lem:jacobian, eq:simplejac/Ltactual/Lwactual) #

    d¹ is the ordered pair of the tame-relator defect and the wild-relator defect (l.1257): writing the lift of the four generators into C ⋉ A as (ρ(σ),a), (ρ(τ),b), (ρ(x₀),c), (ρ(x₁),d), the value d¹(a,b,c,d) is the A-coefficient of the two evaluated relators (Q2Word.tameRelator, Q2Word.wildRelator). This is functorial in A because it evaluates the same two finite words in semidirect products.

    The manuscript's closed form (lem:jacobian, eq:simplejac) — which we take here as the definition — is, with P = 1 + T + ⋯ + T^{e-1} the norm of the cyclic group ⟨T⟩ (e = orderOf T):

      d¹ = ⎛ S⁻¹(1+T)   S⁻¹+1+T    0    0     ⎞      (tame row L_t, eq:Ltactual)
       ⎝ 0          P          0    P+S⁻¹ ⎠      (wild row L_w, eq:Lwactual)

    i.e. L_t = S⁻¹(1+T)·a + (S⁻¹+1+T)·b and L_w = P·b + (P+S⁻¹)·d.

    Derivation of the tame row (proof of lem:jacobian, l.1463): differentiating τ^σ·τ⁻² = rconj τ σ · (τ²)⁻¹ (= Q2Word.tameRelator) in A⋊C with (u,g)(v,h)=(u+gv,gh) gives D(τ^σ τ⁻²)(a,b) = S⁻¹(T-1)a + (S⁻¹-(1+T))b, which over F₂ is exactly L_t.

    Derivation of the wild row (lem:fullwordledger, l.1387; lem:h0shadow, l.1342): Q2Word.wildRelator = h₀·u₁⁻¹·x₁^σ·c₀. By lem:h0shadow the factor h₀ has the first-order value of x₀², hence first derivative 0; the commutator c₀ = (d₀,z₀) has first derivative 0; u₁ = (x₁τ)^{ω₂} differentiates to the norm projector P of the odd T-orbit (lem:finiteexponent makes the ω₂-power independent of the chosen finite integer representative, so D(g^{ω₂}) = P on the odd part — this is the only place ω₂ enters d¹, and it enters as P); x₁^σ contributes S⁻¹. The factor ledger (l.1402) sums to L_w = P·b + (P+S⁻¹)·d.

    The complex property and cohomology #

    d¹∘d⁰ = 0 is the Fox fundamental identity Σᵢ (∂r/∂xᵢ)(xᵢ-1) = r-1: each relator r is trivial in Γ, so its image in GL(A) is trivial, and the row of d¹∘d⁰ is (image of r) − 1 = 0. Concretely the three linear shadows of the group relations are needed:

    • Sinv * T * S = T^2 — the image of the tame relation σ⁻¹τσ = τ²;
    • P * (T - 1) = 0 — P is the norm of ⟨T⟩ (automatic from T^{orderOf T}=1);
    • (P + Sinv) * (X₁ - 1) = 0 — the linear shadow of the wild relator (automatic on a simple tame factor, where X₁ = 1, by lem:h0shadow).

    Cohomology: Z¹ = ker d¹, H¹ = ker d¹ / im d⁰, H² = coker d¹.

                Instantiation from a representation ρ : C →* End A #

                A ρ : C →* Module.End (ZMod 2) A is exactly the datum of a Representation (ZMod 2) C A (a C-action by ZMod 2-linear maps). Given the four lower values s t x₀ x₁ : C (the images ρ(σ), ρ(τ), ρ(x₀), ρ(x₁)), the four endomorphisms are ρ s, ρ t, ρ x₀, ρ x₁, with ρ s⁻¹ the inverse of ρ s and P = ∑_{i<orderOf t} (ρ t)^i the norm. All hypotheses of d1_comp_d0 except the wild shadow are then automatic; the wild shadow is automatic on a simple tame factor (ρ x₁ = 1).
