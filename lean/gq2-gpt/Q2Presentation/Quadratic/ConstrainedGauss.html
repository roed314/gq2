    Stage G / P6 — the constrained quadratic Gauss transform (lem:constrainedgauss) #

    This file formalizes the finite quadratic-form algebra consumed by the §8 zero-edge formula (prop:zeroedge, reference/q2_manuscript.tex l.4314–4369):

    • lem:constrainedgauss (l.3876–3906, eq:constrainedgauss l.3888–3894): for a surjection L : W ↠ E of finite F₂-spaces and a quadratic form Q on W with polar form b_Q,

        2·|E|·#{x : L x = κ, Q(x) = ε} = |W| + G(Q⁰)·∑_{χ ∈ E^∨} (-1)^{χ(κ)+ε+Q⁰(a_χ)},

      where b_Q(a_χ, ·) = χ ∘ L (+ linear part) and G(Q⁰) = ∑_x (-1)^{Q⁰(x)} is the Gauss sum (Dickson.gaussSum). We prove the affine form directly (constrainedGauss_count): the quadratic function is Q⁰ + ℓ with ℓ linear (the eq:Qkappadifference shape, l.2158–2163: Q_κ = Q⁰ + ⟨·,ρ*γ_κ⟩ + const, constants being absorbed into ε), the representing vectors solve b_Q(a_χ, ·) = χ∘L + ℓ (manuscript a_{χ,κ} = (b_q^♭)^{-1}(γ_χ + γ_κ), l.4089–4094), and the surviving phase is the base-form value Q⁰(a_χ) (manuscript Θ_q^0(a_{χ,κ}), eq:Theta0phase l.4097–4100). At ℓ = 0 this is lem:constrainedgauss verbatim (constrainedGauss_count_pure).

    • the completed square (QuadF2.completedSquare): Q(x) + b_Q(a,x) = Q(x+a) + Q(a) — the algebraic core of prop:phaseidentity (l.4108–4146, the display at l.4137–4142) and of the lem:constrainedgauss proof step (l.3899–3904).

    • existence and uniqueness of the representing vectors a_χ from nonsingularity (QuadF2.polar_bijective, QuadF2.exists_polarRep, QuadF2.polarRep_unique) — the manuscript's "let a_χ be uniquely determined by b_Q(a_χ,x) = χ(Lx)" (l.3883–3885).

    • the linear-shift constructor QuadF2.addLinear (eq:Qkappadifference at form level): adding a linear term keeps the polar form, hence nonsingularity — this is why prop:zeroedge may complete the square against the base form.

    Everything here is pure finite linear algebra over F₂ — no arithmetic input, no axioms; the two sources enter only later, through the common value of G(Q⁰) (prop:candidatezero l.2731 / prop:localzero l.3349, see Quadratic/GaussSumValue.lean and audit/BLOCKR_P6_DESIGN.md).

    Proof idioms deliberately mirror the green Quadratic/DicksonCount.lean (sign-character expansion, translation reindexing, Finset calculus).

        The constrained quadratic Gauss transform (lem:constrainedgauss, eq:constrainedgauss l.3888–3894, in the affine form consumed by eq:pointwiseconstrained l.4332–4340):

          2·|E|·N(κ,ε) = |W| + G(Q⁰)·∑_{χ ∈ E^∨} (-1)^{χ(κ) + ε + Q⁰(a_χ)}

        for L : W ↠ E, Q⁰ a quadratic form with representing vectors b_{Q⁰}(a_χ, ·) = χ∘L + ℓ, and N(κ,ε) = #{x : Lx = κ, Q⁰(x) + ℓ(x) = ε}. The Gauss sum G(Q⁰) = ∑_x (-1)^{Q⁰(x)} is Dickson.gaussSum; the phase Q⁰(a_χ) is the manuscript's Θ_q^0(a_{χ,κ})-value (eq:Theta0phase).

        Note the hypotheses are weaker than the manuscript's: nonsingularity is not needed once the representing vectors A are supplied (it is needed only for their existence — constrainedGauss_count_exists), and surjectivity of L is used only to cancel nontrivial characters.
