    Stage G — Quadratic determinant obstructions and the Gauss-sign comparison (manuscript §6) #

    This file is the comparison core of §6 ("Quadratic determinant obstructions", reference/q2_manuscript.tex, l.2025–3597). The linear interface of §5 (Lifting/) does not determine the scalar central pushouts attached to a self-dual simple module V, because their lifting obstruction is quadratic. §6 constructs a normalized equivariant base class κ_q^0, computes the resulting quadratic form on the candidate and local sides, and proves their Gauss sums agree (prop:localzero, l.3347 — the conclusion this file targets).

    The determinant obstruction (l.2034–2049) #

    For a C-equivariant central extension 1 → R → M̃ → M → 1 of class ≤ 2 with R, M elementary abelian, and λ ∈ (R^∨)^C, pushing out along λ : R → F₂ gives a central double cover of M whose square map q_λ(m) = λ(m̃²) ∈ F₂ is quadratic, with polar form the commutator pairing. On the nontrivial simple head V this is a nonzero nonsingular quadratic form (prop:simpleheaddet).

    This is exactly the class-two Heisenberg ledger of Lifting/HeisenbergCup.lean: the square diagonal (u²).z = λ(v) (HeisQ.sq_z) is the square map, and the commutator central coordinate (⁅u,w⁆).z = bcl u w (HeisQ.commutator_z) is the polar form. We package this as QuadF2 (a quadratic form over F₂ with explicit polar form) and exhibit the extraspecial pushout extraspecialForm.

    The candidate-side base form (l.2458–2536, prop:wordquadratic, eq:QA) #

    The complete base second-order word expansion of the wild relator gives

      Q_A^0(c) = q(c) + b_q((P+1)c, U^{-1}c),     U = S^{ω₂}            (eq:QA, l.2470)

    with ramified cases (eq:QAcases): Q_A^0 = q when T=1 (unramified), and Q_A^0(c) = q(c) + b_q(c, U^{-1}c) when V^T = 0 (ramified). Its polar form (eq:polaroperator) is

      b_A(c,c') = b_q(c, (1 + U + U^{-1}) c').                          (eq:polaroperator)

    The operator 1 + U + U^{-1} is exactly Lifting.pairingOp, already proven a unit in HeisenbergCup.lean (pairingOp_powOmega2_isUnit, because U = S^{ω₂} has 2-power order ⟹ unipotent). So the candidate base form (candidateForm) is a genuinely nonsingular QuadF2, proven here by reusing that infrastructure.

    The Gauss sums (l.2731 prop:candidatezero, l.3349 prop:localzero) #

    A nonsingular quadratic form of dimension d over F₂ has # Q⁻¹(0) = 2^{d-1} + (-1)^{Arf} 2^{d/2-1}. Both sides compute the same count (baseZeroCount):

      # Q⁻¹(0) = 2^{d-1} - 2^{d/2-1}   (V unramified, minus type),
  # Q⁻¹(0) = 2^{d-1} + 2^{d/2-1}   (V ramified,  plus  type).
    • Candidate (prop:candidatezero): unramified Arf = 1 by the Hermitian-line classification (lem:unitaryline); ramified Arf = Arf(q) + rank(1+U) = s+s = 0 by Wall doubling (lem:wall) + the ramified Hermitian/fixed-space computation (lem:ramifiedhermitian).
    • Local (prop:localzero): unramified Arf = 1 by the same Hermitian line (lem:unitaryline, the norm-one group acts by isometries); ramified the deep half X_+ = Hom_{H_V}(V^∨, U_{e+1}) is totally singular of dimension d/2 (lem:shapirodet), so the form is hyperbolic (Arf = 0), via the dyadic square-class filtration and Hilbert-symbol orthogonality U_i^⊥ = U_{2e-i+1}, -1 ∈ U_e (eq:unitorth) and the deep-unit Evens-norm vanishing (lem:evensvanish).

    Part 0 — Quadratic forms over F₂ with explicit polar form #

    A QuadF2 V is a function form : V → F₂ together with its polar (commutator) form polar : V → V → F₂ (a genuine bilinear map) satisfying the quadratic law form (x+y) = form x + form y + polar x y. Over F₂ the polar form is automatically alternating (polar_self) and symmetric (polar_comm), both derived below; this is the manuscript's "polar form given by the commutator pairing" (l.2045).

                Part 3 — The Gauss sign of a finite nonsingular F₂ quadratic space #
