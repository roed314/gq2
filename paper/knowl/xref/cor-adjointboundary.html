Corollary 5.17. Common lift numerics and adjoint boundaries.

For either source, use the corresponding notation \(H^i_{\Gamma,\rho}\) and \(Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}\text{.}\) Then the obstruction-space dimensions, unobstructed lift multiplicities, and perfect pairings agree numerically.
🔗
For every short exact sequence
\begin{equation*} 0\to T\to M\to V\to0 \end{equation*}
and its dual, the connecting maps are adjoint: if
\begin{equation*} \partial_{\Gamma,\rho}:H^1_{\Gamma,\rho}(V)\to H^2_{\Gamma,\rho}(T) \end{equation*}
and \(\chi\in(T^\vee)^C\) has dual boundary
\begin{equation*} \gamma_\chi=\partial_C^\vee(\chi)\in H^1(C,V^\vee), \end{equation*}
then
\begin{equation} \langle\partial_{\Gamma,\rho}c,\chi\rangle =\langle c,\rho^*\gamma_\chi\rangle_\Gamma.\tag{5.29} \end{equation}

Proof.
For the candidate, (5.29) is the naturality of the chain map (5.17) applied to the coefficient sequence. For the local source it is the compatibility of connecting homomorphisms with cup product and the local invariant map in the Tate duality pairing used in Proposition 5.16. The numerical assertions are Proposition 5.15, Proposition 5.16.
