Corollary 6.19. Complete source interface.

For every finite lower quotient, every lower exact-image map, and every elementary characteristic-\(2\) coefficient module, the two sources have:
🔗
  1. the same obstruction-space dimension and the same number of lifts when the obstruction vanishes;
    🔗
    🔗
  2. natural perfect pairings for all short exact coefficient sequences, with adjoint connecting homomorphisms;
    🔗
    🔗
  3. the same scalar cup–Bockstein form for the trivial module;
    🔗
    🔗
  4. for every nonzero invariant nonsingular quadratic form on a simple self-dual head, the same base determinant Gauss sum for the class \(\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
Together with the marked maximal pro-\(2\) identification in Proposition 3.10, these are the source-side inputs supplied to the target induction. This corollary is not by itself a determinacy statement: equality of base Gauss sums is used only after Fourier expansion. For a general pushout class
\begin{equation*} \kappa=\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}+\Gamma_{\gamma_\kappa}+ \operatorname{inf}\delta_\kappa, \end{equation*}
the terms \(\gamma_\kappa\) and \(\delta_\kappa\) enter only through the scalar classes obtained by completing the square in Proposition 8.8. The corresponding signed sums are expressed as exact-image counts in central double covers of the lower target.
🔗
🔗

Proof.
Items (i)–(iii) are Proposition 5.15, Proposition 5.16, Corollary 5.17 and (3.18); item (iv) is Proposition 6.9, Proposition 6.18. The later operations are restrictions, pushouts, exact-image partitions, finite Fourier transforms, and central-cover constructions internal to the finite target. The determinacy of those operations is isolated in Theorem 8.17 and then executed in Section 9; the central-cover identity (8.22) supplies the remaining affine information.
