Lemma 8.7. Affine \(T\)-lifting equation.

Choose the splitting \(B/T\cong V\rtimes C\) above and let
\begin{equation*} e\in H^2(C,T) \end{equation*}
be the class obtained by pulling \(B\to V\rtimes C\) back along the zero section \(C\to V\rtimes C\text{.}\) For a lower exact-image map \(\rho:\Gamma\twoheadrightarrow C\text{,}\) a class
\begin{equation*} c\in H^1_{\Gamma,\rho}(V) \end{equation*}
is the \(V\)-coordinate of an actual \(M\)-lift if and only if
\begin{equation} \partial_{\Gamma,\rho}(c)=\rho^*e \quad\text{in }H^2_{\Gamma,\rho}(T).\tag{8.8} \end{equation}
For every such class, the number of raw lifts above it is
\begin{equation} \mu=|B^1_{\Gamma,\rho}(V)|\,|Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}(T)|,\tag{8.9} \end{equation}
which is independent of \(\rho\) and has the same value on the two sources.

Proof.
Represent the \(T\)-extension of \(V\rtimes C\) by a normalized cocycle. Its pure \(V\times V\) component is zero because \(M\) is elementary abelian; its mixed component is the module extension \(0\to T\to M\to V\to0\text{;}\) and its restriction to the zero section is \(e\text{.}\) Pulling this cocycle back along the graph of a \(V\)-cocycle gives exactly \(\partial c+\rho^*e\text{,}\) proving (8.8) in characteristic two.
🔗
Each cohomology class has \(|B^1(V)|\) cocycle representatives, and each solvable \(T\)-defect equation has \(|Z^1(T)|\) solutions. For the nontrivial simple \(V\text{,}\) \(|B^1(V)|=2^{\dim V}\text{;}\) Proposition 5.15, Proposition 5.16 gives the common value of \(|Z^1(T)|\text{.}\)
