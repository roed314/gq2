Lemma 6.3. Base equivariant determinant class.

Let \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) be a nontrivial simple self-dual tame \(\F_2[\notn{Cfin}{C}]\)-module, let \(H=H_V\) be its faithful tame image, and let \(q:\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to\F_2\) be a nonzero \(H\)-invariant nonsingular quadratic form. There is a normalized base class
\begin{equation*} \notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}\in Z^2(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\rtimes H,\F_2) \end{equation*}
whose restriction to \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) has square map \(q\text{.}\)
🔗
Choose an \(H\)-split pair
\begin{equation*} \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\xrightarrow{i}W=\F_2[H]^N\xrightarrow{p}\notnfar{Vhead}{V}, \qquad pi=1, \end{equation*}
and put \(q_W=q\circ p\text{.}\) In the left-regular coordinates \(X_{j,h}\) of \(W\text{,}\) the unique algebraic normal form of the \(H\)-invariant quadratic map \(q_W\) is a sum of orbit polynomials of the following three types:
\begin{align*} S_j&=\sum_{h\in H}X_{j,h}^2,\\ C_{j,k,g}&=\sum_{h\in H}X_{j,h}X_{k,hg} \quad\text{when the corresponding unordered pair has trivial stabilizer},\\ E_{j,g}&=\sum_{u\in\mathcal R_g}X_{j,u}X_{j,ug}, \qquad g^2=1,\ g\ne1, \end{align*}
where \(\mathcal R_g\) is any set of representatives for the right cosets of \(\langle g\rangle\) in \(H\text{.}\) For a square orbit use the \(H\)-invariant factor set
\begin{equation} f_{S_j}(x,y)=\sum_{h\in H}x_{j,h}y_{j,h}, \qquad m_c=0.\tag{6.17} \end{equation}
For a free orbit use
\begin{equation} f_{C_{j,k,g}}(x,y)=\sum_{h\in H}x_{j,h}y_{k,hg}, \qquad m_c=0.\tag{6.18} \end{equation}
For an involution-stabilized orbit use the cocycle (6.15) of Lemma 6.2 on the \(j\)th regular summand, with \(\mathcal R=\mathcal R_g\text{.}\) Let \(\kappa_{q_W}^0\) be the sum of the cocycles corresponding to the orbit polynomials occurring in \(q_W\text{,}\) and put
\begin{equation} \notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}=(i\rtimes1)^*\kappa_{q_W}^0.\tag{6.19} \end{equation}
Then \(\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}\) vanishes on the zero section and \(\operatorname{res}_V(\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0})\) has square map \(q\text{.}\) If a lower target has quotient \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\) acting through \(H\text{,}\) we inflate this class along \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\to H\) and keep the same notation.

Proof.
We construct the class on a split regular module by the three possible orbit types of quadratic monomials and then pull it back to \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\text{.}\)
🔗
If \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is ramified, \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is projective over \(\F_2[H]\) by Lemma 6.11; if \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is unramified, the \(2\)-primary subgroup of the cyclic unramified image acts trivially on every simple characteristic-\(2\) module, so the faithful image \(H\) has odd order and Maschke’s theorem applies. Thus the split pair exists.
🔗
The algebraic normal form of a quadratic map over \(\F_2\) is determined by its values on the coordinate vectors and by its polar form. \(H\)-invariance makes its coefficients constant on the orbits of unordered degree-two coordinate monomials. Since the action on each regular coordinate set is free, the stabilizer of an unordered pair is either trivial or has order two and exchanges the two entries. The latter possibility is exactly \(j=k\) with a nontrivial involution \(g\text{,}\) and gives \(E_{j,g}\text{.}\)
🔗
The factor sets (6.17) and (6.18) are \(H\)-invariant, vanish on the zero section, and have square maps \(S_j\) and \(C_{j,k,g}\text{,}\) respectively. By Lemma 6.2, the explicit cocycle for \(E_{j,g}\) has square map \(E_{j,g}\) and also vanishes on the zero section. Their sum is therefore an actual normalized equivariant central cocycle on \(W\rtimes H\) with fibre square map \(q_W\text{.}\) Pullback along \(i\rtimes1\) gives fibre square map \(q_W(iv)=q(v)\) and preserves zero-section normalization.
