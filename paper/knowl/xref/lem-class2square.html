Lemma 5.2. Exact class-two identity.

Let
\begin{equation*} 1\longrightarrow Z\longrightarrow\widetilde V\longrightarrow V\longrightarrow1 \end{equation*}
be a central extension in which \(V\) and \(Z\) are elementary abelian \(2\)-groups, and let \(\phi\) be an automorphism of \(\widetilde V\) fixing \(Z\text{.}\) For \(X,D\in\widetilde V\text{,}\) put
\begin{equation*} \mathfrak h_\phi(X,D) =\phi(X)X\,\phi(D)D\,D^2[\phi(D),D]. \end{equation*}
Then
\begin{equation} \mathfrak h_\phi(X,D)=\phi(X)X D^{-1}\phi(D).\tag{5.3} \end{equation}
Consequently:
🔗
  1. if \(D\) and \(X\) have the same image in \(V\text{,}\) then \(\mathfrak h_\phi(X,D)=X^2\text{;}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. if \(\phi\) is the identity, then \(\mathfrak h_\phi(X,D)=X^2\) for every \(D\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

Proof.
The group \(\widetilde V\) has class at most two, every commutator has order at most two, and every element has fourth power one. Put \(\notn{Amod}{A}=\phi(\notn{Dfox}{D})\text{,}\) \(B=\notn{Dfox}{D}\text{,}\) and \(k=[\notn{Amod}{A},B]\text{.}\) With our commutator convention \(AB=BAk\text{;}\) since \(k\) is central of order at most two and \(B^2\) is central,
\begin{equation*} ABB^2k=BAB^2=B^3\notn{Amod}{A}=B^{-1}\notn{Amod}{A}. \end{equation*}
This proves (5.3).
🔗
If \(\notn{Dfox}{D}=Xz\) with \(z\in Z\text{,}\) then \(\phi(z)=z\) and \(\notn{Dfox}{D}^{-1}\phi(\notn{Dfox}{D})=X^{-1}\phi(X)\text{.}\) Hence \(\mathfrak h_\phi(X,\notn{Dfox}{D})=\phi(X)^2=X^2\text{,}\) because \(X^2\in Z\) and \(\phi\) fixes \(Z\text{.}\) If \(\phi=1\text{,}\) (5.3) gives the second assertion directly.
