Lemma 7.2. Frattini–centralizer collapse.

The subgroup \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\) is central elementary abelian in \(\notn{Kker}{K}\text{,}\) and \(\notn{Kker}{K}^4=1\text{.}\)
🔗
Lean
🔗

Proof.
We first prove that \(\notn{Kker}{K}\) centralizes its Frattini subgroup, then show that the fourth-power homomorphism vanishes, and finally identify \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\) as central and elementary abelian.
🔗
Let \(H_2\) be the maximal \(2\)-quotient of the finite tame group \(\notnfar{Htarget}{H}\text{,}\) let \(N=\ker(\notnfar{Htarget}{H}\to H_2)\text{,}\) and choose an odd Hall lift \(\widetilde N\) in the preimage of \(N\) in \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\text{.}\) Along a chief series for \(S\text{,}\) the action of \(\widetilde N\) on every factor is trivial; coprime action gives
\begin{equation*} [S,\widetilde N]=1. \end{equation*}
Since \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\le \notn{Kker}{K}\cap S\text{,}\) \(\widetilde N\) centralizes \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\text{.}\)
🔗
Put \(\mathcal C_R=C_K(\notn{Rrad}{R})\text{.}\) It is characteristic in \(\notn{Kker}{K}\) and hence normal in \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\text{.}\) The three-subgroup lemma gives \([\notn{Kker}{K},\widetilde N]\le\mathcal C_R\text{.}\) The group \(N\) acts nontrivially on \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\text{:}\) otherwise the action would factor through the \(2\)-group \(\notnfar{Htarget}{H}/N\text{,}\) whose only simple module in characteristic \(2\) is trivial. Hence \([\notnfar{Vhead}{V},N]=\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} [\notn{Kker}{K},\widetilde N]S=P. \end{equation*}
Thus \(\mathcal C_RS=P\text{,}\) and minimality of \(\notn{Kker}{K}\) gives \(\mathcal C_R=\notn{Kker}{K}\text{.}\) Therefore \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\le Z(\notn{Kker}{K})\text{.}\)
🔗
Since \(\notn{Kker}{K}/\notn{Rrad}{R}\) is elementary abelian, squares lie in \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\) and commutators have order at most \(2\text{.}\) The fourth-power map
\begin{equation*} f:\notn{Kker}{K}\to \notn{Rrad}{R}, \qquad f(k)=k^4, \end{equation*}
is a \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\)-equivariant homomorphism. The subgroup \(\widetilde N\) fixes \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\text{,}\) so \(f([k,n])=1\) for \(k\in \notn{Kker}{K}\text{,}\) \(n\in\widetilde N\text{.}\) The \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\)-normal closure of \([\notn{Kker}{K},\widetilde N]\) still maps onto \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) modulo \(S\text{,}\) hence equals \(\notn{Kker}{K}\) by minimality. Thus \(f=1\) and \(\notn{Kker}{K}^4=1\text{.}\) Finally
\begin{equation*} \notn{Rrad}{R}=\notn{Frattini}{\Phi}(\notn{Kker}{K})=\notn{Kker}{K}^2[\notn{Kker}{K},\notn{Kker}{K}] \end{equation*}
is central of exponent \(2\text{.}\)
