Lemma 9.1. Coprime-kernel subdirect products.

Let \(A\twoheadrightarrow C\) and \(B\twoheadrightarrow C\) be finite epimorphisms whose kernels have coprime orders. If a subgroup \(J\le A\times_C B\) projects onto both \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) then \(J=A\times_C B\text{.}\)
🔗
Lean
🔗

Proof.
The kernel of \(J\to A\) is a subgroup of \(\ker(\notnfar{Bmid}{B}\to \notnfar{Cfin}{C})\text{,}\) and the kernel of \(J\to \notnfar{Bmid}{B}\) is a subgroup of \(\ker(A\to \notnfar{Cfin}{C})\text{.}\) Comparing the two order formulas for \(J\) shows that both kernels have their full possible orders, because the two ambient kernel orders are coprime. Thus \(J\) contains both kernels and is the full fibre product.
