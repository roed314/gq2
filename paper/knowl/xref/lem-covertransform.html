Lemma 8.3. Central-cover exact-image transform.

Let \(\notnfar{calY}{\mathcal Y}=(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y},\notnfar{LY}{L_Y},\notnfar{piY}{\pi_Y},\notnfar{thetaY}{\theta_Y})\) be boundary-framed and let
\begin{equation*} p:\widetilde Y\twoheadrightarrow \notnfar{Ytarget}{Y} \end{equation*}
be a central double cover. Give it the pulled-back structure
\begin{equation*} \widetilde L=p^{-1}(\notnfar{LY}{L_Y}), \qquad \widetilde\pi=\pi_Yp, \qquad \widetilde\theta=\theta_Yp, \end{equation*}
and assume the central kernel lies in \(\ker(\widetilde\pi,\widetilde\theta)\text{.}\) Fix an exact-image subgroup \(J\le \notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\) projecting onto \(\notnfar{Htarget}{H}\text{.}\) If \(u_\Gamma^\beta(p,J)\) counts boundary-framed exact-image maps to \(J\) whose pullback cover is split, then
\begin{equation} 8u_\Gamma^\beta(p,J) =\sum_{\substack{\widetilde J\le p^{-1}(J)\\p(\widetilde J)=J}} \notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}^\beta(\widetilde J,\widetilde J\cap\widetilde L, \widetilde\pi|_{\widetilde J}, \widetilde\theta|_{\widetilde J}).\tag{8.1} \end{equation}
Every term on the right is therefore an ordinary exact-image object in the same global boundary-framed category.

Proof.
An unobstructed map \(f:\Gamma\twoheadrightarrow J\) has a lift, and the set of all lifts is a torsor under \(\Hom(\Gamma,\F_2)\text{.}\) By Lemma 8.2, this group has order \(8\) for both sources, and scalar twisting preserves the boundary-framed condition. Twisting a lift changes only the new central coordinate. Because that coordinate lies in the kernel of both the tame marking and the decoration, every twist still satisfies the identical boundary equation. The exact image of a lift is a subgroup \(\widetilde J\le p^{-1}(J)\) mapping onto \(J\text{,}\) and every such exact-image lift occurs in precisely one summand. This is a literal partition of the eight lifts, proving (8.1); it also proves exact-image and decoration compatibility without an equidistribution assumption.
