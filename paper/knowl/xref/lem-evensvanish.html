Lemma 6.16. Deep-unit Evens norm.

Let \(L/k\) be an unramified quadratic extension of finite dyadic local fields and put \(e=v_k(2)\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation} \Evens_{L/k}(\alpha)=0 \qquad(\alpha\in U_{e+1}(L)\subset L^\times/L^{\times2}).\tag{6.52} \end{equation}
🔗
Lean
🔗

Proof.
We translate the Evens norm into the degree-two Evens–Kahn identity, reduce the trace forms to Hilbert symbols, and then cancel the remaining terms by norm and Steinberg relations.
🔗
By Lemma 6.13, the Evens norm is represented by the explicit two-point graph cocycle (6.40). The index-two Evens–Kahn formula identifies this normalization with the degree-two term in the relative Stiefel–Whitney identity [10], [11]
\begin{equation} w(\Tr_{L/k}\langle a\rangle)= w(\Tr_{L/k}\langle1\rangle) \bigl(1+\cor_{L/k}[a]+\Evens_{L/k}([a])\bigr).\tag{6.53} \end{equation}
🔗
Take a class in \(U_{e+1}(L)\) and choose from the outset a representative
\begin{equation*} a=1+2b,\qquad b\in\mathfrak p_L. \end{equation*}
Write \(L=k(\sqrt d)\text{,}\) where \(d\) is the unramified quadratic square class, and write
\begin{equation*} a=u+v\sqrt d,\qquad n=N_{L/k}(a)=u^2-dv^2. \end{equation*}
If
\begin{equation*} t=\Tr_{L/k}(b),\qquad m=N_{L/k}(b), \end{equation*}
then
\begin{equation} u=1+t\in\mathcal O_k^\times, \qquad n=1+2t+4m.\tag{6.54} \end{equation}
In particular the divisions by \(u\) below are legitimate. The trace forms diagonalize as
\begin{equation*} \Tr_{L/k}\langle a\rangle\simeq \left\langle2u,\frac{2dn}{u}\right\rangle, \qquad \Tr_{L/k}\langle1\rangle\simeq\langle2,2d\rangle. \end{equation*}
Taking degree two in (6.53) first gives
\begin{equation*} \Evens_{L/k}([a])=(2u,2dn/u)+(2,2d)+(d,n). \end{equation*}
We now keep every Hilbert-symbol cancellation visible. Bilinearity and \((x,x)=(x,-1)\) give
\begin{align*} (2u,2dn/u) &=(2u,2)+(2u,d)+(2u,n)+(2u,u),\\ (2u,2)+(2u,u)&=(2u,2u)=(2u,-1),\\ (2,2d)&=(2,-1)+(2,d). \end{align*}
Consequently
\begin{align*} \Evens_{L/k}([a]) &= (u,-1)+(u,d)+(2u,n)+(d,n)\\ &= (u,-d)+(2du,n). \end{align*}
Thus
\begin{equation} \Evens_{L/k}([a])=(u,-d)+(2du,n).\tag{6.55} \end{equation}
🔗
In more detail: the Kummer coordinates are part of the proof, not additional arithmetic hypotheses. Since the residue characteristic is not the field characteristic, an unramified quadratic extension has a presentation \(L=k(\delta)\) with \(\delta^2=d\in k^\times\text{,}\) and every deep unit can be written uniquely as \(a=u+v\delta\text{.}\) The index-two condition ensures \(\delta\notin k\text{;}\) together with \(a\ne0\text{,}\) this shows that \(n=u^2-dv^2\) is nonzero and that the divisions in the displayed diagonalization are legitimate.
🔗
Because \(L/k\) is unramified and \(b\in\mathfrak p_L\text{,}\) one has \(v_k(m)\ge2\text{.}\) From (6.54),
\begin{equation} \frac{n}{2u-1} =1+\frac{4m}{2u-1}\in U_{2e+2}(k).\tag{6.56} \end{equation}
Here \(2u-1\) is a unit and \(v_k(4m)\ge2e+2>2v_k(2)\text{.}\) Hensel’s lemma, applied to \(X^2-n/(2u-1)\) at \(X=1\text{,}\) therefore gives \(U_{2e+2}(k)\subset k^{\times2}\text{.}\) Thus \(n\) and \(2u-1\) have the same square class.
🔗
Unramifiedness is used here in two distinct ways. Equality of value groups gives the depth estimate that makes \(n/(2u-1)\) a square by Hensel’s lemma. In the remaining line, surjectivity of the norm on units kills the symbols involving \(d\text{;}\) the Steinberg relation then reduces the answer to two copies of \((u,-1)\text{,}\) which cancel in characteristic two.
🔗
Every unit of \(k\) is a norm from its unramified quadratic extension, and \(n=N_{L/k}(a)\text{.}\) Hence
\begin{equation*} (u,d)=0,\qquad(d,n)=0. \end{equation*}
Formula (6.55) becomes
\begin{equation*} (u,-1)+(2u,2u-1). \end{equation*}
The Steinberg relation gives \((2u,2u-1)=(2u,-1)\text{.}\) Finally \((2,-1)=0\text{:}\) if \(i\in k\text{,}\) then \(-1\) is a square; otherwise \(2=N_{k(i)/k}(1+i)\text{.}\) Consequently \((2u,-1)=(u,-1)\text{,}\) and the two terms cancel.
