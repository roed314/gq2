Lemma 5.11. Exact cone d\’evissage.

For an elementary \(\F_2[C]\)-module \(A\text{,}\) put
\begin{equation} K(A)=\Cone\!\left( D_{\rho,A}:C^\bullet_{\rho,A}\longrightarrow \Hom_{\F_2}(C^\bullet_{\rho,A^\vee},\F_2)[-2] \right).\tag{5.20} \end{equation}
For every short exact sequence of finite \(\F_2[C]\)-modules
\begin{equation*} 0\longrightarrow A'\longrightarrow A\longrightarrow A''\longrightarrow0, \end{equation*}
there is a degreewise short exact sequence of complexes
\begin{equation} 0\longrightarrow K(A')\longrightarrow K(A)\longrightarrow K(A'') \longrightarrow0.\tag{5.21} \end{equation}
Consequently, if two of \(K(A')\text{,}\) \(K(A)\text{,}\) and \(K(A'')\) are acyclic, then so is the third. In particular, acyclicity for all simple modules implies acyclicity for every elementary module, including nonsplit and nonsemisimple modules.

Proof.
We verify exactness for the word complexes, then for their duals, and finally for the associated mapping cones.
🔗
The finite-word construction is additive and exact in the coefficient module, so the source complexes form a degreewise short exact sequence
\begin{equation} 0\longrightarrow C^\bullet_{\rho,A'} \longrightarrow C^\bullet_{\rho,A} \longrightarrow C^\bullet_{\rho,A''}\longrightarrow0.\tag{5.22} \end{equation}
Dualizing the coefficient sequence gives
\begin{equation*} 0\longrightarrow A''^\vee\longrightarrow A^\vee \longrightarrow A'^\vee\longrightarrow0. \end{equation*}
Applying the same finite-word complex and then \(\Hom_{\F_2}(-,\F_2)\) reverses this sequence once more, and yields
\begin{equation} \begin{split} 0\longrightarrow&\ \Hom_{\F_2}(C^\bullet_{\rho,A'^\vee},\F_2)[-2] \longrightarrow \Hom_{\F_2}(C^\bullet_{\rho,A^\vee},\F_2)[-2]\\ \longrightarrow&\ \Hom_{\F_2}(C^\bullet_{\rho,A''^\vee},\F_2)[-2] \longrightarrow0. \end{split}\tag{5.23} \end{equation}
For compactness write
\begin{equation*} C^\dagger(A)=\Hom_{\F_2}(C^\bullet_{\rho,A^\vee},\F_2)[-2]. \end{equation*}
Strict coefficient naturality of the Heisenberg central coordinate gives the commutative diagram
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccccc} C^\bullet_{\rho,A'}&\longrightarrow&C^\bullet_{\rho,A} &\longrightarrow&C^\bullet_{\rho,A''}\\ \downarrow D_{\rho,A'}&&\downarrow D_{\rho,A}&&\downarrow D_{\rho,A''}\\ C^\dagger(A')&\longrightarrow&C^\dagger(A)&\longrightarrow&C^\dagger(A''). \end{array} \end{equation*}
Here the rows are the nonzero portions of (5.22) and (5.23), and commutativity is Lemma 5.6, Proposition 5.10. Mapping cones are formed degree by degree, so this diagram gives (5.21). The associated long exact cohomology sequence proves the two-out-of-three assertion. Applying it successively to a composition series of \(A\) proves the final statement.
