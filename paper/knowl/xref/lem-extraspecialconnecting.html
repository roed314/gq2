Lemma 6.1. Equivariant extraspecial connecting cocycle.

Let \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) be an elementary \(\F_2[\notn{Cfin}{C}]\)-module, let \(q:\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to\F_2\) be a \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-invariant quadratic form with polar form \(\notn{bq}{b_q}\text{,}\) and let
\begin{equation*} f:\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\times \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to\F_2, \qquad f(v,v)=q(v),\qquad f(v,w)+f(w,v)=\notn{bq}{b_q}(v,w), \end{equation*}
be a normalized factor set for the central extension
\begin{equation*} E_f=\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\times\F_2, \qquad (v,z)(w,t)=(v+w,z+t+f(v,w)). \end{equation*}
When an equivariant lift is chosen, its action in the normalized section is written by functions \(m_c:\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to\F_2\) satisfying
\begin{equation} m_c(v+w)+m_c(v)+m_c(w)=f(cv,cw)+f(v,w)\tag{6.1} \end{equation}
and
\begin{equation} m_{cd}(v)=m_c(dv)+m_d(v),\qquad m_1=0.\tag{6.2} \end{equation}
Equivalently,
\begin{equation*} c\cdot(v,z)=(cv,z+m_c(v)) \end{equation*}
defines an action by automorphisms of \(E_f\text{.}\) The associated normalized base central cocycle on \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\rtimes \notn{Cfin}{C}\) is
\begin{equation} \notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}((v,c),(w,d))=f(v,cw)+m_c(w).\tag{6.3} \end{equation}
Consequently, for a source \(\notnfar{GammaSrc}{\Gamma}\text{,}\) a lower map \(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}:\notnfar{GammaSrc}{\Gamma}\to \notn{Cfin}{C}\text{,}\) and a normalized \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\)-valued \(1\)-cocycle \(b\text{,}\) the base connecting cocycle is
\begin{equation} (b,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho})^*\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}(g,h) =f(b(g),\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}(g)b(h))+m_{\rho(g)}(b(h)).\tag{6.4} \end{equation}
🔗
If \(\kappa\) is another normalized central cocycle on \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\rtimes \notn{Cfin}{C}\) whose restriction to \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is the same quadratic form \(q\text{,}\) then, after replacing \(\kappa\) by a cohomologous normalized cocycle and changing the normalized section, the following is an equality of normalized \(2\)-cochains:
\begin{equation} \kappa=\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}+\Gamma_\gamma+ \operatorname{inf}_C^{\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\rtimes \notn{Cfin}{C}}\delta,\tag{6.5} \end{equation}
where
\begin{equation} \Gamma_\gamma((v,c),(w,d))=\gamma(c)(cw), \qquad \gamma\in Z^1(\notn{Cfin}{C},\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee),\quad \delta\in Z^2(\notn{Cfin}{C},\F_2).\tag{6.6} \end{equation}
After graph pullback and application of the source functional \(\notn{iotaGamma}{\iota_\Gamma}\text{,}\) the corresponding scalar quadratic obstruction is
\begin{equation} Q_{\kappa,\notnfar{GammaSrc}{\Gamma},\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(b) =Q^0_{\Gamma,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(b) +\langle b,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*\gamma\rangle_\Gamma +\notn{iotaGamma}{\iota_\Gamma}(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*\delta),\tag{6.7} \end{equation}
where
\begin{equation*} Q^0_{\Gamma,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(b)= \notn{iotaGamma}{\iota_\Gamma}\bigl((b,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho})^*\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}\bigr). \end{equation*}
Its polarization is the source pairing transported by \(\notn{bq}{b_q}\text{.}\)

Proof.
We verify the equivariant central extension, identify the filtration-one and inflated difference terms, and then compute the polarization.
🔗
Equation (6.1) is exactly the condition that \((v,z)\mapsto(cv,z+m_c(v))\) preserve the multiplication in \(E_f\text{;}\) equation (6.2) says that these automorphisms form a genuine \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-action. Multiplication in the semidirect product attached to this lifted action is
\begin{equation} (v,c,z)(w,d,t)= (v+cw,cd,z+t+f(v,cw)+m_c(w)),\tag{6.8} \end{equation}
which gives (6.3). Pulling back this cocycle by the graph of a \(1\)-cocycle \(b\) gives (6.4). This is the term which would be missing if one incorrectly assumed that the section \(v\mapsto(v,0)\) were \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-equivariant.
🔗
For the difference statement, the quotient \(\kappa-\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}\) restricts trivially to \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\times \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\text{.}\) In the normalized Lyndon–Hochschild–Serre filtration for the split extension \(1\to \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\rtimes \notn{Cfin}{C}\to \notn{Cfin}{C}\to1\text{,}\) such a class has only a filtration-one part in \(Z^1(\notn{Cfin}{C},\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee)\) and an inflated scalar part in \(Z^2(\notn{Cfin}{C},\F_2)\text{.}\) The normalized representative is exactly (6.6); the cocycle condition for it is the condition that \(\gamma\) be a \(1\)-cocycle. Pullback along \((b,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho})\) gives (6.7). Polarization of the base term is obtained by adding the formulas for \(b+b'\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(b'\text{;}\) the \(m_c\)-contribution cancels because each \(m_c\) has polar form \(f(c-,c-)+f(-,-)\text{,}\) and the remaining term is \(\notn{bq}{b_q}(b\smile b')\text{.}\) The difference terms in (6.7) are linear or inflated scalar terms and therefore do not change the polar form.
