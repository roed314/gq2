Lemma 6.11. Faithful-image projectivity.

If \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is ramified, then \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) and \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee\) are projective \(\F_2[H_V]\)-modules.
🔗
Lean (tame pair case)Lean (main)
🔗

Proof.
We use Clifford theory over an algebraic closure to describe the inertia weights, prove regularity on Sylow-\(2\) weight blocks, and then descend projectivity to \(\F_2\text{.}\)
🔗
Let \(\overline{\F}_2\) be an algebraic closure, let \(I\triangleleft H_V\) be tame inertia, and choose an absolutely irreducible constituent \(W_0\) of \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\otimes_{\F_2}\overline{\F}_2\text{.}\) Since \(|I|\) is odd, \(W_0|_I\) is semisimple. Choose a nontrivial inertia character \(\chi\) occurring in it, and let \(H_\chi\) be its stabilizer. Clifford theory expresses \(W_0\) as the induction of a simple \(H_\chi\)-module on which \(I\) acts through \(\chi\text{.}\) The quotient \(H_\chi/I\) is cyclic. Since \(\chi\) is \(H_\chi\)-invariant and \(\overline{\F}_2\) contains all roots needed to satisfy the single cyclic relation, \(\chi\) extends to a one-dimensional character of \(H_\chi\text{.}\) After twisting by that extension, the inducing module factors through the cyclic quotient. Over \(\overline{\F}_2\) every simple module for a cyclic group is one-dimensional, and its \(2\)-primary subgroup acts trivially. Thus every \(\chi\)-weight space in \(W_0\) is one-dimensional.
🔗
Let the Frobenius orbit of \(\chi\) have length \(f=2^a r\) with \(r\) odd, and let \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}\) be a Sylow \(2\)-subgroup of \(H_V\text{.}\) The image of \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}\) in the cyclic quotient \(H_V/I\) is cyclic. Its action on the orbit of \(\chi\) has \(2\)-power orbit length \(2^a\text{.}\) If an element of \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}\) stabilizes \(\chi\text{,}\) it acts on the one-dimensional \(\chi\)-weight space by a scalar of \(2\)-power order, hence trivially because \(\overline{\F}_2^\times\) has no nontrivial \(2\)-power torsion. By conjugacy it then acts trivially on all inertia-weight spaces of \(W_0\text{,}\) and on every Galois conjugate constituent as well. Faithfulness of the image \(H_V\) therefore shows that this stabilizer is trivial. Consequently \(|\notn{Pnorm}{P}|=2^a\text{,}\) and \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}\) acts freely on the weights in blocks of size \(2^a\text{.}\)
🔗
On each block, transport a nonzero vector around the \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}\)-orbit. Because the generator of \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}\) has order \(2^a\text{,}\) the transported vectors form a regular \(\overline{\F}_2[\notn{Pnorm}{P}]\)-basis. Hence the restriction of every absolutely irreducible constituent, and therefore of \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\otimes_{\F_2}\overline{\F}_2\text{,}\) is a direct sum of regular \(\overline{\F}_2[\notn{Pnorm}{P}]\)-modules. It is projective over \(\overline{\F}_2[\notn{Pnorm}{P}]\text{.}\)
🔗
Projectivity descends along the faithfully flat extension \(\F_2\subset\overline{\F}_2\text{,}\) so \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}|_P\) is projective over \(\F_2[\notn{Pnorm}{P}]\text{.}\) The Sylow criterion for modular projectivity now gives projectivity over \(\F_2[H_V]\text{.}\) Replacing every inertia character by its inverse gives the identical Sylow restriction calculation for \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee\text{,}\) and hence proves projectivity of the dual module.
