Lemma 5.1. Finite-exponent independence.

Suppose \(\nu\) and \(\nu'\) represent \(\notn{w}{\w}\) modulo the exponent of every finite semidirect and Heisenberg group occurring in a fixed first- or second-order calculation. Replacing every \(\notn{w}{\w}\)-power in the relators by \(\nu\) or by \(\nu'\) gives the same first derivative and the same mixed second derivative.

Proof.
The two ordinary powers define the same element in each finite group in which the relator is evaluated. First and mixed derivatives are, by definition, the noncentral and central coordinates of those finite evaluations. They are therefore identical. The representative may be chosen separately for each finite coefficient calculation; no global coherent integer is required.
