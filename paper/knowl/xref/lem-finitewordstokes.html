Lemma 5.7. Finite-word Stokes formula.

Let \(F_n=\langle g_1,\dots,g_n\rangle\) be a free group, let \(\rho:F_n\to C\) be a homomorphism to a finite group, and write \(c_i=\rho(g_i)\text{.}\) Let \(r\in F_n\) be an ordinary finite word with \(\rho(r)=1\text{,}\) and let \(\epsilon_i(r)\in\F_2\) be the total exponent of \(g_i\) in \(r\text{,}\) reduced modulo \(2\text{.}\)
🔗
For \(x=(x_i)\in A^n\) and \(y=(y_i)\in(A^\vee)^n\text{,}\) evaluate \(r\) after replacing \(g_i\) by \((x_i,y_i,0;c_i)\in\mathcal H(A)\rtimes C\text{.}\) Write the result as
\begin{equation*} \bigl(L_r^A(x),L_r^{A^\vee}(y),\beta_r(x,y);1\bigr). \end{equation*}
Then, for \(a\in A\) and \(\lambda\in A^\vee\text{,}\)
\begin{align} \beta_r(d^0a,y) &=\langle a,L_r^{A^\vee}(y)\rangle +\sum_{i=1}^n\epsilon_i(r)\,y_i(c_i a),\tag{5.9}\\ \beta_r(x,d^0\lambda) &=\langle L_r^A(x),\lambda\rangle +\sum_{i=1}^n\epsilon_i(r)\,\lambda(x_i),\tag{5.10} \end{align}
where
\begin{equation*} d^0a=((c_i-1)a)_i, \qquad d^0\lambda=((c_i-1)\lambda)_i. \end{equation*}
Consequently, for words \(r_1,\dots,r_m\) and coefficients \(t_j\in\F_2\) satisfying
\begin{equation} \sum_{j=1}^m t_j\epsilon_i(r_j)=0 \qquad(1\le i\le n),\tag{5.11} \end{equation}
the corresponding traced mixed coordinate satisfies the two Stokes identities without correction terms.

Proof.
The two identities follow from conjugating by a primal and a dual coefficient, after which the total exponent vector records the central correction contributed by each generator.
🔗
Put
\begin{equation*} p_a=(a,0,0;1),\qquad p_\lambda=(0,\lambda,0;1), \qquad \mathbf z(t)=(0,0,t;1). \end{equation*}
A direct multiplication in \(\mathcal H(A)\rtimes C\) gives
\begin{align*} p_a^{-1}(0,y_i,0;c_i)p_a &=((c_i-1)a,y_i,0;c_i)\,\mathbf z\bigl(y_i(c_i a)\bigr),\\ p_\lambda^{-1}(x_i,0,0;c_i)p_\lambda &=(x_i,(c_i-1)\lambda,0;c_i)\,\mathbf z\bigl(\lambda(x_i)\bigr). \end{align*}
The elements \(\mathbf z(t)\) are central of order at most two. Therefore, on evaluating an ordinary word, the extra central factor attached to the \(i\)th generator occurs exactly \(\epsilon_i(r)\) times.
🔗
If only the dual variables \(y_i\) are present, the value of \(r\) is \((0,L_r^{A^\vee}(y),0;1)\text{.}\) Conjugating this value by \(p_a\) contributes the central coordinate \(L_r^{A^\vee}(y)(a)\text{.}\) Adding the generatorwise central corrections gives (5.9). Repeating this conjugation calculation with \(p_\lambda\) starts from \((L_r^A(x),0,0;1)\) and gives (5.10). Condition (5.11) cancels the correction terms. Every step is functorial in \(A\text{.}\)
