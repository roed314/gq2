Lemma 5.4. Full finite-word ledger.

At a tame lower map, write first-order variations of \((\notn{sigma}{\sigma},\notn{tau}{\tau},\notn{x0}{x_0},\notnfar{x1}{x_1})\) as \((a,b,c,d)\text{.}\) Let \(\mathsf S,\mathsf T\) be the lower actions of \(\notn{sigma}{\sigma},\notn{tau}{\tau}\text{,}\) put \(\mathsf U=\mathsf S^\notn{w}{\w}\text{,}\) and let \(P=1+\mathsf T+\cdots+\mathsf T^{e-1}\) for the odd order \(e\) of \(\mathsf T\text{.}\) The raw finite Fox rules give
\begin{equation*} D(u_i)=P(Dx_i+D\notn{tau}{\tau}), \qquad D(d_0)=P b+(P+1)c, \qquad D(z_0)=\mathsf U^{-1}c. \end{equation*}
The first-order contributions of the wild factors are
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|cccc} \text{factor}&a&b&c&d\\ \hline h_0&0&0&0&0\\ u_1^{-1}&0&P&0&P\\ \notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}&0&0&0&\mathsf S^{-1}\\ {[d_0,z_0]}&0&0&0&0. \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the wild row is \(L_{\mathrm w}=Pb+(P+\mathsf S^{-1})d\text{.}\)
🔗
Write \(b_{\mathrm{cl}}\) for the bilinear commutator pairing of the chosen class-two coefficient group. For the mixed Heisenberg or extraspecial calculation on the normalized cohomology representative supported on \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\text{,}\) the complete central ledger is
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c} \text{factor}&\text{$V$-coordinate}&\text{central coordinate}\\ \hline h_0&0&\lambda(c)\text{ in }\mathcal H(V),\; q(c)\text{ for }\kappa_q^0\\ u_1^{-1}&0&0\\ \notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}&0&0\\ d_0=(\notn{x0}{x_0}\notn{tau}{\tau})^\notn{w}{\w} \notn{x0}{x_0}^{-1}&(P+1)c&\ast\\ z_0=\notn{x0}{x_0}^{\sigma_2}&\mathsf U^{-1}c&\ast\\ {[d_0,z_0]}&0&b_{\mathrm{cl}}((P+1)c,\mathsf U^{-1}c). \end{array} \end{equation*}
The symbols \(\ast\) are irrelevant because \(d_0\) and \(z_0\) enter the relator only through their commutator. The safety of replacing \(h_0\) by \(\notn{x0}{x_0}^2\) in these ledgers is exactly Lemma 5.3: the variation of \(g_0=\sigma_2^2\) starts in order at least two; when \(P=0\) one has \(Dd_0=D\notn{x0}{x_0}\text{,}\) so Lemma 5.2(i) applies; when \(P=1\text{,}\) the lift of \(\mathsf U\) to the class-two coefficient group is a central automorphism of order at most two, so \(g_0\) acts trivially and Lemma 5.2(ii) applies.
