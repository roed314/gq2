Lemma 5.3. The elementary and class-two shadow of \(h_0\).

Let \(\notn{Vhead}{V}\) be either the trivial module or a nontrivial simple tame \(\F_2[\notn{Cfin}{C}]\)-module. For an arbitrary lower map, the marked wild subgroup acts trivially on every simple factor, so the evaluated simple-factor derivatives are the same as those of the induced tame lower map. At that tame map:
🔗
  1. the first derivative of \(h_0\) is the first derivative of \(\notn{x0}{x_0}^2\text{,}\) namely zero;
    🔗
    🔗
  2. on the normalized degree-one representatives supported on \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\text{,}\) the mixed Heisenberg and extraspecial central coordinates of \(h_0\) are respectively those of \(\notn{x0}{x_0}^2\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
Thus \(h_0\) may replace \(\notn{x0}{x_0}^2\) in every linear, cup-product, and determinant calculation used below.
🔗
🔗

Proof.
We first compute the linear variation of \(h_0\) and then treat its class-two central coordinate separately in the cases \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=0\) and \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=1\text{.}\)
🔗
Let \(\notn{opU}{\mathsf U}=\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^\notn{w}{\w}\text{.}\) If the first-order variations of \(\notn{tau}{\tau}\) and \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\) are \(b\) and \(c\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} Dd_0=\notn{Pnorm}{P} b+(\notn{Pnorm}{P}+1)c, \qquad \notn{Dfox}{D}\notn{x0}{x_0}=c. \end{equation*}
The variation of \(g_0\) affects only terms of order at least two, because the base values of \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\) and \(d_0\) at the tame map are both the identity. If \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=0\text{,}\) then \(Dd_0=c=\notn{Dfox}{D}\notn{x0}{x_0}\text{,}\) and the four linear terms in \(\mathfrak h_{g_0}(\notn{x0}{x_0},d_0)\) cancel. If \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=1\text{,}\) then \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1\) and the \(2\)-primary part \(\notn{opU}{\mathsf U}\) acts trivially on a simple characteristic-\(2\) module; again the four linear terms cancel. This proves (i).
🔗
For the normalized cohomology representatives used in the mixed and quadratic calculations, only \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\) varies. If \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=0\text{,}\) the two elements \(d_0\) and \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\) have the same image \(c\) in \(\notn{Vhead}{V}\text{,}\) so Lemma 5.2(i) applies. If \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=1\text{,}\) then \(Dd_0=0\) and \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1\text{.}\) The \(2\)-primary operator \(\notn{opU}{\mathsf U}\) acts trivially on the simple quotient \(\notn{Vhead}{V}\text{.}\) Its lift to a Heisenberg or extraspecial coefficient group is therefore a central automorphism. Such an automorphism has order at most \(2\text{,}\) because it is determined by a homomorphism from the elementary quotient to the central involution. Hence \(g_0=\sigma_2^2\) acts trivially on the full coefficient group, and Lemma 5.2(ii) applies. For the trivial module, \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=\notn{opU}{\mathsf U}=1\text{,}\) so the second case applies without modification. Taking the mixed Heisenberg central kernel gives the square diagonal \(\lambda(c)\text{;}\) taking the extraspecial central kernel gives \(q(c)\text{.}\) This proves (ii).
