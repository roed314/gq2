Lemma 5.5. Evaluated Jacobian.

Write the lift variables on \((\notn{sigma}{\sigma},\notn{tau}{\tau},\notn{x0}{x_0},\notnfar{x1}{x_1})\) as \((a,b,c,d)\in \notn{Amod}{A}^4\text{.}\) On a simple tame module the actual relation differential is
\begin{equation} d^1_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}= \begin{pmatrix} \notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}(1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T})&\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}+1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}&0&0\\ 0&\notn{Pnorm}{P}&0&\notn{Pnorm}{P}+\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1} \end{pmatrix}.\tag{5.4} \end{equation}
Equivalently,
\begin{align} L_{\mathrm t} &=\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}(1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T})a+(\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}+1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T})b,\tag{5.5}\\ L_{\mathrm w} &=Pb+(\notn{Pnorm}{P}+\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1})d.\tag{5.6} \end{align}
Postcomposing the tame defect coordinate by \(\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}\) gives the row-normalized expression
\begin{equation*} (1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T})a+\bigl(1+\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}(1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T})\bigr)b, \end{equation*}
which may be used for rank calculations only. The word complex, the mixed derivative, and the endpoint maps below use the unnormalized row (5.5).

Proof.
Use \((u,g)(v,h)=(u+gv,gh)\) in \(\notn{Amod}{A}\rtimes \notn{Cfin}{C}\text{.}\) A direct calculation gives
\begin{equation*} \notn{Dfox}{D}(\notn{tau}{\tau}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}\notn{tau}{\tau}^{-2})(a,b) =\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}-1)a+\bigl(\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}-(1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T})\bigr)b. \end{equation*}
Over \(\F_2\) this is exactly (5.5).

For an odd-order \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}\)-orbit, differentiation of a \(\notn{w}{\w}\)-power gives the norm projector \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}\text{.}\) By Lemma 5.3, the word \(h_0\) has the first-order value of \(\notn{x0}{x_0}^2\text{,}\) hence zero. The remaining wild factors give
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|cccc} &a&b&c&d\\ \hline h_0&0&0&0&0\\ u_1^{-1}&0&\notn{Pnorm}{P}&0&\notn{Pnorm}{P}\\ \notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}&0&0&0&\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}\\ {[d_0,z_0]}&0&0&0&0. \end{array} \end{equation*}
Their sum is (5.6). Without the simplicity assumption, the same finite Fox rules define the word complex; the displayed reduction is the simple-factor calculation used in the devissage.
