Lemma 3.5. Marked abelianization, orientation, and initial form.

Use arithmetic local reciprocity \(\notn{rec}{\rec}\text{,}\) normalized so that \(\notn{rec}{\rec}(2)\) is arithmetic Frobenius, and normalize \(\notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}}\) geometrically. Put
\begin{equation*} \notn{bars}{\bar s}=\notn{rec}{\rec}(2)^{-1}=\notn{rec}{\rec}(1/2),\qquad \bar\epsilon=\notn{rec}{\rec}(-1),\qquad \notn{bary}{\bar y}=\notn{rec}{\rec}(-3),\qquad \notn{bara}{\bar a}=\bar\epsilon\notn{bars}{\bar s}^{-2}=\notn{rec}{\rec}(-4). \end{equation*}
Then the pro-\(2\) abelianization of \(\notnfar{Dloc}{D_{\mathrm{loc}}}=G_{\Qtwo}(2)\) is identified with \(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\) by
\begin{equation*} \notn{barA}{\bar A}\longmapsto\notn{bara}{\bar a}, \qquad \notn{barS}{\bar S}\longmapsto\notn{bars}{\bar s}, \qquad \notn{barY}{\bar Y}\longmapsto\notn{bary}{\bar y}. \end{equation*}
Under this identification,
\begin{equation} \notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}}(\notn{bara}{\bar a},\notn{bars}{\bar s},\notn{bary}{\bar y})=(-2,1,0), \qquad \notn{chiloc}{\chi_{\mathrm{loc}}}(\notn{bara}{\bar a},\notn{bars}{\bar s},\notn{bary}{\bar y})=(-1,1,(-3)^{-1}),\tag{3.6} \end{equation}
where \(\notn{chiloc}{\chi_{\mathrm{loc}}}\) is the canonical Demushkin orientation. In the basis of \(H^1(\notnfar{Dloc}{D_{\mathrm{loc}}},\F_2)\) dual to \((\notn{bara}{\bar a},\notn{bars}{\bar s},\notn{bary}{\bar y})\text{,}\) the Demushkin quadratic initial form is
\begin{equation*} \alpha^2+\beta\gamma+\gamma\beta. \end{equation*}
Consequently a Demushkin relator for lifts of these classes has the same quadratic initial form as
\begin{equation*} \notn{r0}{r_0}=\notn{Agen}{A}^2\notn{Sgen}{S}^4[\notn{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}], \end{equation*}
namely \(\notn{Agen}{A}^2+[\notn{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}]\) in degree two. Moreover the pair
\begin{equation*} (\notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}},\notn{chiloc}{\chi_{\mathrm{loc}}}):\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\longrightarrow \mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}_2^\times \end{equation*}
is injective.

Proof.
We compute successively the unramified character, the cyclotomic orientation, the quadratic initial form, and the injectivity of the two-character marking.
🔗
Since \(\notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}}\) is normalized by geometric Frobenius while \(\notn{rec}{\rec}(2)\) is arithmetic Frobenius, one has \(\notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}}(\notn{rec}{\rec}(2))=-1\text{.}\) Hence
\begin{equation*} \notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}}(\notn{bars}{\bar s})=1, \qquad \notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}}(\notn{bary}{\bar y})=0, \qquad \notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}}(\notn{bara}{\bar a})=-2. \end{equation*}
The pro-\(2\) completion of \(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}^\times\) is
\begin{equation*} \widehat{\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}^\times}^{(2)} \cong \langle 2\rangle_{\mathbb{Z}_2} \oplus \langle -1\rangle_{C_2} \oplus \langle -3\rangle_{\mathbb{Z}_2}. \end{equation*}
The single relation among \(\notn{barA}{\bar A},\notn{barS}{\bar S},\notn{barY}{\bar Y}\) is
\begin{equation*} \notn{bara}{\bar a}^2\notn{bars}{\bar s}^4 =\notn{rec}{\rec}((-4)^2(1/2)^4)=\notn{rec}{\rec}(1)=1, \end{equation*}
that is, additively, \(2\notn{barA}{\bar A}+4\notn{barS}{\bar S}=0\text{.}\)
🔗
For a local Demushkin group the canonical orientation is the \(2\)-adic cyclotomic character. With the reciprocity convention above, local reciprocity gives
\begin{equation*} \notn{chiloc}{\chi_{\mathrm{loc}}}(\notn{rec}{\rec}(u))=u^{-1}\qquad(u\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times), \end{equation*}
while the uniformizer component acts trivially on the totally ramified extension \(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}(\mu_{2^\infty})/\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}\text{.}\) This proves the orientation row in (3.6).
🔗
Modulo squares, the classes are
\begin{equation*} \notn{bara}{\bar a}\equiv[-1],\qquad \notn{bars}{\bar s}\equiv[2],\qquad \notn{bary}{\bar y}\equiv[-3]. \end{equation*}
For odd units \(u,v\text{,}\) write
\begin{equation*} \notn{epsu}{\epsilon}(u)=\frac{u-1}{2}\bmod 2, \qquad \notn{omgu}{\omega}(u)=\frac{u^2-1}{8}\bmod 2. \end{equation*}
The dyadic Hilbert-symbol formula
\begin{equation*} (2^\alpha u,2^\beta v)_2 =(-1)^{\notn{epsu}{\epsilon}(u)\notn{epsu}{\epsilon}(v)+\alpha\notn{omgu}{\omega}(v)+\beta\notn{omgu}{\omega}(u)} \end{equation*}
gives, in the square-class basis \((-1,2,-3)\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} (-1,-1)_2=-1, \qquad (2,-3)_2=-1, \end{equation*}
with all other pairings among these basis elements trivial, up to symmetry. Under Kummer theory, cup product is the Hilbert symbol. Thus the dual basis has Bockstein/cup initial form \(\alpha^2+\beta\gamma+\gamma\beta\text{,}\) which is exactly the degree-two initial form of \(\notn{Agen}{A}^2\notn{Sgen}{S}^4[\notn{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}]\text{.}\)
🔗
Finally, in the decomposition (3.4),
\begin{equation*} \notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}}(\notn{tTors}{t},\notn{barS}{\bar S},\notn{barY}{\bar Y})=(0,1,0), \qquad \notn{chiloc}{\chi_{\mathrm{loc}}}(\notn{tTors}{t},\notn{barS}{\bar S},\notn{barY}{\bar Y})=(-1,1,\notn{eta}{\eta}). \end{equation*}
The element \(\notn{eta}{\eta}\) topologically generates \(1+4\mathbb{Z}_2\text{,}\) because \(v_2(\notn{eta}{\eta}-1)=2\text{.}\) The two displayed characters therefore detect the three direct summands of (3.4), proving injectivity.
