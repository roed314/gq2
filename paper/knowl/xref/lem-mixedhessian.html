Lemma 5.14. Mixed Hessian calculation.

Let \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) be a nontrivial simple tame module, put \(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}=\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^\notn{w}{\w}\text{,}\) and represent a class in \(H^1_{A,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notnfar{Vhead}{V})\) by the cocycle supported on \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\) with value \(c\text{.}\) Represent the dual class similarly by \(\lambda\in \notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee\text{.}\) The mixed central contributions are
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|cc} \text{word contribution}&\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1&\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^{\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}}=0\\ \hline r_{\mathrm t}&0&0\\ h_0&\lambda(c)&\lambda(c)\\ {[d_0,z_0]}&0&\lambda(\mathsf Uc)+\lambda(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}c)\\ \text{all other factors of }r_{\mathrm w}&0&0. \end{array} \end{equation*}
Consequently the degree-one pairing is induced by
\begin{equation} \begin{cases} 1,&\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1,\\ 1+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1},&\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^{\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}}=0. \end{cases}\tag{5.26} \end{equation}

🔗

Proof.
The representative exists by the row reduction of (5.4). By Lemma 5.3, the word \(h_0\) contributes exactly the usual Heisenberg square diagonal \(\lambda(c)\text{.}\) In the ramified case the first derivatives on the \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\)-coordinate are
\begin{equation*} Dd_0(c)=c, \qquad Dz_0(c)=\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}c, \end{equation*}
whereas on the dual coordinate they are
\begin{equation*} Dd_0(\lambda)=\lambda, \qquad Dz_0(\lambda)=\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^\vee\lambda. \end{equation*}
Applying (5.7)–(5.8) to the commutator gives
\begin{equation*} \lambda(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}c)+(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^\vee\lambda)(c) =\lambda((\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U})c). \end{equation*}
The remaining factors have either zero first derivative on the normalized representatives or no mixed central term. Adding the \(h_0\) contribution proves the table.
