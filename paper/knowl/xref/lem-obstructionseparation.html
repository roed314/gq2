Lemma 8.10. Scalar pushouts separate the complete obstruction.

Put
\begin{equation*} \notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_R}=(\notn{Rrad}{R}^\vee)^\notnfar{Bmid}{B}=(\notn{Rrad}{R}^\vee)^\notnfar{Cfin}{C}. \end{equation*}
For either source, \(\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_R}\) is canonically the dual of the \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\)-valued obstruction space. If \(o\) is an \(\notn{Rrad}{R}\)-valued lifting obstruction, then
\begin{equation*} o=0\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \lambda_*o=0\text{ for every }\lambda\in\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_R}. \end{equation*}
For \(0\ne\lambda\in\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_R}\text{,}\) the corresponding scalar pushout is the central double cover
\begin{equation*} p_\lambda:B_\lambda=\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}/\ker\lambda\twoheadrightarrow \notnfar{Bmid}{B}. \end{equation*}

🔗

Proof.
By Lemma 7.2, \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\) is elementary abelian and \(\notn{Kker}{K}\) centralizes \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\text{,}\) so the \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\)-action on \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\) factors through \(\notnfar{Cfin}{C}\text{.}\) The two duality theorems give
\begin{equation*} H^2_{A,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notnfar{Rcent}{R})^\vee\cong(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}^\vee)^\notnfar{Cfin}{C}, \qquad H^2_{F,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notnfar{Rcent}{R})^\vee\cong(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}^\vee)^\notnfar{Cfin}{C}. \end{equation*}
For \(\lambda\in(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}^\vee)^\notnfar{Cfin}{C}\text{,}\) the subgroup \(\ker\lambda\) is normal in \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\) and \(\notnfar{Cfin}{C}\) acts trivially on \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}/\ker\lambda\text{.}\) Thus \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}/\ker\lambda\to \notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}/\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\) is a central double cover, and its obstruction is the pairing with \(\lambda\text{.}\) Perfectness proves the equivalence.
