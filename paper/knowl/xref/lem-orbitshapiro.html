Lemma 6.15. Quadratic orbit–stabilizer Shapiro.

Let \(W=\F_2[H]^N\) have coordinates \(X_{r,h}\) with \(H\) acting by left translation on \(h\text{.}\) If \(K/F\) is the \(H\)-extension and \(\alpha_r\in H^1(K,\F_2)\) is the scalar Shapiro coordinate, then the orbit classes of Lemma 6.3 evaluate as follows. For \(g\in H\text{,}\) choose any lift \(\widetilde g\in G_F\) and use the convention
\begin{equation*} (g\alpha_s)(n)=\alpha_s(\widetilde g^{-1}n\widetilde g) \qquad(n\in G_K). \end{equation*}
This is independent of the chosen lift and is the convention fixed in (A.13):
\begin{align} S_r&\longmapsto \cor_{K/F}(\alpha_r^2),\tag{6.45}\\ C_{r,s,g}&\longmapsto \cor_{K/F}(\alpha_r\smile g\alpha_s),\tag{6.46}\\ E_{r,g}&\longmapsto \cor_{K_0/F}\Evens_{K/K_0}(\alpha_r), \qquad K_0=K^{\langle g\rangle}.\tag{6.47} \end{align}

Proof.
We treat square orbits, free orbits, and involution-stabilized orbits in that order, recording at each stage whether the identity is literal at cochain level or only after passage to cohomology.
🔗
Choose lifts \(\widetilde h\in G_F\) of \(h\in H\text{,}\) and write \(\bar\gamma\) for the image of \(\gamma\in G_F\) in \(H\text{.}\) With the left-regular convention used here, the normalized Shapiro map is
\begin{equation*} b_r(\gamma)_h =\operatorname{Sh}(\alpha_r)(\gamma)_h =\alpha_r\!\left(\widetilde h^{-1}\gamma \widetilde{\bar\gamma^{-1}h}\right). \end{equation*}
For scalar cocycles \(\alpha,\beta\) on \(G_K\text{,}\) direct substitution into the normalized bar corestriction gives
\begin{equation} \sum_{h\in H} \operatorname{Sh}(\alpha)(\gamma)_h\, \operatorname{Sh}(\beta)(\notnfar{eta}{\eta})_{\bar\gamma^{-1}h} =\operatorname{cor}_{K/F}(\alpha\smile\beta)(\gamma,\notnfar{eta}{\eta}).\tag{6.48} \end{equation}
The factor set (6.17) therefore gives (6.45). For the free orbit, the coordinate appearing in the monomial is the right shift \(\mathsf R_g\operatorname{Sh}(\alpha_s)\text{.}\) By (A.15) this differs from \(\operatorname{Sh}(g\alpha_s)\) by the explicit coboundary \(dq_{\alpha_s,g}\text{,}\) and (A.16) displays the resulting normalized boundary after taking the cup product and trace. Therefore (6.46) holds in cohomology; it is a literal cochain identity whenever the chosen section is right-\(g\)-equivariant.
🔗
Now let \(J=\langle g\rangle\text{,}\) let \(\mathcal R\) be right-coset representatives for \(H/J\text{,}\) and put \(K_0=K^J\text{.}\) For \(c\in H\) and \(u\in\mathcal R\text{,}\) use the notation
\begin{equation*} c^{-1}u=\pi_c(u)g^{\epsilon_c(u)} \end{equation*}
of Lemma 6.2. Let \(\rho_0:G_{K_0}\twoheadrightarrow J\) be the quotient map. The graph pullback of the explicit half-orbit cocycle (6.15) is
\begin{align} &\sum_{u\in\mathcal R} b_r(\gamma)_u\, b_r(\notnfar{eta}{\eta})_{\bar\gamma^{-1}ug}\notag\\ &\qquad+ \sum_{u\in\mathcal R}\epsilon_{\bar\gamma}(u) b_r(\notnfar{eta}{\eta})_{\pi_{\bar\gamma}(u)} b_r(\notnfar{eta}{\eta})_{\pi_{\bar\gamma}(u)g}.\tag{6.49} \end{align}
For completeness, choose the lifts compatibly so that \(\widetilde{ug}=\widetilde u\,\widetilde g\) for \(u\in\mathcal R\text{,}\) and put
\begin{equation*} \ell_u(\gamma)= \widetilde u^{-1}\gamma\, \widetilde{\pi_{\bar\gamma}(u)} \in G_{K_0}. \end{equation*}
Its image in \(J\) is \(g^{\epsilon_{\bar\gamma}(u)}\text{.}\) In these conventions the normalized corestriction of a \(2\)-cochain \(\nu\) on \(G_{K_0}\) is
\begin{equation} (\operatorname{cor}_{K_0/F}\nu)(\gamma,\notnfar{eta}{\eta}) = \sum_{u\in\mathcal R} \nu\!\left( \ell_u(\gamma), \ell_{\pi_{\bar\gamma}(u)}(\notnfar{eta}{\eta}) \right).\tag{6.50} \end{equation}
Let \(b_J=\operatorname{Sh}_{K/K_0}(\alpha_r)\text{.}\) Compatibility of the transversals gives
\begin{equation*} b_J(\ell_u(\gamma))_1=b_r(\gamma)_u \end{equation*}
and, for \(j\in\{1,g\}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} b_J\!\left(\ell_{\pi_{\bar\gamma}(u)}(\notnfar{eta}{\eta})\right)_j = b_r(\notnfar{eta}{\eta})_{\pi_{\bar\gamma}(u)j}. \end{equation*}
Substituting the explicit two-point cocycle (6.40) into (6.50) gives exactly (6.49): its first summand is the oriented factor-set term, and its second summand is the orientation-reversal correction. Consequently
\begin{equation} (b_r,\rho)^*\kappa_g^{\mathcal R} =\operatorname{cor}_{K_0/F} \left((\operatorname{Sh}_{K/K_0}\alpha_r,\rho_0)^*\kappa_J\right)\tag{6.51} \end{equation}
as normalized cochains. By Lemma 6.13, the inner graph class is \(N_{G_K}^{G_{K_0},\mathrm{Ev}}(\alpha_r)\text{,}\) which is denoted \(\Evens_{K/K_0}(\alpha_r)\) in field notation. Equation (6.47) follows. With the compatible nested transversals just chosen, (6.51) is literal equality of normalized cochains. For arbitrary choices, (A.32) and (A.34) give the two explicit normalized \(1\)-cochains whose boundaries account for the change. Hence the formulas evaluate the actual orbit classes used in \(\kappa_{q_W}^0\text{,}\) including all \(m_c\)-terms, without an unspecified convention-dependent ambiguity.
