Lemma 3.7. Cyclotomic powering of the three peripheral classes.

Let
\begin{equation*} \Delta=\langle P,T\rangle_{\mathrm{pro}-2}, \qquad C=(PT)^{-1}. \end{equation*}
For every \(u\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\) there are an automorphism \(\varphi_u\in\Aut(\Delta)\) and elements \(c_P,c_T,c_C\in\Delta\) such that
\begin{equation} \varphi_u(P)=c_P^{-1}P^u c_P, \qquad \varphi_u(T)=c_T^{-1}T^u c_T, \qquad \varphi_u(C)=c_C^{-1}C^u c_C.\tag{3.7} \end{equation}

Proof.
We obtain the required outer automorphism from the arithmetic Galois action and then choose a representative that realizes the three peripheral conjugacy classes simultaneously.
🔗
Identify \(\Delta\) with the maximal pro-\(2\) geometric fundamental group of
\begin{equation*} X=\mathbb{P}^1_{\mathbb{Q}}\setminus\{0,1,\infty\}, \end{equation*}
with \(P,T,C\) the three compatible peripheral inertia generators. The Galois action on the geometric fundamental group gives
\begin{equation*} G_{\mathbb{Q}}\longrightarrow\operatorname{Out}(\Delta). \end{equation*}
The \(2\)-adic cyclotomic character is surjective, since
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{2^\infty})/\mathbb{Q}) \cong\mathbb{Z}_2^\times. \end{equation*}
Each of the three cusps is rational, so its peripheral conjugacy class is preserved. The corresponding decomposition group acts on the procyclic inertia group through the cyclotomic character; see [8, Section 3.3 and Definition 37]. Concretely, if \(z\) is a local parameter and a chosen inertia generator sends \(z^{1/2^n}\) to \(\zeta_{2^n}z^{1/2^n}\text{,}\) then the arithmetic action of \(g\) replaces \(\zeta_{2^n}\) by \(\zeta_{2^n}^{\chi_2(g)}\text{.}\) With the opposite convention for representing the outer action, the exponent is \(\chi_2(g)^{-1}\text{.}\) Accordingly choose \(g\) with \(\chi_2(g)=u\) in the first convention and with \(\chi_2(g)=u^{-1}\) in the second. In either convention the resulting outer automorphism sends each peripheral generator to a conjugate of its \(u\)-th power. Choosing a representative of the outer class gives (3.7).
