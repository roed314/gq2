Lemma 8.6. Radical edge, variation formula, and descent.

Let \(0\to \notn{Tsub}{T}\to \notnfar{Mmid}{M}\to \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to0\) be the simple-head sequence, and let
\begin{equation*} p:\widetilde B\twoheadrightarrow \notnfar{Bmid}{B} \end{equation*}
be a central double cover whose restriction to \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\) has quadratic form with polar radical \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\) and whose restriction to \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\) is zero. The cover determines a canonical edge class
\begin{equation*} [\varepsilon]\in H^1(\notnfar{Bmid}{B}/\notn{Tsub}{T},\notn{Tsub}{T}^\vee). \end{equation*}
Its restriction to \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is zero, so it is inflated from a unique class
\begin{equation*} [\bar\varepsilon]\in H^1(\notn{Cfin}{C},\notn{Tsub}{T}^\vee). \end{equation*}
For every lower exact-image epimorphism \(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}:\notnfar{GammaSrc}{\Gamma}\twoheadrightarrow \notn{Cfin}{C}\) and every unrestricted \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\)-lift \(f\text{,}\) twisting by \(u\in \notn{Z1Gr}{Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}}(\notn{Tsub}{T})\) changes the scalar obstruction by
\begin{equation} \operatorname{ob}(f_u)=\operatorname{ob}(f) +\bigl\langle [u],\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*[\bar\varepsilon]\bigr\rangle_\Gamma.\tag{8.4} \end{equation}
If \([\bar\varepsilon]\ne0\text{,}\) the free \(\notn{Z1Gr}{Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}}(\notn{Tsub}{T})\)-action partitions the unrestricted \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\)-lifts into orbits in each of which exactly one half satisfy the central relation. If \([\bar\varepsilon]=0\text{,}\) the cover descends to a central double cover of \(\notnfar{Bmid}{B}/\notn{Tsub}{T}\text{;}\) conversely, descent forces the edge class to vanish.

Proof.
We construct the edge cocycle, descend it from \(\notnfar{Bmid}{B}/\notn{Tsub}{T}\) to \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\text{,}\) derive the exact variation formula, and then analyze the nonzero and zero edge cases.
🔗
Let \(z\) generate \(\ker p\text{.}\) Since both the square and commutator forms vanish on \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\text{,}\) the preimage \(\widetilde T=p^{-1}(\notn{Tsub}{T})\) is elementary abelian. Choose a linear complement \(s(\notn{Tsub}{T})\) to \(\langle z\rangle\) in \(\widetilde T\text{.}\)
🔗
For \(\bar b\in \notnfar{Bmid}{B}/\notn{Tsub}{T}\text{,}\) choose a lift \(\widehat b\in\widetilde B\) and define \(\varepsilon(\bar b)\in \notn{Tsub}{T}^\vee\) by
\begin{equation} \widehat b\,s(t)\,\widehat b^{-1} =s({}^{\bar b}t)\, z^{\varepsilon(\bar b)({}^{\bar b}t)}.\tag{8.5} \end{equation}
The definition is independent of the chosen lift \(\widehat b\text{.}\) Applying (8.5) successively to two elements shows
\begin{equation*} \varepsilon(\bar b_1\bar b_2) =\varepsilon(\bar b_1)+{}^{\bar b_1}\varepsilon(\bar b_2), \end{equation*}
so \(\varepsilon\) is a \(1\)-cocycle. Replacing \(s(t)\) by \(s(t)z^{\ell(t)}\) changes it by the coboundary \(d\ell\text{;}\) hence its class is canonical.
🔗
If \(\bar b\in \notnfar{Vhead}{V}=\notnfar{Mmid}{M}/\notn{Tsub}{T}\text{,}\) the exponent in (8.5) is the commutator pairing between a lift of \(\bar b\) and \(t\text{.}\) This is the polar form of the restricted quadratic form, and it is zero because \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\) is the polar radical. Hence \(\res_V[\varepsilon]=0\text{.}\) The group \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}=\notnfar{Mmid}{M}/\notn{Tsub}{T}\) acts trivially on \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\text{,}\) since \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\) is elementary abelian. Inflation–restriction for \(1\to \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to \notnfar{Bmid}{B}/\notn{Tsub}{T}\to \notn{Cfin}{C}\to1\) therefore gives a unique \([\bar\varepsilon]\in H^1(\notn{Cfin}{C},\notn{Tsub}{T}^\vee)\) inflating to \([\varepsilon]\text{.}\) Choose a normalized cocycle representative \(\bar\varepsilon\) of this class. The difference between \(\varepsilon\) and its inflation is a coboundary \(d\ell\) for some \(\ell\in \notn{Tsub}{T}^\vee\text{;}\) changing the complement \(s(\notn{Tsub}{T})\) by \(\ell\) therefore lets us assume from now on that \(\varepsilon=\operatorname{inf}(\bar\varepsilon)\) as cocycles.
🔗
Fix a lower exact-image epimorphism \(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}:\notnfar{GammaSrc}{\Gamma}\twoheadrightarrow \notn{Cfin}{C}\text{,}\) an \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\)-lift \(f:\notnfar{GammaSrc}{\Gamma}\to \notnfar{Bmid}{B}\text{,}\) and lifts \(\widehat f(g)\in\widetilde B\text{.}\) For \(u\in \notn{Z1Gr}{Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}}(\notn{Tsub}{T})\) put
\begin{equation*} f_u(g)=u(g)f(g),\qquad \widehat f_u(g)=s(u(g))\widehat f(g). \end{equation*}
Let \(\omega_f\) and \(\omega_{f_u}\) be the scalar central defects. Using (8.5), the cocycle identity \(u(gh)=u(g)+\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}(g)u(h)\text{,}\) and the fact that \(s(\notn{Tsub}{T})\) is elementary abelian gives the exact identity
\begin{equation} \omega_{f_u}(g,h)=\omega_f(g,h) +\bar\varepsilon(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}(g))\bigl(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}(g)u(h)\bigr).\tag{8.6} \end{equation}
For the local source, applying the invariant map identifies the second term with the ordinary cup pairing. For the candidate source, its evaluation on the two relation defects is the adjoint pairing supplied by the direct finite-word Fox–Heisenberg chain map of Proposition 5.10. Thus (8.6) yields (8.4) in either source theory.
🔗
If \([\bar\varepsilon]\ne0\text{,}\) then \(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*[\bar\varepsilon]\ne0\text{.}\) Indeed, a pulled-back coboundary gives the same coboundary formula on every element of \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\) because \(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}\) is surjective. Perfect degree-one duality therefore makes
\begin{equation*} [u]\longmapsto \bigl\langle[u],\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*[\bar\varepsilon]\bigr\rangle_\Gamma \end{equation*}
a nonzero linear functional on \(\notn{HGr}{H^1_{\Gamma,\rho}}(\notn{Tsub}{T})\text{,}\) and hence on \(\notn{Z1Gr}{Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}}(\notn{Tsub}{T})\text{.}\) This group acts freely on each fibre of the map from \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\)-lifts to their quotient modulo \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\text{.}\) Equation (8.4) is one affine equation with nonzero linear part on every such orbit, so exactly half of its elements satisfy the scalar central relation.
🔗
In more detail: the half-count is a statement about a finite affine torsor, not merely about cohomology classes. Coboundaries lie in the kernel of the variation functional, so nonvanishing is detected on \(H^1\text{;}\) perfect degree-one duality makes the functional nonzero, and a nonzero functional on a finite \(\F_2\)-space has fibres of equal size. The formal proof checks this separately for the two sources: local Tate duality supplies the nonzero class for \(\GQ\text{,}\) while the finite-word obstruction map supplies it for \(\GA\text{.}\) In both cases the scalar obstruction group has two elements, so precisely one half of each twist orbit satisfies the central relation.
🔗
Finally, if \([\bar\varepsilon]=0\text{,}\) then \([\varepsilon]=0\text{.}\) Alter the complement \(s(\notn{Tsub}{T})\) so that the cocycle in (8.5) is identically zero. Then \(s(\notn{Tsub}{T})\triangleleft\widetilde B\text{,}\) and
\begin{equation*} \widetilde B/s(\notn{Tsub}{T})\twoheadrightarrow \notnfar{Bmid}{B}/\notn{Tsub}{T} \end{equation*}
is a central double cover. Conversely, a descended cover supplies a normal complement to the preimage of \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\text{,}\) so the edge class vanishes.
🔗
Vanishing is first used to change the chosen linear complement of the central kernel so that the edge cocycle itself, not only its class, is zero. That complement is then normal, and quotienting by it produces the descended double cover of \(B/T\text{.}\) This normalization is what makes the two alternatives in the later recursion genuinely exhaustive.
