Lemma 6.8. Ramified Hermitian model and Frobenius fixed space.

Let \(I=\langle \mathsf T\rangle\) be tame inertia, let \(V\) be a simple ramified self-dual \(\F_2[H_V]\)-module, and write
\begin{equation*} V|_I\cong W^{\oplus s}, \qquad f=\dim_{\F_2}W=2^a r, \qquad r\text{ odd},\quad a\ge1. \end{equation*}
Let \(q\) be an \(H_V\)-invariant nonsingular quadratic form with polar form \(b_q\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation} \notn{Arf}{\Arf}(q)\equiv s\pmod2.\tag{6.29} \end{equation}
For \(\mathsf U=\mathsf S^{\omega_2}\) one also has
\begin{equation} \dim_{\F_2}V^{\mathsf U}=rs, \qquad \operatorname{rank}(1+\mathsf U)=rs(2^a-1)\equiv s\pmod2.\tag{6.30} \end{equation}
Consequently the ramified candidate base form of (6.25) has \(\notn{Arf}{\Arf}(Q_A^0)=0\text{.}\)

Proof.
We identify the invariant quadratic form with a Hermitian form, compute its Arf invariant one Hermitian line at a time, and then determine the fixed space of the \(2\)-primary Frobenius operator.
🔗
Put
\begin{equation*} F_V=\End_I(W)\cong\F_{2^f}. \end{equation*}
Choose an inertia character \(\zeta\) over \(\overline{\F}_2\text{;}\) then \(W\) is the additive group of \(F_V\) and \(\mathsf T\) acts by multiplication by \(\zeta\text{.}\) Since \(V|_I\) has only the irreducible isomorphism type \(W\) and \(V\cong V^\vee\text{,}\) the inverse character \(\zeta^{-1}\) lies in the Frobenius orbit of \(\zeta\text{.}\) The character is nontrivial of odd order, so this orbit has even length and
\begin{equation*} \zeta^{-1}=\zeta^{2^{f/2}}. \end{equation*}
Set
\begin{equation*} F_{V,0}=\F_{2^{f/2}}, \qquad x^*=x^{2^{f/2}}. \end{equation*}
After an \(I\)-module identification \(V\cong F_V^s\text{,}\) the nondegenerate \(I\)-invariant form \(b_q\) is uniquely expressible as
\begin{equation} b_q(x,y)=\Tr_{F_V/\F_2}(x^{*t}\mathsf H y), \qquad \mathsf H\in\GL_s(F_V),\quad \mathsf H^{*t}=\mathsf H.\tag{6.31} \end{equation}
Indeed, invariance under multiplication by \(\zeta\text{,}\) nondegeneracy of the trace pairing, and symmetry identify the adjoint involution with \(*\) and force the matrix to be Hermitian.
🔗
Define
\begin{equation} q_{\mathsf H}(x)=\Tr_{F_{V,0}/\F_2}(x^{*t}\mathsf H x).\tag{6.32} \end{equation}
The scalar \(x^{*t}\mathsf Hx\) lies in \(F_{V,0}\text{,}\) and
\begin{align*} q_{\mathsf H}(x+y)+q_{\mathsf H}(x)+q_{\mathsf H}(y) &=\Tr_{F_{V,0}/\F_2}(x^{*t}\mathsf Hy+y^{*t}\mathsf Hx)\\ &=\Tr_{F_V/\F_2}(x^{*t}\mathsf Hy)=b_q(x,y). \end{align*}
Thus \(q-q_{\mathsf H}\) is linear. It is \(I\)-invariant, while \((V^\vee)^I=0\) because the inertia action is nontrivial and \(V\) is simple. Hence \(q=q_{\mathsf H}\text{.}\)
🔗
Hermitian diagonalization over the finite field extension \(F_V/F_{V,0}\) gives
\begin{equation*} \mathsf H\sim\operatorname{diag}(a_1,\dots,a_s), \qquad a_i\in F_{V,0}^\times. \end{equation*}
Therefore \(q\) is an orthogonal sum of the \(s\) Hermitian-line forms
\begin{equation*} q_{a_i}(x)=\Tr_{F_{V,0}/\F_2}(a_i xx^*). \end{equation*}
Write \(m=f/2\text{.}\) The norm \(F_V^\times\to F_{V,0}^\times\) is surjective with fibres of size \(2^m+1\text{,}\) and the trace-zero subspace of \(F_{V,0}\) contains \(2^{m-1}-1\) nonzero elements. Hence
\begin{equation*} \#q_{a_i}^{-1}(0) =1+(2^m+1)(2^{m-1}-1) =2^{2m-1}-2^{m-1}. \end{equation*}
Each Hermitian line has Arf invariant \(1\text{.}\) Additivity of the Arf invariant proves (6.29).
🔗
It remains to compute the fixed space of \(\mathsf U\text{.}\) Over \(\overline{\F}_2\text{,}\) the \(f\) inertia weights form one Frobenius cycle, each weight space having multiplicity \(s\text{.}\) Let \(e_\omega\) be the residue modulo \(f=2^ar\) of an integer representing \(\omega_2\text{.}\) By the defining congruences of \(\omega_2\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} e_\omega\equiv1\pmod{2^a}, \qquad e_\omega\equiv0\pmod r. \end{equation*}
Thus translation by \(e_\omega\) on \(\mathbb{Z}/f\mathbb{Z}\) has \(r\) cycles, each of length \(2^a\text{.}\) On one cycle write the successive maps as
\begin{equation*} E_0\xrightarrow{A_0}E_1\xrightarrow{A_1}\cdots \xrightarrow{A_{2^a-1}}E_0, \qquad \dim E_i=s. \end{equation*}
The product \(A_{2^a-1}\cdots A_0\) is the action of \(\mathsf U^{2^a}\) on \(E_0\text{.}\) The shift by \(2^ae_\omega\) is trivial modulo \(f\text{,}\) so \(\mathsf U^{2^a}\) centralizes inertia; it also commutes with \(\mathsf S\text{,}\) being a power of \(\mathsf S\text{.}\) Hence it is a central \(2\)-element of the faithful tame image. A cyclic \(2\)-group acting in characteristic \(2\) has nonzero fixed vectors, and centrality makes its fixed space an \(H_V\)-submodule. Simplicity therefore forces \(\mathsf U^{2^a}=1\) on \(V\text{.}\) Consequently a \(\mathsf U\)-fixed vector on a cycle is uniquely determined by its component in \(E_0\text{,}\) and that cycle contributes dimension \(s\text{.}\) There are \(r\) cycles, proving (6.30).
🔗
Finally, Lemma 6.6 gives
\begin{equation*} \notn{Arf}{\Arf}(Q_A^0)=\notn{Arf}{\Arf}(q)+\operatorname{rank}(1+\mathsf U)=s+s=0 \quad\text{in }\F_2. \end{equation*}
