Lemma 6.14. Regular-module realization of the base local connecting map.

With notation as in Lemma 6.3, the base local determinant form satisfies, for every \(x\in H^1(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo},V)\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} Q^0_{\mathrm{loc},q}(x)=Q^0_{\mathrm{loc},q_W}(i_*x).\tag{6.44} \end{equation}
Under Kummer theory and the normalized Shapiro cochain map, the right side is obtained by applying the orbit operations of Lemma 6.15 to the scalar Shapiro coordinates. Thus the regular-module computation evaluates the actual base equivariant central class \(\kappa_q^0\text{,}\) including the \(m_c\)-terms; it is not merely a polynomial with the same polarization.

Proof.
The equality of classes is the pullback identity \(\kappa_q^0=(i\rtimes1)^*\kappa_{q_W}^0\) from (6.19). Pulling that identity back along a Galois graph cocycle gives equality of normalized central \(2\)-cocycles over \(G_{\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}}\text{,}\) hence (6.44) after applying the local invariant map.

For the second assertion, use the explicit representatives in (6.17), (6.18), and (6.15). Direct substitution of the normalized Shapiro cocycle gives the square and free-orbit corestriction formulas. For a half-orbit, the correction term \(m_c^g\) records exactly whether the action on a right coset of \(\langle g\rangle\) preserves or reverses the chosen orientation of its two coordinates. Thus the graph pullback is the normalized corestriction of the two-point graph cocycle (6.40); by Lemma 6.13 that two-point class is the index-two Evens norm. The full cochain calculation is written out in Lemma 6.15.
