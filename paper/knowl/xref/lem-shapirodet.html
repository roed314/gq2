Lemma 6.17. The deep half is totally singular.

Assume \(V\) is ramified and put
\begin{equation*} X_+=\Hom_{H_V}(V^\vee,U_{e+1})\subset H^1(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo},V). \end{equation*}
Then
\begin{equation*} \dim X_+=\frac12\dim H^1(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo},V), \qquad Q^0_{\mathrm{loc}}|_{X_+}=0. \end{equation*}

Proof.
We first count the contributing unit-filtration layers and then prove vanishing on the deep half by checking square, free, and involution orbits.
🔗
By Hochschild–Serre and Kummer theory,
\begin{equation*} H^1(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo},V)\cong\Hom_{H_V}(V^\vee,M_K), \end{equation*}
and \(\Hom_{H_V}(V^\vee,-)\) is exact on the unit filtration. Hilbert duality pairs the depth-\(j\) and depth-\((2e-j)\) graded pieces, and self-duality of \(V\) gives equal \(V\)-isotypic multiplicities in each pair. The only unpaired depth is \(j=e\text{;}\) by Lemma 6.10 it has trivial inertia and contains no ramified copy of \(V\text{.}\) Hence exactly one member of every contributing pair lies above \(e\text{,}\) proving the dimension statement.
🔗
By Lemma 6.14, compute the base connecting map after an \(H_V\)-split embedding into a regular module, and apply Lemma 6.15 to the complete invariant monomial expansion of \(q\circ p\text{.}\) For a class in \(X_+\) every scalar coordinate \(\alpha_r\) lies in \(U_{e+1}(K)\text{.}\) Free orbits vanish by (6.36). Square orbits vanish because \(\alpha_r^2=\alpha_r\smile(-1)\) and \(-1\in U_e\text{.}\) For an involution orbit, tame inertia has odd order, so \(K/K_0\) in (6.47) is unramified quadratic. The depth parameter is unchanged in an unramified subextension, and Lemma 6.16 makes the Evens norm vanish before corestriction. Every orbit contribution is therefore zero.
