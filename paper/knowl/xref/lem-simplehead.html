Lemma 7.1. Simple head.

There is an exact sequence of \(\F_2[C]\)-modules
\begin{equation*} 0\to T\to M\to V\to0, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} T=\rad_{\F_2[C]}M, \qquad M/T\cong V, \qquad (M^\vee)^C=0. \end{equation*}

Proof.
Since \(K/(K\cap S)\cong V\) is elementary abelian, \(R\le K\cap S\text{.}\) The displayed sequence follows. Since the quotient is simple, \(\rad M\le T\text{.}\) If a maximal submodule \(X<M\) failed to contain \(T\text{,}\) then \(X+T=M\text{,}\) so \(X\) would map onto \(V\text{.}\) Writing \(K_X\) for the inverse image of \(X\) in \(K\text{,}\) the subgroup \(K_X\) would be proper and \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\)-normal while still satisfying \(K_XS=P\text{,}\) contradicting minimality. Thus every maximal submodule contains \(T\text{.}\) The assertion about dual invariants follows because a nonzero map \(M\to\F_2\) would give a trivial simple quotient, whereas the unique simple head is \(V\text{.}\)
