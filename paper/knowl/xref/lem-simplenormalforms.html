Lemma 5.13. Normal forms for nontrivial simple modules.

Let \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) be a nontrivial simple tame \(\F_2[\notn{Cfin}{C}]\)-module. Every class in \(H^1_{A,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notnfar{Vhead}{V})\) has a unique representative supported on \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation} (a,b,c,d)\sim(0,0,c,0).\tag{5.24} \end{equation}
More precisely, the two possible inertia cases are as follows.
🔗
  1. If \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1\text{,}\) then \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=1\text{,}\) \(1+\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}\) is invertible, and
    \begin{equation*} Z^1_{A,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notnfar{Vhead}{V})=\{(a,0,c,0):a,c\in \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\},\qquad B^1_{A,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notnfar{Vhead}{V})=\{((\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}-1)v,0,0,0):v\in \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\}. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
  2. If \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^{\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}}=0\text{,}\) then \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=0\) and \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}-1=1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}\) is invertible. The wild row first forces \(d=0\text{.}\) Subtracting the coboundary of \(v=(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}-1)^{-1}b\) kills \(b\text{,}\) and the tame row then forces \(a=0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
Under the representatives (5.24), the degree-one pairing with the corresponding representative \(\lambda\in \notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee\) is
\begin{equation} (c,\lambda)\longmapsto \begin{cases} \lambda(c),&\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1,\\[1mm] \lambda((1+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1})c),&\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^{\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}}=0. \end{cases}\tag{5.25} \end{equation}
Both operators in (5.25) are invertible.

Proof.
We reduce separately in the cases of trivial inertia and vanishing inertia fixed space, and then evaluate the pairing on the resulting unique normal forms.
🔗
Because tame inertia is normal, \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^{\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}}\) is a \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-submodule; simplicity gives either \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1\) and \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=1\text{,}\) or \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^{\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}}=0\) and \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=0\text{.}\)
🔗
If \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1\text{,}\) the two rows of (5.4) reduce to \(b=0\) and \((1+\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1})d=0\text{.}\) A nonzero kernel of \(1+\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}\) would be the \(\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}\)-fixed subspace and hence a nonzero submodule, so \(1+\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}\) is invertible and \(d=0\text{.}\) The displayed descriptions of \(Z^1\) and \(B^1\) follow. The same argument shows that \(\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}-1\) is invertible, so each class has a unique representative \((0,0,c,0)\text{.}\)
🔗
If \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^{\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}}=0\text{,}\) then \(1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}\) is invertible. The wild row is \(\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}d=0\text{,}\) hence \(d=0\text{.}\) For a cocycle \((a,b,c,0)\text{,}\) subtract the coboundary of \(v=(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}-1)^{-1}b\text{.}\) The new representative has \(b=0\text{,}\) and its tame row is \(\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^{-1}(1+\notn{opT}{\mathsf T})a=0\text{,}\) so \(a=0\text{.}\) Uniqueness follows because a coboundary with zero \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}\)-coordinate has \((\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}-1)v=0\text{,}\) hence \(v=0\text{.}\)
🔗
The pairing formula is Lemma 5.14. In the ramified case \(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}\) has \(2\)-power order, so its minimal polynomial divides a power of \(X+1\text{.}\) Since \(X^2+X+1\) is coprime to \(X+1\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} 1+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}=\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^2+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}+1) \end{equation*}
is invertible. The unramified operator is the identity.
