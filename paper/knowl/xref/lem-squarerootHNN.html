Lemma 3.8. Square-root and HNN lifting.

For every \(u\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\) there is an automorphism \(\Psi_u\in\Aut(\notn{D0}{D_0})\) such that, on \(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} \bar A\longmapsto u\bar A, \qquad \bar S\longmapsto u\bar S.\tag{3.8} \end{equation}
🔗
Lean
🔗

Proof.
The proof first lifts peripheral powering across the square-root relation, then transports the lift to the free pro-\(2\) group on \(S,A\text{,}\) and finally extends it over the HNN stable letter \(\notn{Ygen}{Y}\text{.}\)
🔗
Start with \(\varphi_u\) and the conjugators from Lemma 3.7. Consider
\begin{equation*} E_\square= \left\langle P,T,A\;\middle|\;PTA^2=1\right\rangle_{\mathrm{pro}-2} \cong\langle P,A\rangle_{\mathrm{pro}-2}, \qquad C=A^2. \end{equation*}
View the words \(c_P,c_T,c_C\) in \(E_\square\) via \(P,T\text{,}\) and define
\begin{equation*} P\longmapsto\varphi_u(P), \qquad T\longmapsto\varphi_u(T), \qquad A\longmapsto c_C^{-1}A^u c_C. \end{equation*}
The defining relation is preserved, because
\begin{equation*} (c_C^{-1}A^u c_C)^2 =c_C^{-1}C^u c_C =\varphi_u(C) =(\varphi_u(P)\varphi_u(T))^{-1}. \end{equation*}
Modulo the Frattini subgroup, the free basis \(P,A\) is sent to \(u\bar P,u\bar A\text{,}\) hence to \(\bar P,\bar A\text{.}\) The pro-\(2\) Burnside basis theorem and Hopficity therefore show that this endomorphism is an automorphism; call it \(\theta_u\text{.}\)
🔗
In the free pro-\(2\) group \(F_{S,A}=\langle S,A\rangle_{\mathrm{pro}-2}\) put
\begin{equation*} P=S^3, \qquad T=S^{-3}A^{-2}. \end{equation*}
Since \(3\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\text{,}\) the pair \(P,A\) is again a free basis of \(F_{S,A}\text{,}\) and \(PTA^2=1\text{.}\) For every odd integer \(m\text{,}\) the power map \(x\mapsto x^m\) is bijective on a pro-\(2\) group, with inverse \(x\mapsto x^{m^{-1}}\) for \(m^{-1}\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\text{.}\) Comparing the unique cube roots of the two sides of (3.7) therefore gives
\begin{equation*} \theta_u(S)=c_P^{-1}S^u c_P, \qquad \theta_u(T)=c_T^{-1}T^u c_T. \end{equation*}
The relation of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\) is equivalently
\begin{equation} \notn{Ygen}{Y}^{-1}SY=T, \qquad T=S^{-3}A^{-2}.\tag{3.9} \end{equation}
Define an endomorphism of the presentation (3.9) by
\begin{equation*} \Psi_u|_E=\theta_u, \qquad \Psi_u(\notn{Ygen}{Y})=c_P^{-1}Yc_T. \end{equation*}
Then
\begin{equation*} \Psi_u(\notn{Ygen}{Y})^{-1}\Psi_u(S)\Psi_u(\notn{Ygen}{Y}) =c_T^{-1}\notn{Ygen}{Y}^{-1}S^uYc_T =c_T^{-1}T^u c_T =\Psi_u(T), \end{equation*}
so the relation is respected. On the Frattini quotient of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\text{,}\) the images of \(A,S,\notn{Ygen}{Y}\) form a triangular basis with diagonal entries \(1,1,1\text{.}\) Consequently \(\Psi_u\) is surjective and hence an automorphism. Its action on \(\bar A,\bar S\) is (3.8).
