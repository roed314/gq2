Lemma 3.8. Square-root and HNN lifting.

For every \(u\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\) there is an automorphism \(\notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}\in\Aut(\notn{D0}{D_0})\) such that, on \(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} \notn{barA}{\bar A}\longmapsto u\notn{barA}{\bar A}, \qquad \notn{barS}{\bar S}\longmapsto u\notn{barS}{\bar S}.\tag{3.8} \end{equation}

Lean
🔗

Proof.
The proof first lifts peripheral powering across the square-root relation, then transports the lift to the free pro-\(2\) group on \(\notnfar{Sgen}{S},\notn{Agen}{A}\text{,}\) and finally extends it over the HNN stable letter \(\notn{Ygen}{Y}\text{.}\)
🔗
Start with \(\notn{varphiu}{\varphi_u}\) and the conjugators from Lemma 3.7. Consider
\begin{equation*} \notn{Esq}{E_\square}= \left\langle \notn{Pperi}{P},\notn{Tperi}{T},\notn{Agen}{A}\;\middle|\;PTA^2=1\right\rangle_{\mathrm{pro}-2} \cong\langle \notn{Pperi}{P},\notn{Agen}{A}\rangle_{\mathrm{pro}-2}, \qquad \notn{Cperi}{C}=\notn{Agen}{A}^2. \end{equation*}
View the words \(\notn{cP}{c_P},\notn{cT}{c_T},\notn{cC}{c_C}\) in \(\notn{Esq}{E_\square}\) via \(\notn{Pperi}{P},\notn{Tperi}{T}\text{,}\) and define
\begin{equation*} \notn{Pperi}{P}\longmapsto\notn{varphiu}{\varphi_u}(\notn{Pperi}{P}), \qquad \notn{Tperi}{T}\longmapsto\notn{varphiu}{\varphi_u}(\notn{Tperi}{T}), \qquad \notn{Agen}{A}\longmapsto \notn{cC}{c_C}^{-1}\notn{Agen}{A}^u \notn{cC}{c_C}. \end{equation*}
The defining relation is preserved, because
\begin{equation*} (\notn{cC}{c_C}^{-1}\notn{Agen}{A}^u \notn{cC}{c_C})^2 =\notn{cC}{c_C}^{-1}\notn{Cperi}{C}^u \notn{cC}{c_C} =\notn{varphiu}{\varphi_u}(\notn{Cperi}{C}) =(\notn{varphiu}{\varphi_u}(\notn{Pperi}{P})\notn{varphiu}{\varphi_u}(\notn{Tperi}{T}))^{-1}. \end{equation*}
Modulo the Frattini subgroup, the free basis \(\notn{Pperi}{P},\notn{Agen}{A}\) is sent to \(u\bar P,u\notn{barA}{\bar A}\text{,}\) hence to \(\bar P,\notn{barA}{\bar A}\text{.}\) The pro-\(2\) Burnside basis theorem and Hopficity therefore show that this endomorphism is an automorphism; call it \(\notn{thetau}{\theta_u}\text{.}\)
🔗
In the free pro-\(2\) group \(\notn{FSA}{F_{S,A}}=\langle \notnfar{Sgen}{S},\notn{Agen}{A}\rangle_{\mathrm{pro}-2}\) put
\begin{equation*} \notn{Pperi}{P}=\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^3, \qquad \notn{Tperi}{T}=\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^{-3}\notn{Agen}{A}^{-2}. \end{equation*}
Since \(3\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\text{,}\) the pair \(\notn{Pperi}{P},\notn{Agen}{A}\) is again a free basis of \(\notn{FSA}{F_{S,A}}\text{,}\) and \(PTA^2=1\text{.}\) For every odd integer \(m\text{,}\) the power map \(x\mapsto x^m\) is bijective on a pro-\(2\) group, with inverse \(x\mapsto x^{m^{-1}}\) for \(m^{-1}\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\text{.}\) Comparing the unique cube roots of the two sides of (3.7) therefore gives
\begin{equation*} \notn{thetau}{\theta_u}(\notnfar{Sgen}{S})=\notn{cP}{c_P}^{-1}\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^u \notn{cP}{c_P}, \qquad \notn{thetau}{\theta_u}(\notn{Tperi}{T})=\notn{cT}{c_T}^{-1}\notn{Tperi}{T}^u \notn{cT}{c_T}. \end{equation*}
The relation of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\) is equivalently
\begin{equation} \notn{Ygen}{Y}^{-1}SY=\notn{Tperi}{T}, \qquad \notn{Tperi}{T}=\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^{-3}\notn{Agen}{A}^{-2}.\tag{3.9} \end{equation}
Define an endomorphism of the presentation (3.9) by
\begin{equation*} \notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}|_E=\notn{thetau}{\theta_u}, \qquad \notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}(\notn{Ygen}{Y})=\notn{cP}{c_P}^{-1}\notn{Ygen}{Y}\notn{cT}{c_T}. \end{equation*}
Then
\begin{equation*} \notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}(\notn{Ygen}{Y})^{-1}\notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}(\notnfar{Sgen}{S})\notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}(\notn{Ygen}{Y}) =\notn{cT}{c_T}^{-1}\notn{Ygen}{Y}^{-1}\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^uY\notn{cT}{c_T} =\notn{cT}{c_T}^{-1}\notn{Tperi}{T}^u \notn{cT}{c_T} =\notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}(\notn{Tperi}{T}), \end{equation*}
so the relation is respected. On the Frattini quotient of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\text{,}\) the images of \(\notn{Agen}{A},\notnfar{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}\) form a triangular basis with diagonal entries \(1,1,1\text{.}\) Consequently \(\notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}\) is surjective and hence an automorphism. Its action on \(\notn{barA}{\bar A},\notn{barS}{\bar S}\) is (3.8).
