Lemma 3.4. Standard dyadic group and its orientation.

The group \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\) is the standard rank-three, \(q=2\) Demushkin group, and \(G_{\Qtwo}(2)\) is abstractly isomorphic to \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\text{.}\) The canonical Demushkin orientation of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\) factors through \(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\) and is the character
\begin{equation} \notn{chi0}{\chi_0}(\notn{tTors}{t})=-1, \qquad \notn{chi0}{\chi_0}(\notn{barS}{\bar S})=1, \qquad \notn{chi0}{\chi_0}(\notn{barY}{\bar Y})=\notn{eta}{\eta}.\tag{3.5} \end{equation}
Equivalently,
\begin{equation*} \notn{chi0}{\chi_0}(\notn{barA}{\bar A})=-1, \qquad \notn{chi0}{\chi_0}(\notn{barS}{\bar S})=1, \qquad \notn{chi0}{\chi_0}(\notn{barY}{\bar Y})=(-3)^{-1}. \end{equation*}

🔗

Proof.
The presentation (3.1) is the odd-rank dyadic case of Labute’s classification, with parameter \(q=2\) and secondary exponent \(4\text{;}\) see [2, Theorem 8]. The canonical character for the odd-rank normal form is computed in [2, Theorem 4, case (2)]: for the relation \(\notnfar{x1}{x_1}^2x_2^{2^f}[x_2,x_3]\) it takes the values \(-1,1,(1-2^f)^{-1}\) on \(\notnfar{x1}{x_1},x_2,x_3\text{.}\) With \(f=2\) and the standing commutator convention, this gives \(-1,1,(1-4)^{-1}=(-3)^{-1}\) on \(\notn{Agen}{A},\notn{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}\text{,}\) respectively. The maximal pro-\(2\) Galois group of \(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}\) is the rank-three local Demushkin group with these parameters, so the abstract isomorphism follows from the same classification.
