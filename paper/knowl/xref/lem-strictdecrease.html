Lemma 8.16. Strict decrease of recursive targets.

Every target occurring on the right sides of (8.13), (8.16), (8.17), and (8.22) has marked \(2\)-kernel strictly smaller than \(|\notnfar{LY}{L_Y}|\text{.}\) More precisely,
\begin{equation*} |L_C|=\frac{|\notnfar{LY}{L_Y}|}{|\notn{Kker}{K}|}<|\notnfar{LY}{L_Y}|, \end{equation*}
\begin{equation} 2|J\cap L_B|\le |L_B|<|\notnfar{LY}{L_Y}|\tag{8.23} \end{equation}
for a proper scalar-cover stratum, and
\begin{equation} |L_{C_\zeta}|=2|L_C| =\frac{2|\notnfar{LY}{L_Y}|}{|\notn{Kker}{K}|}<|\notnfar{LY}{L_Y}|.\tag{8.24} \end{equation}
The proper-image bound in the elementary stage is (8.14).

Proof.
The first two inequalities follow from \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}<\notn{Kker}{K}\) and from the fact that a proper subgroup of the elementary abelian group \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}=\notn{Kker}{K}/\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\) has index at least two. A scalar double cover of a proper \(J<\notnfar{Bmid}{B}\) has marked kernel \(2|J\cap L_B|\text{,}\) giving (8.23). Since the non-scalar simple head has dimension at least two, \(|\notn{Kker}{K}|\ge4\text{;}\) hence a phase cover has marked kernel as in (8.24).
