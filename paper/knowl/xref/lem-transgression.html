Lemma 6.21. Determinant transgression relative to the fixed equivariant class.

Let \(q\) be a nonsingular \(C\)-invariant quadratic form on \(V\text{,}\) and assume that a zero-section-normalized equivariant class
\begin{equation*} \kappa_q^0\in H^2(V\rtimes C,\F_2) \end{equation*}
restricting to \(q\) on \(V\) has been fixed. Let
\begin{equation*} 1\to V\to G_\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\to C\to1 \end{equation*}
have extension class \(\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\in H^2(C,V)\text{.}\) Relative to the fixed equivariant lift \(\kappa_q^0\text{,}\) the obstruction to extending the fibre class over \(G_\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\) is
\begin{equation} d_2(q)=b_q^\flat{}_*\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\in H^2(C,V^\vee).\tag{6.58} \end{equation}
Consequently, if \(q\) is the restriction of an actual class in \(H^2(G_\notnfar{eta}{\eta},\F_2)\text{,}\) then \(\notnfar{eta}{\eta}=0\) and \(G_\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\cong V\rtimes C\) over \(C\text{.}\)

Proof.
We choose sections for the extension, compare the induced automorphisms of the fibre extension, and identify their discrepancy with the transgressed class \(b_q^\flat{}_*(\notnfar{eta}{\eta})\text{.}\)
🔗
Let
\begin{equation*} 1\longrightarrow\F_2\longrightarrow E_q\longrightarrow V\longrightarrow1 \end{equation*}
be the central extension represented by \(\kappa_q^0|_V\text{.}\) Central automorphisms of \(E_q\) inducing the identity on \(V\) are canonically \(V^\vee\text{.}\) If \(\widehat v\) is a lift of \(v\in V\text{,}\) inner conjugation by \(\widehat v\) is the central automorphism
\begin{equation} w\longmapsto b_q(v,w)=b_q^\flat(v)(w).\tag{6.59} \end{equation}
Choose the zero-section-normalized representative of \(\kappa_q^0\text{.}\) Its preimage of \(C\) is split, so conjugation by the chosen lifts of \(c\in C\) supplies coherent automorphisms
\begin{equation*} \alpha_c\in\Aut(E_q), \qquad \alpha_c\alpha_d=\alpha_{cd}, \end{equation*}
lifting the prescribed \(C\)-action on \(V\text{.}\)
🔗
Choose a normalized section \(s:C\to G_\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\) and write
\begin{equation} s(c)s(d)=\notnfar{eta}{\eta}(c,d)s(cd).\tag{6.60} \end{equation}
In a putative central extension of \(G_\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\) restricting to \(E_q\text{,}\) lifts of the section elements would satisfy
\begin{equation*} \widehat s(c)\widehat s(d) =z(c,d)\widehat{\notnfar{eta}{\eta}(c,d)}\widehat s(cd) \end{equation*}
for a scalar factor \(z(c,d)\in\F_2\text{.}\) Compare the induced automorphisms of \(E_q\text{.}\) The left side induces \(\alpha_c\alpha_d=\alpha_{cd}\text{,}\) whereas the right side induces
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{Int}(\widehat{\notnfar{eta}{\eta}(c,d)})\alpha_{cd}. \end{equation*}
By (6.59), their discrepancy is the \(V^\vee\)-valued normalized \(2\)-cocycle
\begin{equation} (c,d)\longmapsto b_q^\flat(\notnfar{eta}{\eta}(c,d)).\tag{6.61} \end{equation}
Changing the section \(s\) changes \(\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\) by a coboundary and changes (6.61) by the corresponding \(V^\vee\)-coboundary. Changing the coherent lifts \(\alpha_c\) by central automorphisms \(\gamma(c)\in V^\vee\) changes the discrepancy by \(d\gamma\text{.}\) Hence the cohomology class of the obstruction is exactly \(b_q^\flat{}_*[\notnfar{eta}{\eta}]\text{.}\) This is the \(d_2^{0,2}\) differential after the intrinsic equivariance obstruction has been trivialized by the fixed split class \(\kappa_q^0\text{,}\) proving (6.58).
🔗
If an actual class on \(G_\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\) restricts to \(q\text{,}\) the obstruction vanishes. Since \(q\) is nonsingular, \(b_q^\flat:V\xrightarrow{\sim}V^\vee\) is an isomorphism of \(C\)-modules. Therefore \([\notnfar{eta}{\eta}]=0\text{,}\) so the extension splits over \(C\text{.}\)
