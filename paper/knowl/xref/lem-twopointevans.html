Lemma 6.13. Universal two-point normalization and the index-two Evens norm.

Let
\begin{equation*} J=\langle s\rangle\cong C_2, \qquad E_2=\F_2e_1\oplus\F_2e_s, \qquad s(\notnfar{x1}{x_1},x_s)=(x_s,\notnfar{x1}{x_1}), \end{equation*}
and put
\begin{equation*} q_{\mathrm{hyp}}(\notnfar{x1}{x_1},x_s)=x_1x_s, \qquad f_J(x,y)=x_1y_s, \qquad m_1=0, \qquad m_s(y)=y_1y_s. \end{equation*}
Then
\begin{equation} \kappa_J((x,c),(y,d))=f_J(x,cy)+m_c(y)\tag{6.37} \end{equation}
is a normalized \(2\)-cocycle on \(E_2\rtimes J\text{.}\) It restricts to the quadratic map \(q_{\mathrm{hyp}}\) on \(E_2\) and to zero on the zero section \(J\text{.}\) Its fibre extension is the dihedral group \(D_8\text{:}\) the two coordinate lifts are involutions, their commutator is the central involution, and their product has square equal to that involution. Moreover
\begin{equation} [\kappa_J]=N_{E_2}^{E_2\rtimes J,\mathrm{Ev}}(e_1^\vee) \quad\text{in }H^2(E_2\rtimes J,\F_2).\tag{6.38} \end{equation}
🔗
Now let \(N\triangleleft G\) have index \(2\text{,}\) identify \(G/N\) with \(J\text{,}\) and let \(\alpha\in Z^1(N,\F_2)\text{.}\) Choose lifts \(\widetilde1=1\) and \(\widetilde s\in G\text{,}\) and define the normalized Shapiro cocycle
\begin{equation} b(\gamma)_u= \alpha\!\left(\widetilde u^{-1}\gamma \widetilde{\bar\gamma^{-1}u}\right), \qquad u\in J.\tag{6.39} \end{equation}
This is the degree-one normalized Shapiro map (A.12). The graph pullback of (6.37) is
\begin{equation} \nu_\alpha(\gamma,\eta) =b(\gamma)_1b(\eta)_{\bar\gamma^{-1}s} +\varepsilon(\bar\gamma)b(\eta)_1b(\eta)_s,\tag{6.40} \end{equation}
where \(\varepsilon(1)=0\) and \(\varepsilon(s)=1\text{.}\) Its class is the index-two Evens norm:
\begin{equation} [\nu_\alpha]=N_N^{G,\mathrm{Ev}}([\alpha])\in H^2(G,\F_2).\tag{6.41} \end{equation}
Equivalently, the normalization is
\begin{equation*} N_N^G(1+[\alpha]) =1+\operatorname{cor}_N^G[\alpha] +N_N^{G,\mathrm{Ev}}([\alpha]). \end{equation*}
🔗
Finally, if \(g\in H\) is an involution, \(M=\F_2[H]\text{,}\) \(J=\langle g\rangle\text{,}\) and \(\pi:M\to\F_2[J]\) selects the coordinates indexed by \(1\) and \(g\text{,}\) then for every right-coset transversal \(\mathcal R\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} [\kappa_g^{\mathcal R}] =\operatorname{cor}_{M\rtimes J}^{M\rtimes H} (\pi\rtimes1)^*[\kappa_J].\tag{6.42} \end{equation}

Proof.
We verify the universal cocycle and its fibre extension, identify its class with the Evens norm, pull it back to a general index-two inclusion, and then compare the regular-module half-orbit transfer.
🔗
Expanding \(m_s\) on the two coordinates gives the two identities required in Lemma 6.1: for \(v,w\in E_2\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} m_s(v+w)+m_s(v)+m_s(w)=f_J(sv,sw)+f_J(v,w), \qquad m_s(sv)+m_s(v)=0. \end{equation*}
Thus (6.37) is a normalized cocycle. Its square map is \(x_1x_s\text{,}\) and it vanishes when both fibre coordinates are zero. If \(\widetilde e_1,\widetilde e_s\) are the coordinate lifts, then \(\widetilde e_1^2=\widetilde e_s^2=1\text{,}\) their commutator is the central generator, and \((\widetilde e_1\widetilde e_s)^2\) is that generator; hence the fibre extension is \(D_8\text{.}\)
🔗
For the swap module,
\begin{equation} H^1(J,E_2^\vee)=\frac{\ker(1+s)}{\operatorname{im}(1+s)}=0.\tag{6.43} \end{equation}
The low-degree exact sequence (6.22) therefore says that a class on \(E_2\rtimes J\) is determined by its restriction to \(E_2\) and its restriction to the zero section. Mackey’s formula for the standard Evens norm gives
\begin{equation*} \res_{E_2} N_{E_2}^{E_2\rtimes J,\mathrm{Ev}}(e_1^\vee)=e_1^\vee e_s^\vee, \qquad \res_J N_{E_2}^{E_2\rtimes J,\mathrm{Ev}}(e_1^\vee)=0. \end{equation*}
These are exactly the two restrictions of \([\kappa_J]\text{,}\) proving (6.38). Equivalently, this class is the degree-two component of the relative Stiefel–Whitney class \(w(\operatorname{Ind}\ell)/w(\operatorname{Ind}1)\) under the Evens–Kahn normalization; see [9], [10], [11].
🔗
For the general index-two inclusion, the graph
\begin{equation*} \Phi_\alpha:G\longrightarrow E_2\rtimes J, \qquad \gamma\longmapsto(b(\gamma),\bar\gamma) \end{equation*}
has inverse image of \(E_2\) equal to \(N\text{,}\) and the pullback of \(e_1^\vee\) to \(N\) is \(\alpha\text{.}\) Naturality of the Evens norm in this pullback square and (6.38) give (6.41). Direct substitution of (6.39) into (6.37) gives (6.40) term by term. This is also the degree-two term in Evens’s normalized bar construction [9, §§4--5]. If \(\widetilde s\) is replaced by \(\widetilde s a\) with \(a\in N\text{,}\) then (A.25) gives the exact change of the Shapiro cocycle, and (A.36) gives the resulting normalized \(1\)-cochain whose boundary is the change in (6.40). Thus the lift dependence is controlled at cochain level, not only in cohomology.
🔗
For (6.42), Mackey restriction of the right side to \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\) is
\begin{equation*} \sum_{u\in H/J}x_u x_{ug}, \end{equation*}
and its zero-section restriction is zero. The explicit class \([\kappa_g^{\mathcal R}]\) has the same two restrictions. Moreover \(H^1(H,\notnfar{Mmid}{M}^\vee)=0\) by Shapiro’s lemma, since \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}^\vee\) is coinduced. The same low-degree uniqueness argument proves equality. More precisely, (A.30) identifies the two representatives as normalized cochains for the chosen transversal, and (A.32) displays the normalized \(1\)-cochain whose boundary is the change under an arbitrary new transversal.
