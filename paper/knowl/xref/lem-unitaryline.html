Lemma 6.7. Invariant quadratic forms on a hermitian line.

Let \(F/F_0\) be a quadratic extension of finite fields of characteristic \(2\text{,}\) write \(x\mapsto x^*\) for its involution, and let \(\mathcal U_1=\{u\in F^\times:uu^*=1\}\text{.}\) Let \(W\) be a one-dimensional \(F\)-space. Every nonsingular \(\mathcal U_1\)-invariant quadratic form \(Q:W\to\F_2\) is uniquely of the form
\begin{equation*} Q(x)=\Tr_{F_0/\F_2}(a xx^*) \qquad (a\in F_0^\times). \end{equation*}

Proof.
Choose a generator \(u_0\) of the cyclic group \(\mathcal U_1\text{.}\) Since \(2^m\equiv-1\pmod{2^m+1}\) and no smaller positive power of \(2\) is \(1\) modulo \(2^m+1\text{,}\) the element \(u_0\) has degree \(2m\) over \(\F_2\text{;}\) hence \(\F_2[u_0]=F\text{.}\) Invariance gives
\begin{equation*} b_Q(dx,y)=b_Q(x,d^*y)\qquad(d\in F). \end{equation*}
Putting \(\ell(y)=b_Q(1,y)\text{,}\) we obtain \(b_Q(x,y)=\ell(x^*y)\text{.}\) Write \(\ell(z)=\Tr_{F/\F_2}(cz)\) for the unique \(c\in F\text{.}\) Symmetry of \(b_Q\) forces \(c=c^*\text{,}\) hence \(c\in F_0\text{,}\) and therefore
\begin{equation*} b_Q(x,y)=\Tr_{F_0/\F_2} \bigl(c(xy^*+x^*y)\bigr). \end{equation*}
Conversely these forms are alternating, and they are nonsingular exactly when \(c\ne0\text{.}\) The quadratic form \(q_c(x)=\Tr_{F_0/\F_2}(cxx^*)\) has this polarization. Hence \(Q-q_c\) is a \(\mathcal U_1\)-invariant linear form. Writing such a form as \(x\mapsto\Tr_{F/\F_2}(bx)\text{,}\) invariance under \(u_0\) gives \(b(u_0-1)=0\text{,}\) so \(b=0\text{.}\) Thus \(Q=q_c\text{,}\) uniquely, and nonsingularity gives \(c\in F_0^\times\text{.}\)
