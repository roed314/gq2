Lemma 6.6. Wall doubling.

Let \((\notnfar{Vhead}{V},q)\) be nonsingular over \(\F_2\text{,}\) with polar form \(\notnfar{bq}{b_q}\text{,}\) and let \(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}\in O(\notnfar{Vhead}{V},q)\) have \(2\)-power order. Put
\begin{equation*} q_{\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}}(x)=q(x)+\notnfar{bq}{b_q}(x,\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}x). \end{equation*}
Then \(q_{\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}}\) is nonsingular and
\begin{equation} \notn{Arf}{\Arf}(q_{\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}})=\notn{Arf}{\Arf}(q)+\operatorname{rank}(1+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U})\pmod2.\tag{6.28} \end{equation}

Proof.
Let \(N=1+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}\text{,}\) \(R=NV\text{,}\) and \(K=\ker N\text{.}\) Since \(q(\mathsf Ux)=q(x)\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \notnfar{bq}{b_q}(x,\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}x)=\notnfar{bq}{b_q}(x,\mathsf Ux)=q(Nx), \end{equation*}
so \(q_{\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}}(x)=q(x)+q(Nx)\text{.}\) Define the nondegenerate Wall form on \(R\) by
\begin{equation*} \notn{omgu}{\omega}(Nx,u)=\notnfar{bq}{b_q}(x,u). \end{equation*}
In \((\notnfar{Vhead}{V},q_{\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}})\perp(\notnfar{Vhead}{V},q)\text{,}\) the diagonal \(\{(k,k):k\in K\}\) is totally singular. Its orthogonal quotient is \(R\oplus R\) with quadratic form
\begin{equation*} \Phi_\notn{omgu}{\omega}(v,u)=\notn{omgu}{\omega}(v,v)+\notn{omgu}{\omega}(u,u)+\notn{omgu}{\omega}(v,u). \end{equation*}
If \(v\mapsto\notn{omgu}{\omega}(v,v)\) is nonzero, split an anisotropic plane from \(\Phi_\omega\text{;}\) if \(\omega\) is alternating, split a symplectic plane from \(R\text{,}\) which contributes a hyperbolic four-space. Induction gives \(\notn{Arf}{\Arf}(\Phi_\omega)=\dim R\pmod2\text{,}\) proving (6.28).
