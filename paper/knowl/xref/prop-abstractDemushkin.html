Proposition 3.11. Nielsen transform to the pro-\(2\) boundary.

For any pro-\(2\) group with topological generators \(a,s,y\) and unique defining relation
\begin{equation} a^2s^4[s,y]=1,\tag{3.15} \end{equation}
the Nielsen change
\begin{equation} \notn{sigma}{\sigma}=s,\qquad \notnfar{x1}{x_1}=y,\qquad \notn{x0}{x_0}=s^{-2}a^{-1}\tag{3.16} \end{equation}
changes the closed normal closure of the relator to that of
\begin{equation} \notn{x0}{x_0}^{\notn{sigma}{\sigma}^2}\notn{x0}{x_0}[\notnfar{x1}{x_1},\notn{sigma}{\sigma}].\tag{3.17} \end{equation}
Consequently Proposition 1.1 identifies \(D=G_{\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}}(2)\) abstractly with \(\notn{Pi}{\Pi}\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗

Proof.
Under (3.16), one has \(a=\notn{x0}{x_0}^{-1}\notn{sigma}{\sigma}^{-2}\) and therefore
\begin{equation*} a^2s^4=\notn{x0}{x_0}^{-1}(\notn{x0}{x_0}^{-1})^{\notn{sigma}{\sigma}^2}. \end{equation*}
Writing \(P=\notn{x0}{x_0}^{\notn{sigma}{\sigma}^2}\notn{x0}{x_0}\) and \(Q=[\notnfar{x1}{x_1},\notn{sigma}{\sigma}]\text{,}\) the inverse of the standard relator is \(QP\text{.}\) The words \(QP\) and \(PQ\) are cyclic conjugates, so they have the same closed normal closure. This proves the assertion.
