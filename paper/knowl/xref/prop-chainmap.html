Proposition 5.10. The Fox–Heisenberg chain map.

Under the canonical identifications
\begin{equation*} \Hom((\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee)^2,\F_2)\cong \notn{Amod}{A}^2, \qquad \Hom((\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee)^4,\F_2)\cong \notn{Amod}{A}^4, \qquad \Hom(\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee,\F_2)\cong \notn{Amod}{A}, \end{equation*}
define
\begin{align} \notn{Dfox}{D}^0_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}(a)&=(a,a),\tag{5.14}\\ \langle \notn{Dfox}{D}^1_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}(x),y\rangle&=\mathcal B_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}(x,y),\tag{5.15}\\ \notn{Dfox}{D}^2_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}(u_{\mathrm t},u_{\mathrm w})&=u_{\mathrm t}+u_{\mathrm w}.\tag{5.16} \end{align}
Then \(D_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}=(\notn{Dfox}{D}^0,\notn{Dfox}{D}^1,\notn{Dfox}{D}^2)\) is a natural chain map
\begin{equation} D_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}:C_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}^\bullet \longrightarrow \Hom_{\F_2}(C_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee}^\bullet,\F_2)[-2].\tag{5.17} \end{equation}
More explicitly,
\begin{align} \notn{Dfox}{D}^1_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}d^0_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}} &=(d^1_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee})^\vee \notn{Dfox}{D}^0_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}},\tag{5.18}\\ \notn{Dfox}{D}^2_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}d^1_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}} &=(d^0_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee})^\vee \notn{Dfox}{D}^1_{\rho,\notn{Amod}{A}}.\tag{5.19} \end{align}

Proof.
The endpoint maps are induced by the scalar trace \(\operatorname{tr}(y_{\mathrm t},y_{\mathrm w})=y_{\mathrm t}+y_{\mathrm w}\text{.}\) Equations (5.12) and (5.13), proved by the direct finite-word calculation of Lemma 5.7, are exactly (5.18) and (5.19), respectively. Strict naturality in \(\notn{Amod}{A}\) is Lemma 5.6, Proposition 5.8. Hence (5.17) is a natural chain map in all three degrees.
