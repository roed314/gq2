Proposition 3.14. Fully marked tame and pro-\(2\) quotients.

Let
\begin{equation*} \nu_{\mathrm t}:\notnfar{TA}{\TA}\twoheadrightarrow\mathbb{Z}_2, \qquad \nu_{\mathrm t}(\notn{sigma}{\sigma})=1, \quad \nu_{\mathrm t}(\notn{tau}{\tau})=0, \end{equation*}
and equip \(\notn{Pi}{\Pi}\) with (3.14). For each \(\notn{GammaSrc}{\Gamma}\in\{\notn{GA}{\GA},\notn{GQ}{\GQ}\}\text{,}\) the tame quotient map and the maximal pro-\(2\) quotient map may be chosen so that their composites with \(\nu_{\mathrm t}\) and \(\notn{nu2}{\nu_2}\) are the same natural unramified character
\begin{equation*} \nu_{\Gamma}:\notn{GammaSrc}{\Gamma}\twoheadrightarrow\mathbb{Z}_2. \end{equation*}
The compatibility therefore holds after passage to every finite quotient \(C_{2^m}\text{,}\) not only modulo \(2\text{.}\)
