Proposition 2.3. Epimorphic finite-quotient semantics.

For every finite group \(G\text{,}\) epimorphisms \(\notn{GA}{\GA}\twoheadrightarrow G\) are in natural bijection with generating quadruples \((\notn{sigma}{\sigma},\notn{tau}{\tau},\notn{x0}{x_0},\notn{x1}{x_1})\in G^4\) satisfying (1.5), (1.6), and \(\notn{x0}{x_0},\notn{x1}{x_1}\in O_2(G)\text{.}\)

Proof.
An epimorphism sends \(\notn{WA}{\WA}\) to a normal \(2\)-subgroup of \(G\text{,}\) hence into \(O_2(G)\text{.}\) Conversely, a generating quadruple with \(\notn{x0}{x_0},\notn{x1}{x_1}\in O_2(G)\) has normal \(2\)-group closure and defines an admissible quotient in \(\mathcal Q_A\text{.}\)
