Proposition 8.11. Fourier inversion for the final Frattini obstruction.

Assume \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\ne1\) and put
\begin{equation*} z_R=|\notn{Z1Gr}{Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}}(\notnfar{Rcent}{R})| =2^{2\dim \notnfar{Rcent}{R}+\dim\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_R}}. \end{equation*}
For \(\lambda\in\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_R}\text{,}\) let \(m_{\Gamma,\lambda}(\notnfar{Bmid}{B})\) be the number of exact-image maps to \(\notnfar{Bmid}{B}\) for which the scalar pushout \(\lambda_*o_R\) vanishes. Then \(m_{\Gamma,0}(\notnfar{Bmid}{B})=\notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}(\notnfar{Bmid}{B})\) and
\begin{equation} \boxed{ \notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y})=\frac{z_R}{|\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_R}|} \sum_{\lambda\in\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_R}} \bigl(2m_{\Gamma,\lambda}(\notnfar{Bmid}{B})-\notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}(\notnfar{Bmid}{B})\bigr).}\tag{8.15} \end{equation}

Proof.
For a fixed exact-image map to \(\notnfar{Bmid}{B}\text{,}\) the set of \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\)-lifts, when nonempty, is a torsor of size \(z_R\text{.}\) Every such lift is surjective onto \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\text{:}\) if its image \(J\) maps onto \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}/\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\text{,}\) then \((J\cap \notn{Kker}{K})\notnfar{Rcent}{R}=\notn{Kker}{K}\text{,}\) and \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}=\notn{Frattini}{\Phi}(\notn{Kker}{K})\) forces \(J\cap \notn{Kker}{K}=\notn{Kker}{K}\text{,}\) hence \(J=\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\text{.}\) Fourier inversion (8.2) on the full \(\notnfar{Rcent}{R}\)-valued obstruction, followed by the common lift multiplicity \(z_R\text{,}\) gives (8.15).
