Proposition 8.11. Fourier inversion for the final Frattini obstruction.

Assume \(R\ne1\) and put
\begin{equation*} z_R=|Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}(R)| =2^{2\dim R+\dim\mathcal X_R}. \end{equation*}
For \(\lambda\in\mathcal X_R\text{,}\) let \(m_{\Gamma,\lambda}(B)\) be the number of exact-image maps to \(B\) for which the scalar pushout \(\lambda_*o_R\) vanishes. Then \(m_{\Gamma,0}(B)=\notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}(B)\) and
\begin{equation} \boxed{ \notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y})=\frac{z_R}{|\mathcal X_R|} \sum_{\lambda\in\mathcal X_R} \bigl(2m_{\Gamma,\lambda}(B)-\notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}(B)\bigr).}\tag{8.15} \end{equation}
🔗
🔗

Proof.
For a fixed exact-image map to \(B\text{,}\) the set of \(R\)-lifts, when nonempty, is a torsor of size \(z_R\text{.}\) Every such lift is surjective onto \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\text{:}\) if its image \(J\) maps onto \(\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}/R\text{,}\) then \((J\cap K)R=K\text{,}\) and \(R=\Phi(K)\) forces \(J\cap K=K\text{,}\) hence \(J=\notnfar{Ytarget}{Y}\text{.}\) Fourier inversion (8.2) on the full \(R\)-valued obstruction, followed by the common lift multiplicity \(z_R\text{,}\) gives (8.15).
