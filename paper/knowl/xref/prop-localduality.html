Proposition 5.16. Local lifting duality.

For every elementary \(\F_2[\notn{Cfin}{C}]\)-module \(\notn{Amod}{A}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} H^2_{F,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notn{Amod}{A})^\vee\cong (\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee)^\notn{Cfin}{C}, \qquad \dim Z^1_{F,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notn{Amod}{A})=2\dim \notn{Amod}{A}+\dim(\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee)^\notn{Cfin}{C}.\tag{5.28} \end{equation}
The local cup product and invariant map give perfect pairings
\begin{equation*} H^i_{F,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notn{Amod}{A})\times H^{2-i}_{F,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee)\to\F_2. \end{equation*}

Proof.
This is local Tate duality, with the mod-\(2\) cyclotomic twist trivial, together with the local Euler–Poincaré formula for \(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}\text{;}\) see [1, Chapter VII, Sections 2--3]. Since the action factors through \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\text{,}\) the invariant space is \((\notn{Amod}{A}^\vee)^\notn{Cfin}{C}\text{.}\) Finally
\begin{equation*} \dim Z^1=\dim H^1+\dim B^1 =\dim H^1+\dim \notn{Amod}{A}-\dim \notn{Amod}{A}^\notn{Cfin}{C}, \end{equation*}
and the Euler characteristic gives the stated expression.
