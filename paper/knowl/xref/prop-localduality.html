Proposition 5.16. Local lifting duality.

For every elementary \(\F_2[C]\)-module \(A\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} H^2_{F,\rho}(A)^\vee\cong (A^\vee)^C, \qquad \dim Z^1_{F,\rho}(A)=2\dim A+\dim(A^\vee)^C.\tag{5.28} \end{equation}
The local cup product and invariant map give perfect pairings
\begin{equation*} H^i_{F,\rho}(A)\times H^{2-i}_{F,\rho}(A^\vee)\to\F_2. \end{equation*}

Proof.
This is local Tate duality, with the mod-\(2\) cyclotomic twist trivial, together with the local Euler–Poincar\’e formula for \(\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}\text{;}\) see [1, Chapter VII, Sections 2--3]. Since the action factors through \(C\text{,}\) the invariant space is \((A^\vee)^C\text{.}\) Finally
\begin{equation*} \dim Z^1=\dim H^1+\dim B^1 =\dim H^1+\dim A-\dim A^C, \end{equation*}
and the Euler characteristic gives the stated expression.
