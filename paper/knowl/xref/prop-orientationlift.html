Proposition 3.9. Lifting the orientation-preserving abelian automorphisms.

The natural map
\begin{equation} \Aut(\notn{D0}{D_0})\longrightarrow\Aut(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}},\notn{chi0}{\chi_0})\tag{3.10} \end{equation}
is surjective. More explicitly, every element of \(\Aut(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}},\notn{chi0}{\chi_0})\) has the unique form
\begin{equation} \alpha_{u,b}: \quad \notn{tTors}{t}\longmapsto \notn{tTors}{t}, \qquad \bar S\longmapsto u\bar S, \qquad \bar Y\longmapsto\bar Y+b\bar S,\tag{3.11} \end{equation}
with \(u\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\) and \(b\in\mathbb{Z}_2\text{,}\) and every \(\alpha_{u,b}\) lifts to \(\Aut(\notn{D0}{D_0})\text{.}\)

Proof.
We first determine the orientation-preserving automorphisms of the abelianization, then realize their unit-scaling part, and finally realize the remaining shear.
🔗
Because \(v_2(\notnfar{eta}{\eta}-1)=2\text{,}\) the element \(\notnfar{eta}{\eta}\) topologically generates \(1+4\mathbb{Z}_2\text{.}\) From (3.4) and (3.5) one obtains
\begin{equation*} \ker\notn{chi0}{\chi_0}=\mathbb{Z}_2\bar S. \end{equation*}
The torsion subgroup of \(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\) is the order-two group generated by \(\notn{tTors}{t}\text{.}\) Hence an orientation-preserving automorphism fixes \(\notn{tTors}{t}\text{,}\) sends \(\bar S\) to \(u\bar S\) for a unique unit \(u\text{,}\) and sends \(\bar Y\) to \(\bar Y+b\bar S\) for a unique \(b\text{.}\) Conversely, every map (3.11) is an orientation-preserving automorphism of \(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\text{.}\)
🔗
The automorphism \(\Psi_u\) of Lemma 3.8 sends \(\bar A,\bar S\) to \(u\bar A,u\bar S\text{,}\) so it fixes \(\notn{tTors}{t}=\bar A+2\bar S\) and sends \(\bar S\) to \(u\bar S\text{.}\) The canonical orientation is the action on the dualizing module and is therefore preserved by every automorphism of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\text{.}\) Thus \(\Psi_u\) induces \(\alpha_{u,b(u)}\) for some \(b(u)\in\mathbb{Z}_2\text{.}\)
🔗
For \(b\in\mathbb{Z}_2\text{,}\) define
\begin{equation} \Theta_b(A)=A^{S^b}, \qquad \Theta_b(S)=S, \qquad \Theta_b(\notn{Ygen}{Y})=YS^b,\tag{3.12} \end{equation}
Here \(A^{S^b}=(S^b)^{-1}AS^b\) is conjugation, not the product \(AS^b\text{.}\) This is an automorphism of the free pro-\(2\) group on \(A,S,\notn{Ygen}{Y}\text{,}\) with inverse \(\Theta_{-b}\text{.}\) Moreover
\begin{equation*} [S,YS^b]=[S,\notn{Ygen}{Y}]^{S^b}, \end{equation*}
and therefore
\begin{equation*} \Theta_b(A^2S^4[S,\notn{Ygen}{Y}]) =(A^2)^{S^b}S^4[S,\notn{Ygen}{Y}]^{S^b} =(A^2S^4[S,\notn{Ygen}{Y}])^{S^b}. \end{equation*}
It therefore descends to an automorphism of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\text{,}\) inducing \(\alpha_{1,b}\text{.}\) The composite \(\Theta_{b-b(u)}\circ\Psi_u\) induces \(\alpha_{u,b}\text{,}\) proving surjectivity.
