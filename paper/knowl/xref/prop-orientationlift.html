Proposition 3.9. Lifting the orientation-preserving abelian automorphisms.

The natural map
\begin{equation} \Aut(\notn{D0}{D_0})\longrightarrow\Aut(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}},\notn{chi0}{\chi_0})\tag{3.10} \end{equation}
is surjective. More explicitly, every element of \(\Aut(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}},\notn{chi0}{\chi_0})\) has the unique form
\begin{equation} \notn{alphaub}{\alpha_{u,b}}: \quad \notn{tTors}{t}\longmapsto \notn{tTors}{t}, \qquad \notn{barS}{\bar S}\longmapsto u\notn{barS}{\bar S}, \qquad \notn{barY}{\bar Y}\longmapsto\notn{barY}{\bar Y}+b\notn{barS}{\bar S},\tag{3.11} \end{equation}
with \(u\in\mathbb{Z}_2^\times\) and \(b\in\mathbb{Z}_2\text{,}\) and every \(\notn{alphaub}{\alpha_{u,b}}\) lifts to \(\Aut(\notn{D0}{D_0})\text{.}\)

Proof.
We first determine the orientation-preserving automorphisms of the abelianization, then realize their unit-scaling part, and finally realize the remaining shear.
🔗
Because \(v_2(\notn{eta}{\eta}-1)=2\text{,}\) the element \(\notn{eta}{\eta}\) topologically generates \(1+4\mathbb{Z}_2\text{.}\) From (3.4) and (3.5) one obtains
\begin{equation*} \ker\notn{chi0}{\chi_0}=\mathbb{Z}_2\notn{barS}{\bar S}. \end{equation*}
The torsion subgroup of \(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\) is the order-two group generated by \(\notn{tTors}{t}\text{.}\) Hence an orientation-preserving automorphism fixes \(\notn{tTors}{t}\text{,}\) sends \(\notn{barS}{\bar S}\) to \(u\notn{barS}{\bar S}\) for a unique unit \(u\text{,}\) and sends \(\notn{barY}{\bar Y}\) to \(\notn{barY}{\bar Y}+b\notn{barS}{\bar S}\) for a unique \(b\text{.}\) Conversely, every map (3.11) is an orientation-preserving automorphism of \(\notn{Bab}{B_{\mathrm{ab}}}\text{.}\)
🔗
In more detail: the fact that the coefficient \(u\) is a unit is not an extra assumption in the classification. Applying the same row extraction to an automorphism and to its inverse produces coefficients \(u\) and \(u'\) with \(uu'=1\text{.}\) Preservation of the order-two element \(t=\bar A+2\bar S\) then forces the \(\bar A\)-row once the \(\bar S\)-row is known.
🔗
The automorphism \(\notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}\) of Lemma 3.8 sends \(\notn{barA}{\bar A},\notn{barS}{\bar S}\) to \(u\notn{barA}{\bar A},u\notn{barS}{\bar S}\text{,}\) so it fixes \(\notn{tTors}{t}=\notn{barA}{\bar A}+2\notn{barS}{\bar S}\) and sends \(\notn{barS}{\bar S}\) to \(u\notn{barS}{\bar S}\text{.}\) The canonical orientation is the action on the dualizing module and is therefore preserved by every automorphism of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\text{.}\) Thus \(\notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}\) induces \(\alpha_{u,b(u)}\) for some \(b(u)\in\mathbb{Z}_2\text{.}\)
🔗
For the lift, the formal proof makes a small coordinate correction explicit. If \(\Psi_u\) sends \(\bar Y\) to \(\bar Y+c(u)\bar S\text{,}\) then composing with a shear of parameter \(b'\) contributes \(ub'\text{,}\) not \(b'\text{,}\) after the unit scaling. Thus one takes \(b'=(b-c(u))u^{-1}\text{.}\) The inverse shear has parameter \(-b'\text{,}\) so this construction gives an automorphism rather than only an endomorphism.
🔗
For \(b\in\mathbb{Z}_2\text{,}\) define
\begin{equation} \notn{Thetab}{\Theta_b}(\notn{Agen}{A})=\notn{Agen}{A}^{\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^b}, \qquad \notn{Thetab}{\Theta_b}(\notnfar{Sgen}{S})=\notnfar{Sgen}{S}, \qquad \notn{Thetab}{\Theta_b}(\notn{Ygen}{Y})=YS^b,\tag{3.12} \end{equation}
Here \(\notn{Agen}{A}^{\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^b}=(\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^b)^{-1}AS^b\) is conjugation, not the product \(AS^b\text{.}\) This is an automorphism of the free pro-\(2\) group on \(\notn{Agen}{A},\notnfar{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}\text{,}\) with inverse \(\Theta_{-b}\text{.}\) Moreover
\begin{equation*} [\notnfar{Sgen}{S},YS^b]=[\notnfar{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}]^{\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^b}, \end{equation*}
and therefore
\begin{equation*} \notn{Thetab}{\Theta_b}(\notn{Agen}{A}^2\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^4[\notnfar{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}]) =(\notn{Agen}{A}^2)^{\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^b}\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^4[\notnfar{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}]^{\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^b} =(\notn{Agen}{A}^2\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^4[\notnfar{Sgen}{S},\notn{Ygen}{Y}])^{\notnfar{Sgen}{S}^b}. \end{equation*}
It therefore descends to an automorphism of \(\notn{D0}{D_0}\text{,}\) inducing \(\alpha_{1,b}\text{.}\) The composite \(\notn{Thetab}{\Theta_{b-b(u)}}\circ\notn{Psiu}{\Psi_u}\) induces \(\notn{alphaub}{\alpha_{u,b}}\text{,}\) proving surjectivity.
