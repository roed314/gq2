Proposition 8.8. Completed-square phase identity.

For either source, every lower exact-image map \(\rho\text{,}\) every \(b\in H^1_{\Gamma,\rho}(V)\text{,}\) and every \(\chi\in \mathcal X_T\text{,}\)
\begin{equation} Q_{\kappa,\Gamma,\rho}(b) +\langle\partial_{\Gamma,\rho} b+\rho^*e,\chi\rangle =Q^0_{\Gamma,\rho}(b+\rho^*a_{\chi,\kappa}) +\iota_\Gamma(\rho^*\Delta_{\chi,\kappa}).\tag{8.12} \end{equation}
Here \(Q^0\) is the base determinant form and \(Q_\kappa\) is the obstruction for the actual scalar pushout class (8.7).

Proof.
By Corollary 5.17,
\begin{equation*} \langle\partial_{\Gamma,\rho}b,\chi\rangle =\langle b,\rho^*\gamma_\chi\rangle_\Gamma. \end{equation*}
Using (6.7), the left side of (8.12) is
\begin{equation*} Q^0_{\Gamma,\rho}(b) +\langle b,\rho^*(\gamma_\kappa+\gamma_\chi)\rangle_\Gamma +\iota_\Gamma(\rho^*\delta_\kappa) +\iota_\Gamma(\rho^*(\chi_*e)). \end{equation*}
By the definition of \(a_{\chi,\kappa}\text{,}\) the middle linear term is the polar pairing \(b_q(b,\rho^*a_{\chi,\kappa})\text{.}\) Completing the square for the base quadratic form gives
\begin{equation*} Q^0(b)+b_q(b,\rho^*a_{\chi,\kappa}) =Q^0(b+\rho^*a_{\chi,\kappa}) +\iota_\Gamma\bigl(\rho^*\Theta_q^0(a_{\chi,\kappa})\bigr). \end{equation*}
Adding the two inflated scalar terms proves (8.12). The class \(\Delta_{\chi,\kappa}\) is defined on the finite target \(C\text{;}\) no comparison map between the two source \(H^1\)-spaces is involved.
