Proposition 3.10. Maximal pro-\(2\) quotient.

The maximal pro-\(2\) quotient of \(\notn{GA}{\GA}\) is
\begin{equation} \notn{Pi}{\Pi}= \left\langle \notn{sigma}{\sigma},\notn{x0}{x_0},\notnfar{x1}{x_1}\;\middle|\; \notn{x0}{x_0}^{\notn{sigma}{\sigma}^2}\notn{x0}{x_0}[\notnfar{x1}{x_1},\notn{sigma}{\sigma}]=1 \right\rangle_{\mathrm{pro-}2}.\tag{3.13} \end{equation}
The group \(\notn{Pi}{\Pi}\) is abstractly isomorphic to \(G_{\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}}(2)\text{.}\) Equipped with
\begin{equation} \notn{nu2}{\nu_2}:\notn{Pi}{\Pi}\twoheadrightarrow\mathbb{Z}_2, \qquad \notn{nu2}{\nu_2}(\notn{sigma}{\sigma})=1,\quad \notn{nu2}{\nu_2}(\notn{x0}{x_0})=\notn{nu2}{\nu_2}(\notnfar{x1}{x_1})=0,\tag{3.14} \end{equation}
it is isomorphic to the fully unramified marked pair \((G_{\notn{Qtwo}{\Qtwo}}(2),\notn{nuur}{\nu_{\mathrm{ur}}})\text{.}\)

Proof.
In a finite \(2\)-group quotient, Lemma 3.1 forces \(\notn{tau}{\tau}=1\text{,}\) and \(\notn{w}{\w}\) acts as the identity. Hence
\begin{equation*} u_i=x_i,\qquad d_0=1,\qquad c_0=1,\qquad g_0=\notn{sigma}{\sigma}^2,\qquad d_g=h_c=1, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} h_0=\notn{x0}{x_0}^{\notn{sigma}{\sigma}^2}\notn{x0}{x_0}. \end{equation*}
Moreover
\begin{equation*} u_1^{-1}\notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}=\notnfar{x1}{x_1}^{-1}\notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}=[\notnfar{x1}{x_1},\notn{sigma}{\sigma}]. \end{equation*}
Thus (1.6) becomes precisely the relator in (3.13). Conversely, every finite quotient of (3.13) is admissible after setting \(\notn{tau}{\tau}=1\text{.}\) The fully marked local identification is given by Corollary 3.12.
