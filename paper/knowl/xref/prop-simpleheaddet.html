Proposition 7.4. Simple-head determinant.

For every \(0\ne\lambda\in \mathcal X_R\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} q_\lambda|_{T}=0, \qquad b_\lambda(T,M)=0. \end{equation*}
Hence \(q_\lambda\) descends to a nonzero nonsingular \(C\)-invariant quadratic form
\begin{equation*} \ol q_\lambda:V\to\F_2. \end{equation*}

Proof.
We identify the polar radical, rule out a nonzero restriction to \(T\text{,}\) and then prove that the descended form on \(V\) is nonzero and nonsingular.
🔗
The adjoint \(b_\lambda^\flat:M\to M^\vee\) is \(C\)-linear. Since \(T\) has only trivial composition factors while \(\soc(M^\vee)\cong V^\vee\) is nontrivial simple, \(\Hom_C(T,M^\vee)=0\text{.}\) Thus \(b_\lambda(T,M)=0\text{.}\)
🔗
The restriction \(q_\lambda|_{T}\) is therefore a \(C\)-invariant linear functional. Suppose it is nonzero. Quotienting \(T\) by its kernel yields a \(C\)-module extension
\begin{equation*} 0\to\F_2t\to\ol M\to V\to0, \end{equation*}
with \(t\) in the polar radical and \(q_\lambda(t)=1\text{.}\) The kernel of \(C\to H_V\) acts on \(\ol M\) by possible shears
\begin{equation*} m\longmapsto m+f(\ol m)t. \end{equation*}
Quadratic invariance and \(q_\lambda(t)=1\) force every such \(f\) to vanish, so the extension factors through \(H_V\text{.}\) If \(V\) is ramified, its odd inertia subgroup has no fixed vectors and \(H^*(H_V,V^\vee)=0\) by Hochschild–Serre. If \(V\) is unramified, \(H_V\) is cyclic of odd order and the same vanishing follows from Maschke’s theorem. Hence
\begin{equation*} H^1(H_V,V^\vee)=0, \end{equation*}
so the extension splits. Projection to the scalar summand gives a nonzero \(C\)-map \(M\to\F_2\text{,}\) contradicting \((M^\vee)^C=0\text{.}\) Therefore \(q_\lambda|_{T}=0\text{.}\)
🔗
The descended form is nonzero. Otherwise \(K_\lambda\) would be elementary abelian, whereas
\begin{equation*} \Phi(K_\lambda)=R/\ker\lambda\ne1. \end{equation*}
Its polar radical is a submodule of the simple module \(V\text{;}\) if the radical were all of \(V\text{,}\) the form would be invariant linear, impossible. Thus it is nonsingular.
