Proposition 7.4. Simple-head determinant.

For every \(0\ne\lambda\in \notn{calX}{\mathcal X_R}\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \notn{qlambda}{q_\lambda}|_{T}=0, \qquad \notn{blambda}{b_\lambda}(\notn{Tsub}{T},\notnfar{Mmid}{M})=0. \end{equation*}
Hence \(\notn{qlambda}{q_\lambda}\) descends to a nonzero nonsingular \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-invariant quadratic form
\begin{equation*} \ol q_\lambda:\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to\F_2. \end{equation*}

Proof.
We identify the polar radical, rule out a nonzero restriction to \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\text{,}\) and then prove that the descended form on \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is nonzero and nonsingular.
🔗
The adjoint \(\notn{blambda}{b_\lambda}^\flat:\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\to \notnfar{Mmid}{M}^\vee\) is \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-linear. Since \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\) has only trivial composition factors while \(\soc(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}^\vee)\cong \notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee\) is nontrivial simple, \(\Hom_C(\notn{Tsub}{T},\notnfar{Mmid}{M}^\vee)=0\text{.}\) Thus \(\notn{blambda}{b_\lambda}(\notn{Tsub}{T},\notnfar{Mmid}{M})=0\text{.}\)
🔗
The restriction \(\notn{qlambda}{q_\lambda}|_{T}\) is therefore a \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-invariant linear functional. Suppose it is nonzero. Quotienting \(\notn{Tsub}{T}\) by its kernel yields a \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-module extension
\begin{equation*} 0\to\F_2t\to\ol M\to \notnfar{Vhead}{V}\to0, \end{equation*}
with \(t\) in the polar radical and \(\notn{qlambda}{q_\lambda}(t)=1\text{.}\) The kernel of \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\to H_V\) acts on \(\ol M\) by possible shears
\begin{equation*} m\longmapsto m+f(\ol m)t. \end{equation*}
Quadratic invariance and \(\notn{qlambda}{q_\lambda}(t)=1\) force every such \(f\) to vanish, so the extension factors through \(H_V\text{.}\) If \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is ramified, its odd inertia subgroup has no fixed vectors and \(H^*(H_V,\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee)=0\) by Hochschild–Serre. If \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\) is unramified, \(H_V\) is cyclic of odd order and the same vanishing follows from Maschke’s theorem. Hence
\begin{equation*} H^1(H_V,\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^\vee)=0, \end{equation*}
so the extension splits. Projection to the scalar summand gives a nonzero \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-map \(\notnfar{Mmid}{M}\to\F_2\text{,}\) contradicting \((\notnfar{Mmid}{M}^\vee)^\notn{Cfin}{C}=0\text{.}\) Therefore \(\notn{qlambda}{q_\lambda}|_{T}=0\text{.}\)
🔗
In more detail: the vanishing of \(q_\lambda\) on \(T\) is the one part of the argument that uses the boundary frame, rather than only the abstract minimal block. If \(q_\lambda(t)=1\text{,}\) conjugation on the extension \(0\to\F_2t\to\bar M\to V\to0\) could a priori contain shears by elements of \(V^\vee\text{.}\) Quadratic invariance kills the shears coming from the kernel of the action, so the extension is controlled by the faithful acting image \(H_V\text{.}\) It is important here to pass to \(H_V\text{,}\) not to make a normal-odd-inertia assertion about the original group \(C\text{.}\) The framed tame quotient makes \(H_V\) either cyclic of odd order or an extension with odd inertia, and in both cases \(H^1(H_V,V^\vee)=0\text{.}\) The resulting splitting yields an invariant functional on \(M\text{,}\) contradicting the simple-head construction.
🔗
The descended form is nonzero. Otherwise \(K_\lambda\) would be elementary abelian, whereas
\begin{equation*} \notn{Frattini}{\Phi}(K_\lambda)=\notn{Rrad}{R}/\ker\lambda\ne1. \end{equation*}
Its polar radical is a submodule of the simple module \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\text{;}\) if the radical were all of \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\text{,}\) the form would be invariant linear, impossible. Thus it is nonsingular.
