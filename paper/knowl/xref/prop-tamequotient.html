Proposition 3.2. Common tame quotient.

There are canonical isomorphisms
\begin{equation*} \notn{GA}{\GA}/\notn{WA}{\WA}\cong \notn{TA}{\TA}\cong \notn{GQ}{\GQ}/\notn{WF}{\WF}, \end{equation*}
where \(\notn{WF}{\WF}\) is wild inertia.

Proof.
In the quotient by \(\notn{WA}{\WA}\text{,}\) the elements \(\notn{x0}{x_0},\notnfar{x1}{x_1}\) vanish. By Lemma 3.1, \(\notn{tau}{\tau}\) has odd order in every finite quotient, so \(\notn{tau}{\tau}^\notn{w}{\w}=1\text{.}\) Hence \(u_i=d_0=z_0=c_0=d_g=h_c=h_0=1\text{;}\) the words \(\sigma_2\) and \(g_0\) may remain nontrivial, but they only conjugate the identity. The wild relation therefore disappears. For the local group, the tame quotient has pro-prime-to-\(2\) cyclic inertia and procyclic unramified quotient; geometric Frobenius acts on tame inertia by \(x\mapsto x^2\text{.}\) With \(x^g=g^{-1}xg\text{,}\) this is precisely the relation \(\notn{tau}{\tau}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}=\notn{tau}{\tau}^2\text{.}\)
