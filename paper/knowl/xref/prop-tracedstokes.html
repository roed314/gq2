Proposition 5.8. Traced finite-word Stokes identity.

Let \(\beta_{\mathrm t}\) and \(\beta_{\mathrm w}\) be the mixed central coordinates obtained from the tame and wild relators, and set
\begin{equation*} \mathcal B_{\rho,A}=\beta_{\mathrm t}+\beta_{\mathrm w}. \end{equation*}
Let \(L_{\mathrm t}\) and \(L_{\mathrm w}\) denote the two unnormalized first relation differentials. Then, before passage to cohomology,
\begin{align} \mathcal B_{\rho,A}(d^0_{\rho,A}a,y) &=\langle a, L_{\mathrm t}^{A^\vee}(y)+L_{\mathrm w}^{A^\vee}(y)\rangle,\tag{5.12}\\ \mathcal B_{\rho,A}(x,d^0_{\rho,A^\vee}\lambda) &=\langle L_{\mathrm t}^{A}(x)+L_{\mathrm w}^{A}(x),\lambda\rangle.\tag{5.13} \end{align}
The identities are natural in \(A\text{.}\) They are assertions about the sum of the two relator coordinates; in general they are false for either relator separately.

Proof.
We compute the two relators’ mod-\(2\) exponent vectors, apply the finite-word Stokes formula to their sum, and then invoke finite-exponent independence and coefficient naturality.
🔗
Choose an odd ordinary integer representative of \(\notn{w}{\w}\) which is valid in every finite semidirect-Heisenberg group in the calculation, and replace the two profinite relators by the resulting ordinary words \(r_{\mathrm t}\) and \(r_{\mathrm w}\text{.}\) In the ordered generator list \((\notn{sigma}{\sigma},\notn{tau}{\tau},\notn{x0}{x_0},\notnfar{x1}{x_1})\) their mod-\(2\) exponent vectors are
\begin{equation*} \epsilon(r_{\mathrm t})=(0,1,0,0), \qquad \epsilon(r_{\mathrm w})=(0,1,0,0). \end{equation*}
Indeed, \(r_{\mathrm t}=\notn{tau}{\tau}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}\notn{tau}{\tau}^{-2}\) has odd total \(\notn{tau}{\tau}\)-exponent. For \(r_{\mathrm w}=h_0u_1^{-1}\notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma} c_0\text{,}\) the commutator \(c_0\) has zero exponent vector. The word \(h_0\) also has zero exponent vector: the two \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\)-terms cancel modulo \(2\text{,}\) the two occurrences of \(d_0\) cancel, \(d_0^2\) has even exponent vector, and \(h_c\) is a commutator. Finally, \(u_1^{-1}\) contributes \(\notnfar{x1}{x_1}+\notn{tau}{\tau}\) and \(\notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}\) contributes \(\notnfar{x1}{x_1}\text{.}\) Thus the sum of the two exponent vectors is zero.
🔗
Apply Lemma 5.7 with trace coefficients \((1,1)\text{.}\) The correction terms in (5.9) cancel and give (5.12); the correction terms in (5.10) cancel and give (5.13). Independence of the chosen integer representative is Lemma 5.1, and strict coefficient naturality is Lemma 5.6.
