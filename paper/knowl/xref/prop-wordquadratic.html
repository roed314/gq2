Proposition 6.5. Complete base second-order word expansion.

With \(\notn{opS}{\mathsf S},\notn{opT}{\mathsf T},\notn{Pnorm}{P},\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}=\notn{opS}{\mathsf S}^\notn{w}{\w}\) as above, evaluation of the actual relator (1.6) for the base class \(\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}\) gives
\begin{equation} Q_A^0(c)=q(c)+\notnfar{bq}{b_q}((\notn{Pnorm}{P}+1)c,\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}c).\tag{6.24} \end{equation}
Equivalently,
\begin{equation} Q_A^0(c)= \begin{cases} q(c),&\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1,\\ q(c)+\notnfar{bq}{b_q}(c,\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}c),&\notnfar{Vhead}{V}^{\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}}=0. \end{cases}\tag{6.25} \end{equation}
Its ramified polar form is
\begin{equation} b_A(c,c')=\notnfar{bq}{b_q}(c,(1+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}+\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1})c').\tag{6.26} \end{equation}
For a general determinant class \(\kappa=\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}+\Gamma_{\gamma_\kappa}+\operatorname{inf}\delta_\kappa\text{,}\) one has
\begin{equation} Q_{A,\kappa,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(c)=Q_A^0(c) +\langle c,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*\gamma_\kappa\rangle_A +\iota_A(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*\delta_\kappa).\tag{6.27} \end{equation}

Proof.
We evaluate the square contribution of \(h_0\text{,}\) the commutator contribution of \(d_0\) and \(z_0\text{,}\) and then polarize the resulting quadratic expression.
🔗
Normalize a cohomology representative so that only \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\) varies, by \(c\text{.}\) In terms of (6.8), the complete base contribution ledger is
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c|c} \text{factor}&\text{$\notnfar{Vhead}{V}$-coordinate}&\text{central coordinate}\\ \hline h_0&0&q(c)\\ u_1^{-1}&0&0\\ \notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}&0&0\\ d_0=(\notn{x0}{x_0}\notn{tau}{\tau})^\notn{w}{\w} \notn{x0}{x_0}^{-1}&(\notn{Pnorm}{P}+1)c&\ast\\ z_0=\notn{x0}{x_0}^{\notn{sigma}{\sigma}^\notn{w}{\w}}&\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}c&\ast\\ {[d_0,z_0]}&0&\notnfar{bq}{b_q}((\notn{Pnorm}{P}+1)c,\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}c)\\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
The starred entries contain ordinary \(f\)-terms and the \(m\)-terms of the lifted \(\notn{Cfin}{C}\)-action. They occur only as central coordinates of \(d_0\) and \(z_0\text{,}\) and central coordinates do not affect the commutator. Since the lower values of \(d_0\) and \(z_0\) at the tame map are both the identity, the commutator contribution is exactly the polar form of their \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\)-coordinates, \(\notnfar{bq}{b_q}((\notn{Pnorm}{P}+1)c,\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}^{-1}c)\text{.}\)
🔗
The first line is the extraspecial case of Lemma 5.3, but we recall why it remains valid with the \(m\)-terms present. The class-two identity Lemma 5.2 uses only that the lift of the relevant tame operator is an automorphism of the central extension and fixes the centre; it does not assume that the chosen section is equivariant. If \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=0\text{,}\) then \(d_0\) and \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\) have the same image in \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\text{,}\) so the identity gives the square of \(\notn{x0}{x_0}\text{,}\) namely \(q(c)\text{.}\) If \(\notn{Pnorm}{P}=1\text{,}\) then \(\notn{opT}{\mathsf T}=1\) and the lift of \(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}\) is a central automorphism of order at most \(2\text{,}\) so \(g_0=\sigma_2^2\) acts trivially on the class-two coefficient group and the same identity applies.
🔗
The factors \(u_1^{-1}\) and \(\notnfar{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}\) have zero \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\)-coordinate on the normalized representative, and hence no quadratic central contribution. Multiplying the table gives (6.24). Polarizing and using the \(\notnfar{opU}{\mathsf U}\)-invariance of \(\notnfar{bq}{b_q}\) gives (6.26); the operator is invertible by the same polynomial argument used in Proposition 5.15. Finally, (6.27) is exactly (6.7) on the candidate source.
