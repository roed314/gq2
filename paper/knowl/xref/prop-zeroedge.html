Proposition 8.14. The zero radical-edge case.

Assume that the radical edge vanishes. Write the descended scalar pushout class as
\begin{equation*} \kappa=\kappa_q^0+\Gamma_{\gamma_\kappa} +\operatorname{inf}\delta_\kappa \quad\text{on }V\rtimes C, \end{equation*}
and put
\begin{equation*} \mathcal X_T=(T^\vee)^C, \qquad r=\dim\mathcal X_T, \qquad d=\dim V, \qquad \mu=|B^1_{\Gamma,\rho}(V)|\,|Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}(T)|. \end{equation*}
For each lower map \(\rho\text{,}\)
\begin{align} &\#\{c:\partial_{\Gamma,\rho}c=\rho^*e, \ Q_{\kappa,\Gamma,\rho}(c)=0\}\notag\\ &\qquad=\frac1{2^{r+1}} \left(2^d+G(Q^0)\sum_{\chi\in\mathcal X_T} (-1)^{\iota_\Gamma(\rho^*\Delta_{\chi,\kappa})}\right).\tag{8.19} \end{align}
Consequently
\begin{equation} \boxed{ Z_{\Gamma,\lambda}(B/C) =\frac{\mu}{2^{r+1}} \left(2^d \notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}(C) +G(Q^0)\sum_{\chi\in\mathcal X_T} s_\Gamma(\Delta_{\chi,\kappa})\right),}\tag{8.20} \end{equation}
where
\begin{equation*} s_\Gamma(\zeta)= \sum_{\rho\in X_\Gamma(C)} (-1)^{\iota_\Gamma(\rho^*\zeta)}. \end{equation*}

Proof.
The vanishing edge and Lemma 6.21 identify \(B/T\) with \(V\rtimes C\text{.}\) By Lemma 8.7, the admissible \(V\)-coordinates satisfy \(\partial c=\rho^*e\) and each occurs with multiplicity \(\mu\text{.}\) The map \(\partial_{\Gamma,\rho}\) is surjective because \(H^2_{\Gamma,\rho}(M)=0\text{.}\) Apply the constrained Gauss formula (8.3) and the completed-square identity (8.12); the common Gauss sum is supplied by Proposition 6.9, Proposition 6.18. This gives (8.19). Multiplication by \(\mu\) and summation over \(X_\Gamma(C)\) give (8.20).
