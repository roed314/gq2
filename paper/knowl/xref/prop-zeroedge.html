Proposition 8.14. The zero radical-edge case.

Assume that the radical edge vanishes. Write the descended scalar pushout class as
\begin{equation*} \kappa=\notnfar{kappaq0}{\kappa_q^0}+\Gamma_{\gamma_\kappa} +\operatorname{inf}\delta_\kappa \quad\text{on }\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\rtimes \notnfar{Cfin}{C}, \end{equation*}
and put
\begin{equation*} \notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_T}=(\notn{Tsub}{T}^\vee)^\notnfar{Cfin}{C}, \qquad r=\dim\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_T}, \qquad d=\dim \notnfar{Vhead}{V}, \qquad \mu=|B^1_{\Gamma,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(\notnfar{Vhead}{V})|\,|\notn{Z1Gr}{Z^1_{\Gamma,\rho}}(\notn{Tsub}{T})|. \end{equation*}
For each lower map \(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}\text{,}\)
\begin{align} &\#\{c:\partial_{\Gamma,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}c=\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*e, \ Q_{\kappa,\notnfar{GammaSrc}{\Gamma},\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}(c)=0\}\notag\\ &\qquad=\frac1{2^{r+1}} \left(2^d+G(Q^0)\sum_{\chi\in\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_T}} (-1)^{\notn{iotaGamma}{\iota_\Gamma}(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*\notn{DeltaChiKappa}{\Delta_{\chi,\kappa}})}\right).\tag{8.19} \end{align}
Consequently
\begin{equation} \boxed{ \notn{ZGl}{Z_{\Gamma,\lambda}}(B/\notnfar{Cfin}{C}) =\frac{\mu}{2^{r+1}} \left(2^d \notnfar{eGamma}{e_\Gamma}(\notnfar{Cfin}{C}) +G(Q^0)\sum_{\chi\in\notnfar{calX}{\mathcal X_T}} s_\Gamma(\notn{DeltaChiKappa}{\Delta_{\chi,\kappa}})\right),}\tag{8.20} \end{equation}
where
\begin{equation*} s_\Gamma(\zeta)= \sum_{\rho\in \notnfar{XGamma}{X_\Gamma}(\notnfar{Cfin}{C})} (-1)^{\notn{iotaGamma}{\iota_\Gamma}(\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*\zeta)}. \end{equation*}

Proof.
The vanishing edge and Lemma 6.21 identify \(\notnfar{Bmid}{B}/\notn{Tsub}{T}\) with \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\rtimes \notnfar{Cfin}{C}\text{.}\) By Lemma 8.7, the admissible \(\notnfar{Vhead}{V}\)-coordinates satisfy \(\partial c=\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}^*e\) and each occurs with multiplicity \(\mu\text{.}\) The map \(\partial_{\Gamma,\notnfar{rhoEpi}{\rho}}\) is surjective because \(\notn{HGr}{H^2_{\Gamma,\rho}}(\notnfar{Mmid}{M})=0\text{.}\) Apply the constrained Gauss formula (8.3) and the completed-square identity (8.12); the common Gauss sum is supplied by Proposition 6.9, Proposition 6.18. This gives (8.19). Multiplication by \(\mu\) and summation over \(\notnfar{XGamma}{X_\Gamma}(\notnfar{Cfin}{C})\) give (8.20).
