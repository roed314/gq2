Theorem 1.2. Presentation theorem.

The absolute Galois group \(\notn{GQ}{\GQ}\) is isomorphic to the profinite group topologically generated by
\begin{equation*} \notn{sigma}{\sigma},\notn{tau}{\tau},\notn{x0}{x_0},\notn{x1}{x_1}, \end{equation*}
for which the closed normal subgroup generated by \(\notn{x0}{x_0},\notn{x1}{x_1}\) is pro-\(2\text{,}\) and which is subject to the two relations
\begin{equation} \notn{tau}{\tau}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma}=\notn{tau}{\tau}^2\tag{1.5} \end{equation}
and
\begin{equation} h_0u_1^{-1}\notn{x1}{x_1}^\notn{sigma}{\sigma} c_0=1,\tag{1.6} \end{equation}
where the auxiliary words are defined in (1.1)–(1.3).
